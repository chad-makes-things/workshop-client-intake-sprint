User Journey

The Content Concierge in Action

Seven scenes across a single week — from a morning SMS brief to published content on six channels, all without a marketing team.

7:30 AM — The Daily Brief Arrives

Tuesday Morning · Innovative Dental Springfield · Jessica Martinez, Content Champion

Jessica's phone buzzes. "Good morning Jessica! 3 captures today: 1) Before photo — Full Arch Implant at 9:30 AM (💡 Front + 45° angle, fill frame with smile). 2) Testimonial — Smile Makeover finishing today (💡 Ask at checkout). 3) Team photo — no appointment needed."

The brief was generated overnight by the Prompt Engine — it pulled tomorrow's appointments from Sikka ONE API, matched procedure codes to the playbook, checked consent records, and composed the guidance. Jessica didn't have to think about any of it.

Key behavior: The guidance is specific ("front + 45-degree angle, fill frame with smile") not generic ("take a good photo"). Specificity eliminates the most common failure mode.

9:45 AM — The Before Photo

Operatory 3 · Patient: Michael K. · Procedure: Full Arch Implant Consultation

Jessica takes two photos on her phone — front and 45° as instructed — and texts them to the Content Concierge number. 18 seconds later: "Got it! ✅ 2 photos received. Our team will review and publish within 24 hours. This week: 4-day streak 🔥"

In the background: photos downloaded from Twilio, uploaded to S3, metadata tagged (practice, champion, procedure, consent ID), Airtable record created for editorial review.

Key metric: Under 60 seconds total — brief received, photo taken, submitted. Zero login, zero app, zero friction. SMS reply is the right interface for a busy front-desk environment.

4:30 PM — After Photo + Testimonial

Checkout Desk · Patient: Sarah M. · Procedure: Smile Makeover Complete

Sarah is beaming at her result. Jessica follows the playbook guidance: "Would you be willing to share your experience on video? Just 30 seconds." Sarah says yes. Jessica records 45 seconds on her phone and sends via WhatsApp (better quality than MMS for video).

Plus the after photo — which triggers the system to automatically pair it with Michael's morning before photo for a side-by-side comparison asset.

Key behavior: The post-op follow-up scheduler will also fire at 30 and 90 days to capture a longer smile reveal testimonial from Sarah — built-in without any manual scheduling.

Wednesday 9 AM — Editorial Review

HQ Content Team · Airtable Review Queue

Two editors open Airtable and work through the network's 47 captures from yesterday. Michael's before photos are flagged for an upcoming before/after pair. Sarah's testimonial video is pulled from S3 presigned URL and reviewed. Caption drafted: "Sarah came in for her dream smile — and left with it. ✨ Full smile makeover at Innovative Dental Springfield."

Status → Approved. Slack notification fires: "✅ Approved: Testimonial Video from Innovative Dental Springfield."

Phase 1 reality: Human editors handle production and post manually to Sprout Social/Later. Phase 2 replaces this with AI caption generation and automated distribution — same result, no editorial team.

Phase 2: AI Pipeline Takes Over

Months 2–4 · Same captures, no human editors needed for formatting

Sarah's 45-second testimonial enters the AI pipeline: Whisper transcribes the audio, AI generates platform-specific captions (Instagram: short + emoji, LinkedIn: narrative, YouTube: SEO-optimized description), OpusClip detects the strongest 15-second moment, Cloudinary generates 6 format variants (IG Reel vertical, IG feed square, FB landscape, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Google Business).

One 45-second video becomes 10 distinct content pieces, each formatted for its platform, with captions written and hashtags tagged. 90 minutes from upload to publish-ready.

Key metric: 1 capture → 10 published pieces. At 5 captures/week per practice, that's 50 content pieces/week — equivalent to a full content team's output, without the team.

Auto-Distribution Across 6 Channels

Wednesday 1 PM, 3 PM · Scheduled per Playbook Rules

Sarah's 15-second reel goes to Instagram at the AI-determined optimal time for Innovative Dental Springfield (Tuesday–Thursday, 11 AM–1 PM based on historical engagement). The before/after side-by-side posts to Facebook 2 hours later. The full video goes to Google Business Profile. The before/after automatically updates the practice's Digital Storefront implant gallery via Practice Concierge API.

Michael's before/after (with the after photo from a future appointment) gets queued for the Digital Storefront gallery and tagged `paid_media_eligible` for the BD team's ad campaigns.

Network effect: Every approved capture from every practice flows into a growing tagged asset library available to the paid media team, the BD team for outreach, and the brand content library.

Monday — Jessica's Weekly Recap

Monday 8 AM SMS · Jessica Martinez

"Jessica's week in review 📊 Last week: 5 captures (target: 5) ✅ Published: 18 content pieces. Top piece: Sarah's testimonial reel got 847 views. Streak: 12 days 🔥 This week's focus: before/after pairs for Dr. Patel's implant cases. First prompt tomorrow at 7:30 AM!"

Jessica didn't log into a dashboard. She didn't check analytics. She got the number that matters — her streak — and the social proof that her captures are producing real results. That's the feedback loop that sustains the behavior.

Behavior design: The streak counter is the most important metric in Phase 1. It creates a daily habit without requiring any intrinsic motivation. The weekly recap proves impact without asking champions to measure anything themselves.
The AI Pipeline

From Raw Capture to 20 Published Pieces

Phase 2 introduces an AI production pipeline that takes every raw capture and generates publish-ready content for every target platform automatically.

📥 Ingest

Validate, tag, HIPAA screen, and route every incoming capture to the right processing path.
  • Metadata validation (practice, champion, consent ID)
  • HIPAA screen via AWS Rekognition (detect visible PHI)
  • Content type classification (before/after, testimonial, topic video)
  • Before/after pairing logic (match today's after to prior before)

🎨 Enhance (Photos)

Dental-specific photo processing: color correction, smart crop centered on smile area, brand overlay.
  • Auto color correction + exposure adjustment via Cloudinary
  • Smile-area smart crop (mouth landmarks via AWS Rekognition)
  • Before/after side-by-side generation (4 format variants)
  • Practice logo + brand color overlay application

🎬 Enhance (Video)

Video stabilization, auto-captioning, and AI clip extraction for short-form platforms.
  • Video stabilization + color grade via FFmpeg
  • AI transcription via OpenAI Whisper
  • Burned-in captions (bold, high-contrast for mobile)
  • Highlight moment detection + clip extraction via OpusClip API

✍️ Generate

AI-written captions, hashtags, descriptions, and alt text per platform — with dental context.
  • Platform-specific captions (Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, Facebook, TikTok, GBP)
  • Hashtag sets (practice-branded + procedure-specific + trend)
  • Alt text for accessibility compliance
  • Blog excerpt draft for Doctor Topic videos

🔍 Review

Human quality gate in Phase 1 (Airtable); AI auto-approval for high-confidence content types in Phase 3.
  • HQ review queue with batch approve/reject
  • Caption editing before approval
  • Rejection feedback → SMS to capturing champion
  • AI confidence score; types with >95% approval rate auto-approved

📡 Distribute

Approved content routed to all target platforms automatically at playbook-defined optimal times.
  • Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok, Google Business Profile
  • Practice Concierge Digital Storefront (gallery auto-update)
  • Tagged library for paid media + BD sales enablement
  • Engagement data feedback into next-cycle scheduling optimization
Architecture Decisions

Key Technical Decisions

The architectural choices that define the Content Concierge — and why they were made this way.

SMS First, App Second — Why Phase 1 is Intentionally Low-Tech

▼
Decision: Phase 1 uses SMS/WhatsApp for capture interface — no mobile app, no login, no UI.

Why: The core failure mode is behavior, not technology. Champions don't capture content because it requires effort. SMS removes all friction: receive the brief, take a photo, reply. Under 60 seconds. No app to remember to open, no login, no form. The behavior question must be answered before investing in a native app.

Alternatives considered: Build the app first (Phase 2), which is the "obvious" move. Rejected because it delays the behavior test by 3+ months. If champions don't respond to SMS prompts, they won't open an app either. SMS proves the behavior at minimal cost.

Phase 2 transition: Once Phase 1 demonstrates 50%+ participation, the app is justified. The app adds guided camera overlays, offline capture, and the scorecard — all of which build on an established behavior, not create it.

Sikka ONE API — Why One PMS Integration Covers 90% of the Network

▼
Decision: Integrate with Sikka ONE API as the primary PMS data source.

Why: Sikka ONE aggregates 400+ dental practice management systems (Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental, Curve, Carestream, and hundreds more) into a single API. One integration covers ~90% of SGA's practice PMS landscape without building 5+ individual connectors.

Alternative: Build direct connectors to each PMS. Dentrix requires a Dentsply partner agreement (4–6 weeks), Eaglesoft requires Patterson agreement, Open Dental is open-source (fastest). Total: 6+ months of connector work. Sikka ONE: 1 integration.

Trade-off: Sikka ONE charges per-connection fees. At 260+ practices, this cost is justified by eliminating 6 months of engineering time. Sikka also handles API version changes and new PMS releases — zero maintenance overhead.

Airtable for Phase 1 Review — Intentionally Not a Dashboard

▼
Decision: Use Airtable Enterprise as the editorial review queue for Phase 1. Build the Next.js HQ dashboard in Phase 2.

Why: Phase 1 has 2 human editors reviewing 30–50 captures/day across 10–15 practices. Airtable's Kanban view, built-in automations (Slack notification, webhook triggers), and filtering cover this use case without 4–6 weeks of dashboard development.

Explicit trade-off: Airtable is Phase 1 ONLY. Editors are informed of this. The investment in Airtable configuration is intentionally minimal — no complex views or automations that will be thrown away. The $240/month Enterprise cost (required for HIPAA BAA) is trivially justified by the time saved.

Phase 2: Next.js HQ dashboard replaces Airtable entirely. Review queue, batch approval, AI caption editing, network pipeline view, and analytics — all in-house.

Cloudinary + OpusClip vs. Custom Processing Pipeline

▼
Decision: Use Cloudinary for photo processing and OpusClip API for video highlight detection in Phase 2. Custom models in Phase 4 only.

Why (Cloudinary): Cloudinary's transformation pipeline handles multi-format resizing, brand overlay application, and color correction without a custom Lambda. AWS Rekognition provides smile-area landmark coordinates that we pass to Cloudinary's crop parameters. No custom photo processing code to maintain.

Why (OpusClip): Building a custom highlight detection model requires labeled dental testimonial video data we don't yet have. At ~30 videos/day Phase 2 volume, OpusClip costs $90–180/month — trivial. Phase 4 builds the custom model when we have 10,000+ labeled dental videos and can outperform OpusClip on dental content specifically.

Trade-off: Vendor dependency risk. Mitigated by modular architecture — replacing either service requires changing one service, not rewriting the pipeline.

Per-Practice Meta Business Accounts — The Hardest Distribution Problem

▼
Decision: Each practice requires its own Meta Business Account + Instagram/Facebook authorization of the SGA Content Concierge app to enable distribution.

The problem: Meta's Graph API requires explicit OAuth authorization per practice page. 260 practices × 2 platforms = 520 authorization flows. This is an onboarding problem as much as an engineering problem.

Solution: Build a "Connect your Instagram" onboarding flow in the Phase 2 mobile app. During practice setup, the app walks the practice through Meta authorization in under 5 minutes. Tokens stored encrypted in RDS, refreshed automatically every 55 days.

Priority: Register the SGA Content Concierge Meta Developer App and submit for advanced access (Pages API, Instagram Content Publishing API) immediately — approval takes 1–4 weeks and must happen before Phase 2 distribution goes live.

Tech Stack

Phase 1 Technology

Phase 1 is intentionally lean — SMS over infrastructure, Airtable over custom dashboards, human editors over AI pipelines. The stack grows with validation.

📱

Twilio

SMS + WhatsApp Business API for capture interface. HIPAA BAA available. Inbound webhook receives MMS media for S3 upload.

🦷

Sikka ONE API

400+ dental PMS systems via single integration. Nightly appointment sync drives the Prompt Engine. Eliminates 6+ individual PMS connectors.

🗄️

PostgreSQL (AWS RDS)

Practices, champions, assignments, captures, consents. Relational integrity for HIPAA consent chain. AES-256 encryption at rest.

☁️

AWS S3

Raw and processed media storage. 7-year HIPAA retention, SSE-KMS encryption, presigned URLs (no public media). CloudFront CDN for Phase 2.

⚙️

Node.js on AWS ECS

Scheduler Service (nightly sync, daily brief), Capture Service (Twilio webhooks), Admin Service (practice config CRUD). TypeScript monorepo via Turborepo.

🎯

Next.js 14 + Vercel

Internal admin dashboard. Practice configuration, champion management, capture monitoring. Google OAuth via NextAuth.js. Server Components for fast load.

📋

Airtable Enterprise

Phase 1 editorial review queue. Kanban workflow, built-in Slack automation, webhook to champion rejection SMS. HIPAA BAA on Enterprise tier.

🤖

Claude API + Whisper (Phase 2)

Caption, hashtag, and description generation per platform. Whisper transcribes video for auto-captioning and blog excerpts. Dental context prompt tuned per playbook.

🎬

Cloudinary + OpusClip (Phase 2)

Cloudinary: multi-format photo processing, brand overlay, smile-area smart crop. OpusClip: video highlight detection, 15s/30s/60s clip generation from testimonials.

📡

Platform APIs (Phase 2)

Meta Graph API (Instagram + Facebook), YouTube Data API v3, LinkedIn Marketing API, TikTok Content Posting API, Google Business Profile API.
