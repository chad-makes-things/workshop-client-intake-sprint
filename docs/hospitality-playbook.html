The Playbook

Caring with Charisma — Five Pillars of Hospitality in Dentistry

A dental visit can be one of the most anxious experiences in a person's life — or one of the most affirming. The difference is not clinical skill. It is the quality of every human interaction before, during, and after the chair. "Caring with Charisma" is SGA's system for designing that difference at scale — specific, observable, teachable behaviors.

Key Insights

01
The Morning Huddle is Underutilized Infrastructure
Every SGA practice already has a morning huddle. Dental Intel already provides a platform. The problem was not the ritual — it was the absence of content worth running. The highest-leverage intervention is a content strategy and production commitment, not new technology.
02
Culture and Measurement Require Each Other
Culture without measurement becomes untestable faith. Measurement without culture becomes hollow surveillance. The H-Score works because it is grounded in real hospitality behaviors — phone greetings, trust transfer language, review velocity. Culture gives the score meaning; the score makes culture sustainable.
03
Career Value Is the Difference Between Compliance and Culture
In high-turnover service roles, training programs perceived as employer-centric generate compliance. Programs perceived as career-valuable generate genuine engagement. The jacket, LinkedIn badge, and resume credential are not perks — they are the entire motivation architecture.
04
Champions Are the Durability Layer the Platform Needs
Every platform eventually stops being new. The Champions — people who have made the culture part of their professional identity — are the human infrastructure that sustains engagement after novelty fades. The platform supports them; it does not replace them.

The Daily Ritual

Five Minutes That Change Everything

Every morning, before the first patient arrives, the entire practice team gathers. The platform delivers the experience — no preparation required. The morning huddle is the delivery mechanism for the entire playbook. Run it, and everything else follows.

0:00

Culture Moment

Grant or a Champion tells a hospitality story — 60 seconds, direct to camera. This week's theme. The mythology precedes the mechanics: why this matters before how to do it.

1:00

Yesterday's Wins

Live Swell data: reviews received in the last 24 hours, staff mentions highlighted, H-Score trend sparkline. A real patient, a real reaction, connected to yesterday's behaviors.

2:00

Today's Focus — Team Practice

This week's hospitality behavior. 30-second team role-play exercise with the exact scripted phrase. Practice before the first patient. No improvisation required.

3:30

Accountability Moment

Trust Transfer Commitment tap. Certification spotlight (anyone who earned a badge since last huddle). Environmental checklist: 3 items, 30 seconds.

4:30

The Intention

"Today, every guest leaves knowing their name matters here."

Read by the Champion. Repeated by the team. The same structure, every day. The ritual IS the culture delivery system.

Architecture Decisions

Five Decisions That Define This Platform

Each ADR captures a significant architectural choice — what was decided, why, and what it means for every future design decision.

ADR-01The Morning Huddle is the primary delivery container — not a new app or LMS
▼
All training content, certification prompts, H-Score displays, and cultural rituals are delivered through the morning huddle inside Dental Intel.
The morning huddle is the only moment in the day when the entire practice team is together and not yet in patient-facing mode. It is a captive attention window that already exists. Building a new app creates an adoption problem: something new to download, log into, remember, and use. Extending the morning huddle — which already happens imperfectly — eliminates the adoption barrier entirely.
ConsequenceEvery platform decision must ask "can this surface in the morning huddle?" before creating a separate interface. The huddle is the UX.
ADR-02The H-Score algorithm is fully transparent and documented — no black box
▼
Every component, its weight, and its calculation method is published and readable by any practice owner in plain language.
In healthcare and high-trust service environments, opaque scoring systems create resentment and distrust. If staff believe the score measures things outside their control, they will game it or ignore it. Transparency about what the score measures and how it is calculated is the only path to the score being trusted as fair — which is the precondition for it being motivating.
ConsequenceAll H-Score documentation is written for a practice owner audience — plain language, not technical documentation. The API exposes the algorithm at a public endpoint.
ADR-03H-Score benchmarking is segmented by practice type — rural is never compared to urban
▼
Five practice-type cohorts: rural, suburban, urban, cosmetic, pediatric. All benchmarking, leaderboard rankings, and intervention thresholds are calculated within cohort.
A rural practice with 12 patients per day generates fundamentally different data volumes than an urban practice with 80. The same hospitality behaviors produce fewer reviews and fewer phone analytics data points. Comparing them on raw metrics is unfair and demoralizing — rural practices would systematically bottom-rank regardless of actual hospitality quality.
ConsequencePractice type is a required field in all scoring calculations. A "Most Improved" leaderboard (percent-change, not absolute score) allows any practice type to compete for network recognition.
ADR-04The CCA credential is portable and externally verifiable from day one
▼
Every certificate includes a unique verification URL. LinkedIn integration via Credly is built into the Bronze issuance flow at launch. All curriculum is structured to academic standards.
The difference between a credential and a training completion record is portability. If the credential only lives in SGA's HR system, it has no career value to staff — and career value is the entire motivation architecture for CCA engagement. Building the external credentialing infrastructure at launch (not as a future upgrade) makes the credential genuinely valuable from the first day it is issued.
ConsequenceAll CCA content must meet the documentation standards required for academic submission. This is a positive constraint that creates higher content quality and advances the dental school adoption pathway.
ADR-05The playbook is a living, versioned document — never printed as final
▼
The "Caring with Charisma" playbook is versioned like software. Major versions are significant releases. Minor versions are incremental. Every practice knows which version their training is built on.
The biggest failure mode for culture programs is the "binder on the shelf" problem — written once, out of date, sitting unread. By designing the playbook as a living document with a clear versioning and distribution system, SGA can evolve the culture standard as the network grows, as Champions contribute insights, and as academic research generates new best practices.
ConsequenceSGA needs a "Playbook Editor" role. Staff who completed Bronze under v1.0 receive a "What's Changed" micro-module when v2.0 launches — not a full recertification. Champion contributions feed the next version cycle.
