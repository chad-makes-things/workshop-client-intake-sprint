Key Concerns for CIO Review
Four areas requiring executive attention before platform build begins
Concern 1 — High Risk
Call Recordings Through External AI
Audio call recordings from Peer Logic, RingCentral, 8x8, and Weave are sent to OpenAI Whisper for transcription. These recordings contain verbal PHI disclosures (patient names, treatment discussions, insurance details). Unlike text-based PHI which passes through the PHI filter, audio cannot be pre-filtered — the raw audio stream reaches the external API. A BAA with OpenAI is required and must cover audio data specifically. Transcriptions then flow to Claude for analysis, passing through the PHI filter, but the initial Whisper call is unfiltered.
Concern 2 — High Risk
Four Vendors Without BAAs
Cloudinary processes before/after dental photos (patient facial images = PHI). De-identification is architected but relies on metadata stripping — faces remain identifiable. Swell aggregates patient reviews that may contain health information. Cinnamon processes call analytics data. Dental Intel (95% adoption) handles practice analytics derived from PMS data. All four need BAA verification or architectural mitigation before PHI flows through them.
Concern 3 — Medium Risk
PHI Surface Area Breadth
The platform touches PHI across 6 source categories, processes it through 8 internal services, and outputs to 7 external destinations. Each touchpoint is a potential breach vector. At 260 practices generating ~13,000 messages/day, ~65,000 call-minutes/day, and continuous PMS sync events, the volume of PHI in motion is substantial. The Compliance & Audit Layer is designed as cross-cutting middleware, but its effectiveness depends on consistent implementation across all 8 processing services with zero gaps.
Concern 4 — Medium Risk
PHI Filter as Single Point of Trust
The AI Service's PHI filter is the sole mechanism preventing PHI from reaching external LLM APIs (Anthropic Claude). It uses pattern matching (regex + NER) to detect and substitute PHI with placeholders. This is a single point of failure: if the filter misses a PHI token (novel format, encoding edge case, multi-language input), that PHI reaches the external API. The architecture recommends evaluating OursPrivacy/Freshpaint as purpose-built PHI detection, but this is not yet implemented. Defense-in-depth is needed.
