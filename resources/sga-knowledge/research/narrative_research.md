## 1. Origin Story Elements

### The Thurston Group's Dental Thesis

**Story Core:** A 37-year-old private equity firm saw what was broken in dental consolidation and decided to build something different -- not once, but three times, each iteration closer to the vision.

The Thurston Group is not a typical private equity investor dabbling in healthcare. They are serial dental builders. Before SGA existed as a combined entity, Thurston had already launched Smile Doctors (orthodontics), US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management, and South Georgia Dental Management. Over 37 years, they returned over $4 billion to partners.

But the most narratively interesting detail is not the track record -- it is the *progression*. Each platform taught them something. South Georgia Dental Management taught them community dentistry operations. Then they built Gen4 to fix what traditional DSOs got wrong about doctor autonomy. Then they built MODIS to attack the specialty/education gap. And now they are merging all three into SGA because they finally have all three pieces: community reach, collaborative culture, and clinical innovation.

**Key Quote (Patrick J. Haynes III, Thurston Founder):** "Thurston Group is committed to staying at the forefront of innovation in the dental industry, and the launch of MODIS is a testament to that. We believe that our partnership with the Pikos Institute will set a new standard in implantology education and technology."

**Narrative Angle:** The "three attempts" structure. Thurston did not get it right the first time. They built, learned, rebuilt. SGA is the synthesis -- the platform that combines what took 37 years to understand.

### Gen4's Founding Story (August 2021)

**Story Core:** A Marine Corps officer turned McKinsey consultant and a practicing dentist who had seen both sides of the DSO coin decided the entire model was broken -- and named their company after the fix.

Gen4 is not just a name. It is a thesis. The founders looked at the history of dental consolidation and saw four generations:

- **Gen1:** Back-office accounting machines. Efficient but soulless.
- **Gen2:** The cookie-cutter standardizers. "Same supplies in the same place in every office." Efficient but dehumanizing.
- **Gen3:** Buying groups and fragmented consortiums. Better intentions but no scale.
- **Gen4:** The collaborative model. Clinician-centric, patient-obsessed, built around peer learning.

They were so convinced this was a generational shift that they put it in the company name.

**Key Quote (Lamonte Jensen, CEO):** "We are extremely excited about the launching of Gen4 Dental Partners. Together with our partner doctors and Thurston Group, we are building a company with a unique, collaborative and thoughtful approach to treating patients as well as our employees."

**Key Quote (Dr. Mitch Ellingson, CCO):** "It is an honor to join Thurston and Gen4 to create what dentistry has needed to secure its future. The Gen4 Dental Partners platform will allow dentist to operate THEIR practice THEIR way with world class support behind them."

**Narrative Detail -- Lamonte Jensen's journey:** Weber State for psychology. Marine Corps infantry officer with multiple combat tours in Iraq. MBA from University of Chicago (strategic planning and entrepreneurial finance). McKinsey consultant. Then VP of Customer Success at Spear Education's consulting branch, where he built integrated service models for dental practices. That Spear experience is the turning point -- he saw from the inside what dentists actually struggled with, and realized the DSO model needed to be rebuilt from the dentist's perspective, not the investor's.

**Narrative Detail -- Dr. Mitch Ellingson's journey:** 13 years as a practicing dentist owning multiple practices in Phoenix. Then he founded and led Spear Practice Solutions. He has personally lived the transition from "king of your own island" to collaborative leader. As he put it: "I've personally experienced it. I've been through the transition of going from private-practice dentist to working inside a group, and it is really hard. It's way harder than anybody anticipates."

**Growth Speed:** Founded April 2021. By end of 2022 (20 months): 90 practices, 12 states, 165 clinicians, 323 hygienists, 1,365 employees. Jensen predicted it would double the following year. That velocity itself tells a story -- dentists were hungry for this model.

### MODIS's Founding Story (September 2023)

**Story Core:** Thurston saw a gap no one else was filling -- implant dentistry was booming but dentists had nowhere to get world-class hands-on training AND business support. So they partnered with the most respected implantologist in the country to build the platform.

MODIS is an acronym: **MOD**ern **I**mplantologi**S**ts. The name itself is a narrative -- this is not your father's dental implant practice.

The anchor partnership is with Dr. Michael A. Pikos and the Pikos Institute, a globally recognized dental education center. Dr. Pikos is a pioneer in implantology. Having him as the founding partner gave MODIS instant clinical credibility that no amount of marketing could buy.

**Key Quote (Pete Wong, MODIS CEO):** "Our goal is to provide implant professionals with the tools they need to thrive in today's dental industry. MODIS will offer world-class, hands-on education and support services, all of which will allow our partner practices to expand their capabilities and provide better care to their patients."

**Key Quote (Dr. Michael A. Pikos):** "The future of implantology will be driven by innovation. Advancing technology will make procedures more efficient while enhancing patient comfort and outcomes. It begins with building a community of lifelong learners who share our ambition to push boundaries."

**Narrative Angle:** MODIS solved the education problem. Most DSOs acquire practices and squeeze margins. MODIS acquires practices and makes their dentists *better*. The Pikos Institute is not a cost center -- it is the competitive moat.

### Dr. Grant Olson's Personal Journey

**Story Core:** A kid from Springfield, Missouri who started out wanting to be an accountant discovered dentistry, built a YouTube channel with 138,000 subscribers and 23 million views, patented implant technology, and became the most visible clinical leader in a billion-dollar DSO platform -- all without leaving his hometown.

Dr. Grant Olson's journey is the single most powerful narrative asset SGA has. The MODIS Community Conversations podcast (Episode 103) reveals his arc: initial interest in accounting, then a pivot to dentistry, then a deep dive into implant dentistry, then building Innovative Dental in Springfield into something that does not look like a dental practice at all -- it looks like a spa. Then building a YouTube audience that most dental practices could never dream of. Then partnering with MODIS.

**Key Details for Storytelling:**
- Grew up in Springfield, Missouri -- stayed to build his practice there
- One of only 300 CEREC Mentors worldwide
- Uses patented guided implant technology
- Built a YouTube presence of 138K subscribers and 23M+ views -- organic, no paid promotion agency
- His practice has 3,390+ five-star reviews
- His standard opening: "What's up, guys? This is Dr. O from Innovative Dental" -- casual, approachable, not clinical
- His patients travel from across the country (Josh, a wedding photographer from Florida; patients from multiple states)
- His sign-off: "Keep smiling... it looks good on you!"
- Moving to live, interactive content in 2026: "You're going to see a lot less canned videos, edited videos... what you're going to see is a lot of live content. That authentic interaction, the face to face."

**Key Quote (Dr. Grant Olson):** "It's astonishing how many people suffer emotionally and physically from a poor smile. We love seeing the impact our work can make on peoples' lives when they put their trust in us to transform their smile."

**Key Quote (Dr. Grant Olson, from product brief):** "Implant Dentistry is about giving people back all of life's moments. If you have to think about your teeth, those moments aren't there. I want to dedicate my life to helping my patients and a team rally around the cause of making sure everybody lives full every one of life's moments."

**Narrative Angle:** The "anti-corporate dentist." He could be anywhere -- celebrity dentist in LA, faculty at a dental school, running a national chain. Instead, he is in Springfield, Missouri, building a spa-like practice with a concierge who guides patients through a "care itinerary," doing live YouTube sessions to talk directly to scared patients, and training other practices to replicate what he has built. That is the SGA story in one person.

### The Convergence Moment

**Story Core:** Three entities, built independently over five years, each solving a different piece of the dental puzzle, realize they are stronger as one.

SGA (the original South Georgia Dental Management) had the Southeast community dentistry network. Gen4 had the collaborative doctor culture and national general dentistry footprint. MODIS had the specialty vertical, the education platform, and the clinical innovators.

Separately, each was good. Together, they cover the full spectrum: community dentistry to cutting-edge implantology, small-town practices to nationally recognized clinical leaders, back-office support to continuing education.

**Narrative Angle:** This is not a corporate merger. It is a convergence of philosophies. The "scale vs. soul" tension that every DSO faces is not resolved by choosing one -- it is resolved by building all three layers (community, collaboration, innovation) and then connecting them.

## 2. Customer Transformation Stories

### The Practice Owner Who Found Relief

**Composite Narrative Arc (from Cavendish case study and collaborative model content):**

**Before:** Dr. Matt Cavendish, 20 years in private practice in Phoenix. "Maybe was in a spot of complacency, not being challenged as much, just getting through the day and not enjoying it as much." Spending his time on employee benefits, wifi routers, Facebook ads -- "a hundred other things that have little to do with dental excellence." Dealing with staff issues alone: "I had issues with staff that needed to be corrected and before I had a mentor, I was just trying to figure out by myself what I needed to do. It was very difficult."

**Turning Point:** Joined Gen4. Paired with a mentor (Dr. Bryan Shanahan) through the Individual Growth Engine. Observed his first all-on-four procedure. "His lab is very skilled in those kinds of appliances and has a more reasonable fee. So just to pick his brain on his surgical technique, treatment planning, and the way he works with his lab -- that's something I would have never had an opportunity to do if I were still in a solo practice."

**After:** Revenues TTM up 16.5%. EBITA beating plan by 2.3 points and beating last year by $45,266. Total payroll expenses down. Staff retention at 100%. Cost of dental supplies beating plan by 6.4 points. And beyond the numbers: "Finding these doctors passionate about dentistry -- well, that might kind of rejuvenate you a bit. Make it fun again."

**Key Quote (Dr. Cavendish):** "Don't push back. There's no downside to having a mentor."

**Key Quote (Dr. Cavendish):** "When you've been in dentistry for 20 years like I have, sometimes it's not as fun as it used to be. So to be able to do a complex case like this, with somebody who is also working at a very high skill level, it is fun."

### The Patient Who Got Their Life Back

**Josh's Story (from Innovative Dental YouTube, "Discover the Life-Changing Power of All on 4 Dental Implants"):**

**Before:** Josh is a wedding photographer from Florida. He suffered with failing teeth for 8-10 years. His teeth were not visible to others -- the damage was hidden -- but he was suffering. He found Dr. O through the YouTube channel and started with a virtual consultation at YourSmileDestination.com.

**Turning Point:** Flew to Springfield, Missouri. Dr. O's team discovered infection in his 3D X-ray that had been invisible on standard imaging. In a single visit: 5 implants on top, 6 on the bottom, infection removed, teeth placed same day.

**After:** Health dramatically better (chronic infection removed). Full smile restored. And Dr. O did something remarkable -- he grilled a steak for Josh after the procedure. A patient testimonial from another similar case captures the emotional payoff: "As soon as I got my final fitting there with my new teeth, Dr. Olsen grilled a nice New York strip steak for me. Nicely seasoned, cooked perfectly like I ordered, medium rare, and it was so juicy and fresh off the grill. It was awesome."

**Narrative Power:** The steak moment. That is not a clinical outcome -- it is a life moment. The ability to bite into a steak is something most people never think about. For someone who has lost their teeth, it is everything. This is Dr. O's philosophy made tangible: "giving people back all of life's moments."

## 3. Industry Tension Narratives

### What is Broken in the DSO Industry

The central tension in dental consolidation is captured perfectly by Dr. Mitch Ellingson:

**"I love being a dentist, and I want to protect my profession from becoming like medicine. In medicine, as it consolidated it became less about the doctors and patients, and more about the organization. We want it to be more about the doctors and the patients."**

This is the "ghost story" of the dental industry. Every dentist knows what happened to medicine. The physicians lost control. The administrators took over. The patient became a number. The craft became a factory. Dentistry is watching the same thing happen in real time as corporate DSOs expand, and many dentists are terrified.

**The Traditional DSO Fear:** "In a typical DSO, you fear you'll lose control. Accustomed to directing your staff as you feel best, you worry the staff will be directing you instead -- clock your minutes per patient, say, or shave your cost of materials." (Gen4 collaborative model article)

**The Elephant in the Room:** Dr. Ellingson calls it out directly: "I'm a 'call-out-the-elephant-in-the-room type guy.' And the elephant in the room is that doctors are worried Operations are going to lead them and operation teams are worried doctors are going to push back. That's been the world for the last quarter century, right?"

### Why SGA Is Different (The Anti-Corporate DSO Narrative)

SGA's positioning is not "we're a better DSO." It is "we are here to change what a DSO means."

**Key Quote (Dr. Ellingson):** "We tell them we're not a DSO. We're not playing by their rules. We are here to change dentistry. And if you don't embody that and believe that inside of your soul, you should not join Gen4. Because we aren't saying it just to attract people. We're saying it because that's who we are."

**Key Quote (Dr. Brian Harris):** "This organization is fully invested in *us* -- in us loving what we do and being successful in what we do so we can provide excellent care to our patients."

The Gen4/MODIS about pages consistently use language that positions against traditional DSOs: "Status quo? Yeah, no." (MODIS partnership page). "We studied what works for dentists. We asked leading dentists, business experts, and data analysts. We spoke to everyone who could give us insight into what drives success and prosperity for dentists. Gen4 is our answer." (Gen4 what-is page).

---

### The Autonomy Paradox

This is SGA's most counter-intuitive and therefore most powerful narrative: joining a group gives you MORE freedom, not less.

**Key Quote (Dr. Ellingson):** "Typically, you're worried someone's going to start telling you what you can and can't do. But when we talk about autonomy, actually, it's the opposite of that -- the reality is we're going to ask you to lead more. We're going to amplify your voice. Your ideas are going to influence everyone. And theirs are going to influence you. That's the exact opposite of going it alone."

**Key Quote (Dr. Ellingson):** "For a lot of docs, they've been on their own for such a long time that they almost forgot how exciting it is to be with peers who are challenging each other to think bigger, to take the next step, to evolve the idea."

The "goose bumps" moment from the Gen4 workshops crystallizes this: senior clinicians brainstorming together "naturally elevated the ideas together. And when you elevate an idea together, that's where the goose bumps come from, right? That's where you go, 'wow, okay!' They're like, 'I've never thought about it like that.' Now you got me, right?"

## 4. Aspirational Narratives

### The "Last Great Profession" Vision

**Key Quote (Dr. Ellingson):** "Dentistry is the last great profession that is run by the people who do it -- the dentists."

This is a rallying cry. Medicine lost this. Pharmacy lost this. Dentistry is the last holdout. And the question SGA poses is: will consolidation destroy dentistry the way it destroyed the doctor-patient relationship in medicine? Or can consolidation be done differently -- in a way that actually strengthens the profession?

The ADA endorsement adds institutional weight to this narrative. At the first-ever DSO event that the ADA president addressed, Dr. Cesar R. Sabates said: "Today I offer you this simple message: The ADA has your back. You are the living embodiment of the ADA's vision to achieve optimum dental health for all."

### The "Improve 1% Every Day" Philosophy

Gen4's stated goal -- "Improve 1% every day" -- is a powerful narrative device because it is unglamorous, consistent, and true to how excellence actually works. It stands in contrast to the "disrupt everything" language of corporate DSOs. SGA is not trying to blow up dentistry. It is trying to make it incrementally better, every single day, across every practice, through collaboration.

## 6. Brand Mythology Seeds

### Recurring Themes Across All Source Material

**Theme 1: "Life's Moments"**
Dr. Olson's phrase -- "giving people back all of life's moments" -- appears repeatedly. The steak after surgery. The wedding photographer who can smile at weddings again. The nurse who can finally show confidence. This is not about teeth. It is about the moments teeth make possible.
*Usage:* Patient-facing content, emotional campaigns, video outros

**Theme 2: "Dentists Teaching Dentists"**
The mentorship model runs through Gen4 (Individual Growth Engine, Dr. Cavendish/Dr. Shanahan), MODIS (Pikos Institute), and Innovative Dental (Dr. O training other offices). This is the cultural DNA -- not corporate training programs, but peer-to-peer learning.
*Usage:* Recruitment content, LinkedIn thought leadership, conference positioning

**Theme 3: "Rejuvenation"**
Dr. Cavendish uses the word. The nurse's story is one of rejuvenation. Even Dr. O's shift to live content in 2026 is about rejuvenation -- finding authentic connection after years of edited videos. SGA does not just fix practices -- it rejuvenates the people who run them.
*Usage:* Mid-career targeting, brand narrative, partner testimonials

**Theme 4: "The Fourth Generation"**
The generational framework (Gen1 through Gen4) is a powerful structuring device. It implies inevitability -- this is where the industry was always heading. It implies progress -- each generation learned from the last. And it implies that anyone still operating a Gen2 or Gen3 model is already behind.
*Usage:* Competitive positioning, explainer content, sales collateral

**Theme 5: "Keep Smiling... It Looks Good on You"**
Dr. O's sign-off. Casual, warm, human. The exact opposite of corporate dental language. This could become a brand-level tagline or campaign anchor.
*Usage:* Social content sign-offs, merchandise, brand voice anchor

### SGA's Signature Narrative (The Story That Contains All Stories)

Every piece of SGA content can trace back to a single narrative spine:

**Dentistry was losing its soul. The people who loved the profession most decided to save it -- not by fighting consolidation, but by reimagining what consolidation could be. They built a place where dentists teach dentists, patients get care that changes their lives, and the business of dentistry serves the craft of dentistry instead of the other way around.**

That is the brand mythology. Every origin story, every patient transformation, every mentorship story, every industry tension narrative is a chapter in that larger arc.

The question SGA answers is not "should I join a DSO?" It is: "What kind of dentist do I want to be, and which organization will let me become that person?"
