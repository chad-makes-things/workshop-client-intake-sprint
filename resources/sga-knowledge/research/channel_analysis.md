# SGA Dental Partners -- Marketing Channel Analysis

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Identify and rank the highest-ROI marketing channels across SGA's multi-audience model
**Audiences:** B2B (practice acquisition + dentist recruitment), B2C (patient acquisition)

---

## Executive Summary

SGA Dental operates across three distinct audience segments, each with fundamentally different buying psychology, sales cycles, and channel behavior. Practice acquisition targets (dentists considering selling) have a 6-18 month decision cycle driven by trust and peer validation. Recruitment targets (associate dentists) respond to career-stage-specific messaging on professional networks. Patient acquisition targets respond to high-intent local search and community-level social proof.

This analysis ranks 7 channels across these segments, provides cost benchmarks, and recommends a 90-day launch plan with budget allocation.

---

## Channel Rankings

### Rank 1: Google Ads -- Search (B2B Practice Acquisition)

**Why it fits:** Dentists exploring a sale or DSO partnership are high-intent searchers. Queries like "sell my dental practice," "dental practice valuation," "DSO partnership options," and "dental practice transition" represent bottom-of-funnel buyers actively researching. No other channel captures this demand as directly. The dental practice broker landscape is crowded (Henry Schein, DDSmatch, Choice Transitions, Legacy Practice Transitions all bid on these terms), which validates the demand but also signals that SGA must compete here to be in the consideration set.

**Estimated CPC/CPA:**
- CPC for dental practice transition keywords: $12-$35 (niche B2B terms with low volume but high intent)
- CPA (qualified lead): $200-$500 per inquiry
- Note: Standard dental patient keywords run $6.50-$9.75 CPC, but B2B practice sale keywords are lower volume, higher value, and less competitive than patient acquisition terms

**Best content format:** Responsive Search Ads leading to a dedicated "Explore Partnership" landing page. Headlines should reference specific outcomes: equity retention, clinical autonomy, operational relief. Descriptions should name the Thurston Group track record ($4B+ returned to partners) as a trust signal. Sitelink extensions to partner testimonials, the valuation process, and FAQ pages.

**Audience size potential:** Narrow but high-value. Approximately 202,000 licensed dentists in the US, with an estimated 10-15% actively considering transition options in any given year. Search volume for core keywords is low (hundreds to low thousands per month nationally), but each conversion represents a multi-million dollar acquisition opportunity.

**Classification:** Quick win. Campaigns can be live within 1-2 weeks. Results within 30-60 days given the longer B2B sales cycle.

**Competitor activity:** Dental brokers (Henry Schein Dental Practice Transitions, Professional Transition Strategies, ADS Transitions) dominate the "sell dental practice" keyword space. Competing DSOs like Heartland Dental, Pacific Dental Services, and Aspen Dental run brand campaigns but are less aggressive on transition-specific search terms. This creates an opening for SGA to own intent-based acquisition queries where brokers currently capture demand that should flow directly to DSOs.

**Primary recommendation:** Run branded search (protect "SGA Dental," "Gen4 Dental," "MODIS Dental") alongside non-branded intent campaigns targeting transition, valuation, and partnership queries. Allocate $3,000-$5,000/month initially, scaling based on lead quality.

### Rank 2: LinkedIn (B2B Practice Acquisition + Recruitment)

**Why it fits:** LinkedIn is the only platform where SGA can target dentists by job title, practice ownership status, specialty, geography, and career stage simultaneously. For practice acquisition, LinkedIn Sponsored Content positions SGA's thought leadership (Lamonte Jensen's industry perspective, the Gen4 model, Thurston Group's track record) in front of practice owners during professional browsing. For recruitment, LinkedIn is where associate dentists evaluate career opportunities. The platform serves double duty as both an acquisition and recruitment channel.

**Estimated CPM/CPA:**
- CPM: $31-$50 for healthcare/dental professional targeting
- CPC: $8-$15 for dentist-targeted campaigns
- CPL (Lead Gen Forms): $100-$150 per qualified lead
- Note: LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms convert at 6-10%, roughly double the rate of external landing pages, making the higher CPL more efficient on a cost-per-qualified-lead basis

**Best content format:**
- *Acquisition:* Thought Leader Ads featuring Lamonte Jensen or Dr. Mitch Ellingson speaking about the Gen4/SGA model. Document Ads with a gated "Partnership Guide" or "Practice Valuation Checklist." Sponsored Content linking to partner testimonial case studies.
- *Recruitment:* Sponsored job posts for associate positions at SGA practices. Carousel ads showcasing CE opportunities (Pikos Institute, 4Front symposium), benefits, and culture.

**Audience size potential:**
- Practice owners/dentists on LinkedIn: Estimated 80,000-120,000 profiles (not all 202K licensed dentists are active, but LinkedIn penetration among practice owners skews higher than general population)
- Targetable by geography to match SGA's footprint states (GA, MS, AL, LA, TN, AZ, UT, MI, CA, NV, MN, TX, FL, MO, NC, MD, IA)
- Recruitment audience is broader: any licensed dentist or dental school graduate in target states

**Classification:** Long-term play for acquisition (trust-building through consistent thought leadership). Quick win for recruitment (job ads generate applicants within days).

**Competitor activity:** DSOs are underinvesting on LinkedIn relative to the opportunity. Most dental industry LinkedIn activity is organic thought leadership from individual dentists and consultants, not paid campaigns from DSOs. This is a gap SGA can exploit. Heartland Dental and Pacific Dental Services maintain LinkedIn company pages but do not run aggressive paid campaigns targeting practice owners for acquisition.

**Primary recommendation:** Split LinkedIn into two campaign objectives. Acquisition campaigns: $2,000-$4,000/month on Thought Leader Ads and Document Ads targeting practice owners in SGA footprint states. Recruitment campaigns: $1,500-$2,500/month on sponsored job posts and culture-focused content targeting associate dentists.

### Rank 3: Google Local Services Ads + Google Business Profile (B2C Patient Acquisition)

**Why it fits:** Patients searching for dental services have the highest commercial intent of any audience segment. "Dentist near me," "emergency dentist," "dental implants [city]" are bottom-of-funnel queries that convert directly to appointments. Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) sit above organic results and standard search ads, displaying a Google Verified badge that builds immediate trust. For a multi-location DSO, this channel scales per-practice while maintaining local relevance.

**Estimated CPL:**
- LSA cost per lead: $45-$85 for general dentistry, $90-$130+ for high-value services (implants, cosmetic)
- Standard search CPC for patient keywords: $6.50-$9.75 in competitive markets, $2-$3 in smaller towns
- Google Business Profile optimization: $0 direct cost (organic), but requires ongoing review management and content posting

**Best content format:**
- LSAs: Profile optimization with verified licenses, high review counts, and specific service categories enabled. Toggle off low-value services (cleanings) to focus budget on high-value patients (implants, crowns, cosmetic).
- Google Business Profile: Weekly posts, photo updates, Q&A management, and active review response. Each SGA practice location needs an optimized profile.

**Audience size potential:** Massive. Dental services have consistent year-round demand. Each practice location can capture patients within a 5-15 mile radius. With 60+ locations across 17+ states, the aggregate patient acquisition opportunity runs into hundreds of thousands of annual searches.

**Classification:** Quick win. LSAs can be activated per practice within 3-5 weeks (including Google Verified badge verification). GBP optimization begins generating organic visibility within 30-60 days.

**Competitor activity:** Every dental practice competes in local search. DSOs with centralized marketing (Heartland, Aspen, Pacific Dental) have an advantage because they systematize GBP management across all locations. SGA must do the same. The competitive advantage here is not strategy but execution consistency across the entire network.

**Primary recommendation:** Roll out LSAs to all SGA practice locations in a phased approach (start with highest-revenue practices). Budget $500-$2,000/month per practice depending on market competitiveness. Simultaneously implement a centralized GBP optimization protocol across all locations.

### Rank 4: Meta Ads -- Facebook/Instagram (B2C Patient Acquisition + B2B Awareness)

**Why it fits:** Meta serves two functions for SGA. For patient acquisition, Facebook and Instagram local ads are the most cost-effective way to generate demand for dental services among people not actively searching (demand creation vs. demand capture). For B2B awareness, Meta's targeting can reach dentists through interest and behavior-based audiences, though with less precision than LinkedIn.

**Estimated CPM/CPA:**
- Patient acquisition CPL: $50-$80 for general dentistry leads (industry average is $76.71, trending upward with a 97% YoY increase)
- CPC for dental: $9.78 average (significantly above the cross-industry average of $1.92)
- CTR for dental lead campaigns: 1.05% (lowest among all industries on Meta)
- B2B (dentist awareness): CPM $15-$30, but conversion to qualified acquisition leads is low; use as a top-of-funnel supplement to LinkedIn

**Best content format:**
- *Patient acquisition:* Before/after transformation photos (especially for the MODIS implant vertical, where Dr. Grant Olson's content library is a major asset). Short-form video testimonials from patients. Carousel ads showing multiple service categories. Local event promotions and community content.
- *B2B awareness:* Video snippets of the 4Front symposium, partner testimonials, Pikos Institute content. These run as awareness campaigns to warm up dentists before they see LinkedIn or search ads.

**Audience size potential:**
- B2C: Virtually unlimited. Facebook reaches 70%+ of US adults. Geo-targeted to practice locations with demographic layering (household income, family status, age).
- B2B: Meta estimates approximately 1-2 million people in the US with "dentist" as a job title or employer affiliation. Much less precise than LinkedIn for targeting practice owners specifically.

**Classification:** Quick win for B2C patient acquisition (campaigns live in days, leads within the first week). Long-term play for B2B awareness (requires sustained frequency to build familiarity).

**Competitor activity:** Dental practices are heavy Meta advertisers. Heartland Dental, Aspen Dental, and independent practices all run local patient acquisition ads on Facebook and Instagram. The Meta Ad Library shows continuous dental advertising across geographies. DSOs with centralized marketing run templated campaigns across locations. SGA's advantage here is Dr. Grant Olson's content library (138K YouTube subscribers, 23M+ views) -- repurposing that video content for Meta ads gives SGA authenticity that templated corporate dental ads lack.

**Primary recommendation:** B2C patient acquisition: $1,000-$3,000/month per practice cluster (can be managed centrally with location-specific ad sets). Focus on the MODIS implant vertical first, where patient lifetime value is highest and Dr. Olson's content provides a creative moat. B2B awareness: $1,000-$2,000/month as a supplementary awareness layer.

### Rank 5: Dental Industry Events and Conferences (B2B Practice Acquisition)

**Why it fits:** The dental practice acquisition decision is fundamentally a relationship and trust decision. Dentists selling their life's work need face-to-face validation. Events like the Dykema DSO Conference, ADA Annual Meeting, Hinman Dental Meeting, and state dental association conferences are where practice owners encounter DSO options in a low-pressure, peer-surrounded environment. SGA's existing events (the 4Front symposium, MODIS Community Conversations) are also recruitment and retention tools that double as acquisition marketing.

**Estimated cost:**
- Conference sponsorship: $5,000-$50,000 depending on tier and event
- Booth staffing and materials: $3,000-$10,000 per event
- Speaking engagements: Often free if accepted, but require preparation investment
- Cost per qualified lead from events: $300-$800 (higher than digital, but lead quality is significantly better)

**Best content format:** Booth presence with partner testimonials on display. Speaking engagements by Lamonte Jensen, Dr. Ellingson, or Dr. Pikos (established industry authority). Hosted dinners or small-group conversations with prospective partners. Post-event retargeting campaigns on LinkedIn and Meta to attendees.

**Audience size potential:** Concentrated but high-quality. The Dykema DSO Conference draws 1,500+ attendees. ADA Annual Meeting draws 25,000+. State meetings draw 500-5,000. The audience is pre-qualified: they are practicing dentists who invest in their professional development.

**Classification:** Long-term play. Event ROI compounds over time as SGA builds name recognition in the conference circuit. Each event is also a content creation opportunity (recorded talks, photos, partner stories) that feeds digital channels.

**Competitor activity:** Every major DSO invests in event presence. Heartland Dental, Pacific Dental, and Aspen Dental are fixtures at Dykema and major state meetings. Thurston Group portfolio companies (Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners) also attend. SGA must match this presence to be taken seriously in the acquisition conversation.

### Rank 6: Dental Trade Publications (B2B Acquisition + Recruitment)

**Why it fits:** Dental Economics, DentistryIQ, ADA News, Becker's Dental Review, and Group Dentistry Now are where practice owners consume industry news and trend analysis. Thought leadership articles, sponsored content, and display advertising in these publications reach dentists during their professional information-gathering behavior. Becker's annual "DSOs to Know" list is particularly relevant -- SGA should be on it.

**Estimated cost:**
- Sponsored article/native content: $3,000-$10,000 per placement
- Display advertising: CPM $30-$80 for dental trade publications
- Contributed editorial (byline articles): Free, but requires content production investment
- Podcast sponsorship (dental industry shows): $500-$3,000 per episode

**Best content format:** Contributed articles by SGA leadership on topics that matter to prospective partners: succession planning, the evolution of DSO models, clinical autonomy in partnership structures, how PE-backed DSOs create wealth for dentists. These are not advertisements; they are thought leadership pieces that position SGA as the informed, dentist-centric alternative to corporate consolidation.

**Audience size potential:** Dental Economics reaches approximately 120,000 dentists. DentistryIQ and Becker's Dental Review have significant digital audiences. Group Dentistry Now is specifically focused on DSO-interested dentists and is the most targeted publication for acquisition marketing.

**Classification:** Long-term play. Trade publication presence builds authority over months and years. The content also feeds digital retargeting: dentists who read an SGA article in Dental Economics can be retargeted on LinkedIn and Meta.

### Rank 7: Patient Referral Programs + Community Presence (B2C Patient Acquisition)

**Why it fits:** SGA's brand positioning as "hometown dental practices with big-platform support" makes grassroots patient referral the most brand-aligned growth channel. Referral patients have lower acquisition cost, higher lifetime value, and better retention than any paid channel. Community sponsorships (Little League teams, school dental health days, charity events) reinforce the local identity that SGA explicitly preserves when acquiring practices.

**Estimated cost:**
- Referral program: $25-$75 per referred patient (gift cards, account credits, or charitable donations in the patient's name)
- Community sponsorship: $500-$5,000 per event depending on market size
- Cost per acquired patient via referral: $15-$40 (dramatically lower than any paid channel)

**Best content format:** In-office referral cards and signage. Post-visit email/text with referral link. Social media posts celebrating community involvement. Patient appreciation events. Nextdoor posts from practice staff about community participation.

**Audience size potential:** Each existing patient has an average social circle of 5-15 people who need dental care. With 60+ practice locations and thousands of active patients, the referral network potential is enormous.

**Classification:** Quick win for setup (referral programs can be standardized across all practices within 30 days). Long-term compounding asset (referral volume grows as patient base grows).

## Budget Allocation Recommendation

### Total recommended monthly budget: $25,000-$40,000

This assumes an initial 90-day testing phase. Budget should increase 30-50% after the first quarter if channel performance validates the allocation.

| Channel | % of Budget | Monthly Range | Rationale |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Google Search (B2B acquisition) | 15% | $3,750-$6,000 | Highest-intent channel for acquisition leads. Low volume but high value per conversion. |
| LinkedIn (B2B acquisition + recruitment) | 20% | $5,000-$8,000 | Only platform with precise professional targeting. Split 60/40 between acquisition and recruitment. |
| Google LSAs (B2C patient acquisition) | 25% | $6,250-$10,000 | Distributed across practice locations. Most direct patient acquisition channel. Scales per-practice. |
| Meta Ads (B2C + B2B awareness) | 20% | $5,000-$8,000 | B2C patient demand generation (80% of Meta spend) and B2B awareness (20%). Leverages Dr. Olson content. |
| Trade Publications + Content | 10% | $2,500-$4,000 | Contributed articles, 1-2 sponsored placements. Builds authority for B2B acquisition pipeline. |
| Events + Community | 10% | $2,500-$4,000 | Conference deposits, community sponsorships, referral program infrastructure. Front-loaded in Q1. |

### Budget Scaling Triggers

Increase channel budget by 25-30% when:
- Google Search B2B: Cost per qualified acquisition lead drops below $350
- LinkedIn B2B: Lead Gen Form CPL drops below $120, or a lead converts to a partnership conversation
- Google LSAs: Practice-level ROI exceeds 5:1 on new patient lifetime value
- Meta B2C: CPL stabilizes below $65 with acceptable patient show rates

Reduce or pause channel budget when:
- LinkedIn B2B acquisition: CPL exceeds $200 with no qualified conversations after 60 days
- Meta B2C: CPL exceeds $100 with declining CTR (signal of creative fatigue or audience saturation)

## Key Strategic Notes

**1. The acquisition funnel is a trust funnel, not a conversion funnel.** Dentists selling their practice are making a once-in-a-career decision. Paid ads start the conversation, but conversion happens through peer validation, personal meetings, and demonstrated track record. Every paid channel should feed into a nurture system (email, retargeting, personal outreach from the business development team) rather than expect immediate form fills.

**2. Dr. Grant Olson's content library is SGA's single biggest creative advantage.** 138K YouTube subscribers and 23M+ views in dental content is a competitive moat no other DSO can replicate quickly. Repurpose this content across Meta ads, LinkedIn, and even Google YouTube campaigns for the implant vertical. This alone justifies prioritizing the MODIS implant vertical for B2C patient acquisition.

**3. Centralized marketing operations are the real differentiator.** Individual dental practices cannot run sophisticated multi-channel campaigns. The DSOs that win patient acquisition (Heartland, Aspen) do so through systematized, centralized marketing deployed across every location. SGA's promise of "marketing and brand support" to partner dentists must be backed by a real marketing operations infrastructure that manages LSAs, GBP, Meta ads, and review management at scale.

**4. Practice acquisition marketing has almost no competition from other DSOs in paid search.** Dental brokers own the paid search landscape for practice transition queries. DSOs that run their own acquisition search campaigns will intercept prospects before they enter the broker ecosystem, saving the typical 8-12% broker commission on deal value.
