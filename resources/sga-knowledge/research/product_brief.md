# SGA Dental Partners -- Product Brief

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Status:** Active roll-up / scaling phase
**Category:** Dental Service Organization (DSO)

---

## 1. Product / Brand Name and Category

**SGA Dental Partners** is a dental service organization (DSO) consolidating multiple dental practice groups and specialty DSOs under a unified ownership and operational platform. SGA is headquartered at 43 Ford Way, Richmond Hill, GA 31324. The brand encompasses general dentistry, pediatric dentistry, periodontics, oral surgery, implantology, orthodontics, and cosmetic dentistry across a growing multi-state footprint.

Two previously independent Thurston Group-backed DSOs -- **Gen4 Dental Partners** and **MODIS Dental Partners** -- are being rolled into the SGA umbrella, creating a significantly larger combined entity.

---

## 2. Business Model

SGA operates the **private equity-backed DSO roll-up model**, with the Thurston Group as the financial sponsor. The model works as follows:

- **Acquisition:** SGA acquires dental practices through a joint-venture equity partnership. Selling dentists receive a liquidity event while retaining an equity stake in the combined entity, aligning their financial interests with the broader platform's growth.
- **Operational support:** SGA provides centralized back-office functions (billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing, recruiting, benefits administration) so that dentists can focus exclusively on clinical care.
- **Clinical autonomy:** Partner dentists retain full clinical independence. Practices keep their existing brand identity, standards of care, patient relationships, and community presence.
- **Equity participation:** Partners receive ownership shares in SGA for as long as they remain active partners, creating long-term wealth through the platform's compounding value.

The Thurston Group has used this exact playbook across 8+ dental and healthcare platforms (Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management, South Georgia Dental Management, Gen4 Dental Partners, MODIS, ARC Health, US Orthopaedic Partners). Over its 37-year history, Thurston has returned over $4 billion of invested capital to partners.

## 3. Core Value Proposition

**One sentence:** SGA gives dentists financial freedom, operational relief, and a collaborative peer community while preserving the clinical autonomy and local brand identity they built.

**Expanded:** Unlike corporate DSOs that standardize and homogenize practices (Gen2/Gen3 models), SGA positions itself as a "partnership of dentists invested in each other." The pitch to prospective partners is: you keep your name, your team, your way of practicing -- SGA handles the business operations you don't want to deal with, connects you to a network of high-performing peers, invests in your technology and growth, and gives you an equity stake in something much larger than your solo practice. As one partner put it: "SGA supports us and manages things behind the scenes. Our partnership allows us to focus on changing lives." -- Dr. Mitchel S. Godat, Periodontal Associates of Memphis.

The tagline on the SGA website captures this: **"Clinical freedom surrounded by the best of everything."**

## 4. Target Audiences

### Primary: Dentists and practice owners considering a DSO partnership
- Mid-career dentists looking for a liquidity event without losing their practice identity
- Practice owners feeling the weight of administrative burden (HR, billing, compliance) who want to "get back to dentistry"
- Growth-minded practitioners who want access to better technology, CE, and peer collaboration
- Dentists approaching retirement who need a succession plan but want to stay involved

### Secondary: Patients at SGA-affiliated practices
- Families seeking general and pediatric dentistry in hometown communities across the Southeast
- Implant patients (a major growth vertical through MODIS/Innovative Dental) seeking life-changing full-arch reconstructions
- Patients who value a premium, spa-like dental experience (the Innovative Dental model)

### Tertiary: Potential acquisition targets
- Independent dental practices and small group practices in underserved or growing markets
- Specialty practices (periodontics, oral surgery, implantology) that fit the MODIS implant-focused vertical
- Multi-location groups seeking operational scale they cannot achieve independently

## 5. Key Features and Services

### For partner dentists:
1. **Full clinical autonomy** -- Dentists practice their way; SGA does not dictate clinical protocols
2. **Back-office support** -- Billing, payroll, HR, compliance, accounts payable, materials ordering, recruiting
3. **Equity ownership stake** -- Partners own shares in SGA, diversifying their portfolio and creating long-term financial security
4. **Technology investment** -- Modern equipment acquisitions, CEREC, guided implant systems, digital workflows
5. **Continuing education** -- Through MODIS, access to the Pikos Institute for world-class implantology training; through Gen4, collaborative peer learning sessions and the annual "4Front" symposium
6. **Marketing and brand support** -- New websites, digital marketing, patient acquisition
7. **Peer collaboration network** -- Regular partner calls, clinical advisory board, cross-practice learning (dentists teaching dentists their specialties)
8. **Employee benefits package** -- 401(k), PTO, CE stipends, healthcare for practice teams
9. **Retention focus** -- SGA boasts an 85% staff retention rate

### For patients:
10. **Comprehensive services** -- General dentistry, implants, orthodontics, cosmetic dentistry, periodontics, oral surgery, pediatric dentistry, and med spa services -- often under one roof

## 6. Competitive Context

### Direct DSO competitors:
- **Smile Doctors** -- Another Thurston Group portfolio company (orthodontics-focused)
- **Heartland Dental** -- One of the largest DSOs nationally; Gen2 standardization model
- **Aspen Dental** -- Large-scale, consumer-facing DSO brand
- **Pacific Dental Services** -- Owner-dentist model competitor
- US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management -- Thurston specialty DSO siblings

### Competitive differentiation:
SGA's explicit positioning is as the **"4th generation" DSO**, a term coined by Gen4. The argument: Gen1 DSOs were accounting-driven back-office machines. Gen2 was the cookie-cutter standardization model ("same supplies in the same place in every office"). Gen3 was a fragmented mix of buying groups and consortiums. Gen4/SGA is the **collaborative model** -- clinician-centric, patient-obsessed, and built around peer learning rather than top-down management.

As Gen4 CEO Lamonte Jensen put it: "Most DSOs are built around the synergies of the business -- standardized accounting, automated payroll, bulk purchasing. What we wanted to do was make an organization that was clinician-focused and patient-obsessed."

CCO Dr. Mitch Ellingson added: "I love being a dentist, and I want to protect my profession from becoming like medicine. In medicine, as it consolidated it became less about the doctors and patients, and more about the organization. We want it to be more about the doctors and the patients."

## 7. Deal Structure

Based on available information, the partnership model includes:

- **Joint-venture equity partnership** -- Partners sell a portion of their practice equity to SGA while retaining an ownership stake in the combined platform
- **Honest valuation process** -- Three-step approach: Connect (introductory meeting), Engage (evaluation walkthrough), Partner (mutual fit determination and direct valuation)
- **Ownership shares for as long as you remain a partner** -- Ongoing equity participation tied to continued partnership
- **MODIS-specific:** "The joint venture equity partnership model we use has generated wealth for partners across many industries, with repeated success in the dental world. It's designed to accelerate growth and create value faster than you might on your own."

## 8. Current Stage

**Active scaling through multi-platform consolidation.** SGA is rolling up Gen4 and MODIS under a single brand umbrella. This represents a significant phase in the organization's lifecycle:

- **SGA Dental Partners (original):** Established platform with 17+ practice groups across Georgia, Mississippi, Alabama, Louisiana, and Tennessee. Originated as South Georgia Dental Management, one of Thurston Group's earlier dental investments.
- **Gen4 Dental Partners:** Formed August 2021. Started with 5 group practices and 15 locations in Arizona and Utah. Has since expanded to practices across Arizona, California, Nevada, Minnesota, Michigan, Texas, and other states. Led by CEO Lamonte Jensen (ex-McKinsey, ex-Spear Education).
- **MODIS Dental Partners:** Launched September 2023. Implantology-focused platform built around Dr. Michael A. Pikos and the Pikos Institute. Currently 9 partner practices including Innovative Dental (Springfield, MO), Troy Family Dental (MI), Coastal Jaw Surgery, Pensacola Perio (FL), Total Care Dental, Jacksonville Dental Specialists, Wake Dental Care, Dental Health Group, and the Pikos Institute itself.

The combined entity represents a substantial national footprint with differentiated verticals (general/community dentistry via SGA, collaborative general/specialty via Gen4, implantology/specialty via MODIS).

## 12. Strategic Vision

The combined SGA entity is being positioned as a next-generation DSO that solves the core tension in dental consolidation: scale vs. soul. The strategic vision has several layers:

### Short-term: Consolidation
Merge Gen4, MODIS, and legacy SGA practices under a unified brand and operational platform, preserving the strengths of each:
- SGA's established Southeast community dentistry network
- Gen4's collaborative peer-learning model and national general dentistry footprint
- MODIS's implantology specialty vertical, education platform (Pikos Institute), and high-profile clinical innovators (Dr. Grant Olson)

### Medium-term: Differentiated growth verticals
- **General/community dentistry:** Scale through acquisition of hometown practices that want operational support without losing their identity
- **Implantology/specialty:** Build on the Pikos Institute + Innovative Dental playbook to create a national network of implant-focused practices with shared training, technology, and protocols. The "Smile Center" concept site suggests this patient experience model could be templated and deployed to other locations.
- **Continuing education as competitive moat:** Both Gen4 (4Front symposium, peer collaboration) and MODIS (Pikos Institute) use education as a differentiator. This makes SGA attractive to growth-minded dentists who want to upskill, not just cash out.

### Long-term: Industry standard-bearer
As Lamonte Jensen articulated: "What I would like to do is live up to our name. I would like to be on the cutting edge of DSOs -- of dentistry, really. I would like to gather a group of very smart, very successful dentists together who like to collaborate so we can change the game a little bit."

Dr. Grant Olson's vision from the MODIS Community Conversations podcast is even more personal: "Implant Dentistry is about giving people back all of life's moments. If you have to think about your teeth, those moments aren't there. I want to dedicate my life to helping my patients and a team rally around the cause of making sure everybody lives full every one of life's moments."

### Content and marketing as growth engine
Innovative Dental's YouTube presence (138K subscribers, 23M+ views) represents a significant competitive advantage. Dr. Olson is moving toward live, interactive content in 2026: "You're going to see a lot less canned videos, edited videos... what you're going to see is a lot of live content. That authentic interaction, the face to face." This content model could be scaled across the SGA network.
