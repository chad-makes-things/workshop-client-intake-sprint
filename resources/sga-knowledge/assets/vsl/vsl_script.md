# SGA Dental Partners -- Video Sales Letter Script

**Project:** Practice Owner Acquisition VSL
**Target Avatar:** Dr. Mark Hendricks (mid-career practice owner, 52, exhausted, solution-aware, Schwartz Level 4 sophistication)
**Target Length:** 16:00-17:30
**Brand Archetype:** Sage (earned authority, evidence over claims, never hype)
**Primary Formula:** PASTOR (Problem > Amplify > Story > Transformation > Offer > Response), adapted for Sage voice
**Presenter:** Dr. Mitch Ellingson, CCO (or Lamonte Jensen, CEO) -- practicing dentist who has lived the transition

---

## Pre-Production Notes

**Tone Direction:** This is not a pitch. It is a quiet, evidence-heavy conversation between a dentist who has been on both sides and a dentist who is considering the crossing. Think Charlie Rose interview energy, not QVC. Pacing is deliberate. Pauses matter. The presenter speaks like someone who has seen everything and is sharing what they learned -- not selling.

**Visual Direction:** Clean, warm, professional. No stock footage of smiling businesspeople. Real offices. Real people. Real equipment. Muted color grade -- not cold, not oversaturated. Cinematic but grounded. Think documentary, not commercial.

**Music Direction:** Minimal. Ambient, understated piano or acoustic guitar under problem sections. Silence during key quotes and testimonials. Music should never compete with the words.

**Read-Aloud Pace:** Script written at approximately 140 words per minute (conversational pace, not announcer pace). Total word count targets ~2,300-2,450 words of spoken dialogue.

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** *76% of practices find hygienist recruiting "extremely challenging." -- ADA Workforce Report*

**Path two: You sell to a corporate DSO.**

This is the path seven out of ten dentists regret.

You know the stories. You have heard them at Hinman. You have read them on Dentaltown.

"I didn't get the support I thought. They just took control, but I'm still running it."

"The money -- they had a clawback I didn't know about."

"I wish I would have never done this."

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** *"I wish I would have never done this." -- Dentist with $10M in cash, Dentist Advisors Podcast*

And he still wished he had not done it.

Because the money is not the problem.

The problem is what happens after.

Your name comes off the door. Your staff gets "reorganized." Your patients start seeing a different face every six months.

And you -- the person who built the thing -- become an employee in your own practice.

[BEAT]

Better than the first two. Maybe.

But a broker's job is to close a transaction.

Not to protect your legacy.

Not to invest in your clinical growth.

Not to make sure your team has a career path.

A broker gets paid at closing. What happens to you in Year Two, Year Three, Year Five -- that is not their problem.

**VISUAL:** Presenter, direct to camera. Calm. Unhurried.

Three paths. Two dead ends. And one that is transactional by design.

What if there is a fourth?

If producing this VSL, capture video testimonials from these partners (in priority order):

1. **Dr. Mitchel Godat** -- "QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office" quote is gold. Capture him in his Memphis office, speaking naturally about the relief.
2. **Dr. Matt Cavendish** -- The rejuvenation arc. Capture him describing the mentorship experience and the "it is fun" moment. Ideally in a clinical setting.

### Text Overlay Style Guide

- Font: Clean sans-serif (Avenir, Inter, or similar). Not bold. Light weight.
- Color: White text, slight drop shadow, on dark semi-transparent overlay
- Animation: Fade in, hold 3-4 seconds, fade out. No slides, no swoops, no kinetic typography.
- Placement: Lower third for attributions. Center screen for key statistics and VoC quotes.
- Quotes always attributed with name and role.
- Statistics always attributed with source.
