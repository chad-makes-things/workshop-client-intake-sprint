## Part 2: Testimonial Collection System

### When to Ask -- Milestone Triggers

Timing is the single biggest driver of testimonial quality. Ask at peak emotional moments, not when it is convenient for SGA.

**Trigger 1: 90-Day Mark (First Wins)**

This is the highest-quality window for practice partners. The initial fears have been resolved. The first tangible operational changes have happened (payroll automated, billing team engaged, first HR question handled by PeopleOps). The emotional relief is fresh.

For patients: 90 days post-procedure is when the temporaries have been replaced by finals, healing is complete, and the full emotional payoff of the transformation is being lived daily.

What to ask for at 90 days: written testimonial, willingness to do a 30-minute video testimonial call.

**Trigger 2: 6-Month Mark (Results Are Visible)**

For practice partners: the first financial results are visible. Revenue trends, staff retention data, EBITA comparisons. This is when the quantifiable proof exists.

For associates: first complex cases completed with mentor support. Equity investment made. Real career trajectory visible.

What to ask for at 6 months: documented results for case study (revenue figures, EBITA, staff retention, new procedures added), extended video testimonial, willingness to speak to prospective partners.

**Trigger 3: 12-Month Mark (The Full Story)**

The most complete and credible proof. A year of data, a year of relationship. This is the "Dr. Cavendish level" case study moment.

What to ask for at 12 months: long-form case study interview (45-60 minutes), permission to publish specific financial metrics, willingness to be contactable by prospective partners ("call them yourself" offer).

**Trigger 4: Organic Praise (Never Let It Pass)**

When a partner, patient, or staff member expresses unsolicited gratitude -- in a meeting, on a call, in a text message, on social media -- that is a collection opportunity. Acknowledge the compliment, then ask: "I am so glad to hear that. Would you be willing to say that in a more formal way so we can share it?"

**Trigger 5: NPS Score of 9 or 10**

Any partner, patient, or staff member who scores 9 or 10 on a Net Promoter Score survey is a self-identified promoter. Follow up immediately (within 24 hours) with a request for a testimonial.

### What to Ask -- The 10 Proof-Generating Questions

Regardless of format (written or video), these questions generate the most deployable proof content.

1. What was your practice/life situation like before you made this decision?
2. What was the single thing you were most worried about?
3. What made you take the first conversation with SGA seriously?
4. What happened in the first 90 days that confirmed you made the right choice -- or the wrong one?
5. Give me a specific number, outcome, or moment that captures what changed.
6. What do you spend your time on now that you used to spend on something else? Or: What do you NOT do anymore?
7. Is there anything that went differently from what you expected -- positively or negatively?
8. What is the thing about this partnership that nobody would guess from the outside looking in?
9. Who in your life has this affected beyond just your practice? (Family, staff, patients)
10. If you were having coffee with a dentist/patient/colleague who is exactly where you were a year ago -- what would you say to them?

### Review Request Process (Per Practice)

Build this into the post-procedure workflow so review requests happen systematically, not when someone remembers.

**For patients:**
1. At checkout, staff says: "We are so glad your appointment went well. Would you mind sharing your experience with others? A quick Google review helps other patients find us." Hand them a printed card with QR code linking directly to the review page.
2. 24 hours later: automated text or email with direct link and 3-question prompt.
3. 7 days later: second request (once only, do not repeat after two requests).

**For partners (new practice onboarding):**
1. At 90-day check-in call, partnership team asks directly: "Your experience has been really positive. Would you be open to sharing a review on Google or Glassdoor? It helps other dentists who are evaluating their options."
2. Provide a direct link. Make it as easy as possible.

**Response protocol for all negative reviews:**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Acknowledge the concern directly
- Do not be defensive
- Offer to resolve it offline with contact information
- Do not reveal patient or partner details in the public response

## Part 4: Social Proof Placement Map

The right proof at the wrong stage of the funnel does not convert. This map specifies which proof type deploys where.

### Trust Signal Hierarchy by Funnel Stage

**TOFU (Top of Funnel -- Awareness)**

Objective: Establish category credibility. The prospect does not know SGA exists or has only heard the name. They need to quickly understand why this is worth learning more about.

Proof types to deploy:
- Aggregate numbers (scale + track record): "100+ partner practices. 38 years. $4B+ returned."
- Third-party validation: press mentions, Becker's Dental features, ADA endorsement at 4Front Symposium
- Authority signals: Thurston Group name, Dr. Michael Pikos name, Dr. Grant Olson's YouTube audience
- Logo bar: recognizable SGA partner practice names (Gen4 practices, MODIS practices, Southeast SGA practices)

Where: Paid social ads, YouTube pre-roll, Dentaltown sponsored content, Google display, podcast sponsorships

Tone: Numbers only. No stories yet. The TOFU job is pattern interruption and credibility signaling. "38 years. 8 platforms. $4B+ returned to partners." Full stop.

---

**MOFU (Middle of Funnel -- Consideration)**

Objective: The prospect knows SGA exists and is evaluating. They are actively comparing. They need to understand specifically how SGA is different and whether it works for people like them.

Proof types to deploy:
- Named testimonials with specific outcomes (Dr. Godat: "QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office." Dr. Cavendish: 16.5% revenue growth, 100% staff retention)
- Case study summaries (one-page format or case study teaser with "read the full story" CTA)
- Comparison content: "Why SGA vs. [category of DSO]" framed through partner voices
- Avatar-matched proof: show the practice owner talking to practice owners, the specialist to specialists, the patient to patients

Where: Email nurture sequences, blog/SEO content, LinkedIn thought leadership, retargeting ads, landing pages, webinar content

Tone: Specificity. The MOFU reader is sophisticated (Schwartz Level 4). They will dismiss generic claims. Give them names, numbers, and enough detail that they could verify it themselves.

---

**BOFU (Bottom of Funnel -- Decision)**

Objective: The prospect is ready to act or close to it. They are asking "why SGA and not someone else?" and "is this real?" They need the strongest possible proof and the lowest possible barrier to starting the conversation.

Proof types to deploy:
- Full-length video testimonials (3-5 minutes, partner or patient in their own words)
- Long-form case studies (full 1,500-2,000 word format)
- "Call them yourself" offers: partner phone numbers or email addresses offered to prospective partners who want to speak to someone who has already made the decision
- ROI calculator / valuation assessment tool (where does their practice fit in the equity model?)
- Live or recorded partner panel Q&A
- Specific objection-handling testimonials: short video clips where a partner directly addresses the most common fear (autonomy, financial terms, staff impact)

Where: Partnership landing pages, sales meeting follow-up emails, post-webinar follow-up, proposal appendix, one-on-one sales conversations

Tone: Direct and low-pressure. The BOFU proof does not need to persuade -- it needs to confirm. The reader wants validation, not a pitch. Give them every piece of evidence they need and then step back.

## Part 5: User-Generated Content Strategy

### Partner Spotlight Program

**Objective:** Create a steady stream of authentic content from SGA partners that can be amplified across SGA channels, reducing content production burden while increasing proof depth.

**Format:**
Monthly "Partner Spotlight" feature. One practice partner per month. Two to three questions asked via email or 15-minute call. Published as:
- LinkedIn post on SGA's company page (with partner tagged)
- Instagram carousel or quote graphic
- Email newsletter inclusion
- Blog post (if detailed enough)

**Question prompts for Partner Spotlight:**
1. What is one clinical case or patient outcome from the last 30 days that made you proud?
2. What is something you are able to focus on now that you could not before the partnership?
3. What is one thing you wish other practice owners knew about what the partnership actually looks like day-to-day?

**Process:**
- Partnership team nominates partners based on recent milestone (90-day mark, 12-month mark, significant clinical achievement)
- Email request sent using Template A (adapted for spotlight framing)
- Review for accuracy and permission before publishing
- Tag partner in all posts; send them a shareable link

### Patient Transformation Story Program (MODIS / Innovative Dental)

**Objective:** Build a library of before/after stories that can fuel Facebook ads, YouTube content, and landing pages. Dr. Olson's YouTube channel demonstrates the model -- extend it systematically.

**Permission requirements:**
- Written photo/video release form signed before any patient content is used
- Explicit consent for before photos and after photos separately
- Offer: provide the patient with professional photos for their own use in exchange for permission to use in SGA marketing

**Content types:**
1. Before/after photo carousel (Instagram, Facebook)
2. 60-second video testimonial (filmed with phone camera at practice -- authenticity over production value)
3. Written story (for email and website)
4. "First meal" story: ask every All-on-X patient what they ate first with their new teeth. Collect these. They are the most emotionally resonant proof assets in the library.

**Collection trigger:** At the final restoration appointment. The emotional peak. This is the moment.

### #MySGAStory Campaign

**Objective:** Activate the partner and patient community as content creators. Reduce friction by giving them a framework.

**Mechanics:**
- Provide partner dentists with a simple "Share your story" toolkit: suggested social copy, a branded frame for practice photos, and the hashtag #MySGAStory
- Prompt patients to tag Innovative Dental or their MODIS partner practice in posts about their transformation (especially the "first meal" moment)
- Feature selected #MySGAStory posts in SGA's newsletter and social feed
- Annual "Partner Spotlight Award" announced at 4Front Symposium for the partner whose content generated the most engagement or articulated the SGA story most clearly

**Activation hook:** At the 4Front Symposium, run a live "Dentists teaching dentists" video segment where partner doctors are asked to share one clinical lesson they learned from a peer in the SGA network. Film it. Post it. This is the culture on video.

## Critical Notes for the Partnership Team

**Never fabricate or embellish.** Every testimonial must be in the partner's own words, approved before publication, and attributable by name and practice. A single fabricated quote, if discovered, destroys the credibility of every real quote in the library.

**Specificity beats superlatives.** "16.5% revenue growth" is more credible than "significant revenue improvement." "QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office" is more credible than "I feel less stressed." Push for the specific detail in every testimonial collection effort.

**Permission documentation is non-negotiable.** Every piece of proof that uses a real name, a real image, a real financial figure, or a real clinical outcome requires documented written permission from the individual. Create a simple release form and use it consistently.

**Respond to everything publicly posted.** A negative review with no response looks like the company does not care. A negative review with a professional, empathetic response demonstrates that the company is accountable. Respond to all platform reviews within 48 hours.

**The "call them yourself" offer is the most powerful BOFU proof.** When a prospective practice owner says "I want to speak to someone who has actually done this," the ability to say "Here is Dr. Godat's email -- ask him anything you want" is more persuasive than any case study. Maintain a standing list of partners who have agreed to take calls from prospective partners.
