## 2. Search Intent Analysis

### What Searchers Want

Dentists searching "sell dental practice" are at a pivotal career moment. They have decided (or are seriously considering) that they want to make a change, but they do not yet know the best path forward. They need:

1. **Clarity on options** -- What are the actual paths? Private sale, broker, DSO acquisition, DSO partnership?
2. **Financial grounding** -- What is my practice worth? What are realistic numbers?
3. **Fear reduction** -- What happens to my staff? My patients? My identity?

5. **Trust signals** -- Who is giving me this information, and why should I believe them?

### H2: Your Four Options for Transitioning Your Dental Practice

**Word count: 500-700**

*H3: Option 1 -- Private Sale to Another Dentist*
- How it works: sell the entire practice to an individual buyer
- Typical timeline: 6-18 months
- Valuation: typically 60-80% of annual revenue, or 3-6x SDE
- Pros: clean exit, simple structure, practice continues under new owner
- Cons: limited buyer pool, lower valuations, no ongoing income or equity, buyer may not maintain standards
- Best for: dentists ready for a complete exit who prioritize simplicity

*H3: Option 2 -- Broker-Assisted Sale*
- How it works: engage a dental practice broker to market and sell the practice
- Typical timeline: 8-14 months with a broker
- Broker fees: 5-10% of sale price
- Pros: professional marketing, wider buyer pool, negotiation support
- Cons: broker fees reduce net proceeds, still limited to private buyer valuations, no ongoing relationship
- Best for: dentists who want professional support through a traditional sale

*H3: Option 3 -- DSO Outright Acquisition*
- How it works: a DSO purchases your practice; you receive cash and may continue as an employed dentist
- Typical timeline: 3-6 months
- Valuation: 6-10x EBITDA (often higher than private sale)
- Pros: highest cash at close, fast process, operational relief
- Cons: you become an employee; limited or no ongoing equity; practice may be standardized; brand may change
- This is the Gen2 model (Heartland, Aspen). For some dentists, this is the right answer.

*H3: Option 4 -- DSO Equity Partnership (Joint Venture)*
- How it works: you sell a portion of your equity and retain an ownership stake in the combined platform
- Typical timeline: 3-8 months to LOI, 60-120 days to close
- Valuation: competitive with outright acquisition; plus ongoing equity appreciation
- Pros: liquidity event AND ongoing equity upside; clinical autonomy preserved; practice name preserved; operational support; peer community
- Cons: partial liquidity (not a full cash-out); ongoing involvement expected; PE sponsor timeline considerations
- This is the Gen4/SGA model.

**Comparison table:** All four options side-by-side across: cash at close, ongoing equity, clinical autonomy, brand preservation, operational support, timeline, and ideal candidate profile.
