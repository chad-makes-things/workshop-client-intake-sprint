### H1

How to Evaluate a DSO Partnership: A Practice Owner's Checklist

### Outline

**H2: Why Evaluating a DSO Matters More Than Ever**
- DSO market in 2026: more options, more noise, harder to tell the difference
- The "7 out of 10 frustrated or regretful" stat and what drives it
- Why due diligence on a DSO partnership is as important as due diligence on any business deal
- Transition to the framework

**H2: The 12-Point DSO Evaluation Checklist**

*H3: 1. Clinical Autonomy Protections*
- Does the DSO contractually guarantee clinical independence?
- Who controls treatment planning, materials, labs, and scheduling?
- Ask for specific contractual language, not just marketing claims
- Red flag: "We don't dictate clinical decisions" with no written protection

*H3: 2. Equity Model and Deal Structure*
- Outright acquisition vs. JV equity partnership vs. management services
- What percentage of equity do you retain? In what entity?
- How is ongoing equity valued? What triggers liquidity events?
- Key question: "Do I have equity in the individual practice or the platform?"

*H3: 3. Private Equity Sponsor Track Record*
- How long has the PE firm been in dental? Healthcare?
- How many platforms have they built? What were the outcomes for partners?
- What is the planned hold period? What happens at exit?
- Key question: "Can I speak with partners from your PE firm's prior dental platforms?"

*H3: 4. Practice Brand and Identity Preservation*
- Will your practice name remain? Your logo, signage, community identity?
- What are you required to change?
- Ask for examples of existing practices that retained their names 3+ years post-partnership

*H3: 5. Staff Impact and Retention*
- What happens to your current team?
- Benefits upgrade or downgrade?
- What is the DSO's staff retention rate? (SGA: 85%)
- Who handles HR, payroll, and benefits administration post-partnership?

*H3: 6. Operational Support Scope*
- What back-office functions does the DSO actually handle?
- Billing, HR, compliance, marketing, recruiting, technology, procurement?
- What remains your responsibility?
- How quickly is support deployed after close?

*H3: 7. Continuing Education and Clinical Development*
- Is CE a stipend check or an actual development program?
- Access to mentorship, peer learning, advanced training?
- Key question: "What clinical training opportunities are available that I could not access on my own?"

*H3: 8. Peer Community and Culture*
- How do partners interact? Regular calls, events, advisory boards?
- Is the culture top-down management or peer collaboration?
- Ask to attend a partner event or sit in on a partner call before signing

*H3: 9. Technology Investment*
- What technology does the DSO provide? CBCT, intraoral scanners, digital workflows?
- Who pays for equipment upgrades?
- What practice management software is used?

*H3: 10. Growth Support and Marketing*
- Does the DSO invest in local marketing for your practice?
- Patient acquisition support, digital marketing, website?
- What growth targets are expected? Are they mandated or collaborative?

*H3: 11. Partner References*
- Can you speak with 3+ current partners?
- Ask for partners who have been in the network 2+ years, not just recent joins
- Ask partners: "What surprised you most about the first year?"

*H3: 12. Exit and Succession Provisions*
- What happens if you want to leave?
- What is the non-compete scope?
- What is the succession plan if you retire?

**H2: Red Flags That Should Make You Pause**
- Reluctance to share partner references
- Vague equity terms with no written documentation
- No named clinical leadership (only MBAs running the show)
- Aggressive timeline pressure ("this offer expires in 30 days")
- No current partners willing to speak on the record

**H2: How SGA Dental Partners Measures Up**
- Concise overview of SGA against the 12 criteria (not a hard sell -- evidence-based)
- Named partners willing to speak: Dr. Godat, Dr. Cavendish, others
- Thurston Group track record: 38 years, $4B+ returned, 8 platforms
- Pikos Institute + 4Front as education differentiators
- 85% staff retention rate as proof of culture

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**
- Q: How long does the DSO evaluation process typically take?
- Q: Should I hire an advisor to help evaluate DSO offers?
- Q: What is the difference between a DSO and a DPO?
- Q: How do I compare EBITDA multiples between DSO offers?

### H1

Dental Practice Valuation Guide 2026: What Your Practice Is Actually Worth

### Outline

**H2: Why Practice Valuations Matter in 2026**
- Record M&A activity in dental; more buyers competing for practices
- Valuation determines your negotiating position whether selling to a private buyer, broker, or DSO
- The gap between "what you think it's worth" and "what the market will pay"

**H2: Three Primary Valuation Methods**

*H3: EBITDA Multiple Method*
- What EBITDA means in a dental practice context
- Current market multiples: 6-12x depending on practice size, profitability, and market
- What drives premium multiples: geographic cluster value, growth trajectory, specialty mix, associate leverage
- Market ceiling: approximately 12x EBITDA in 2026 except in extraordinary cases

*H3: Revenue-Based Valuation*
- Revenue multiples: 150-300% of annual revenue for high performers
- When revenue-based is used vs. EBITDA-based
- Limitations: revenue without profitability overstates value

*H3: Asset-Based Valuation*
- Equipment, real estate, patient records, goodwill
- Most common in smaller practices or retirement sales
- Typically yields the lowest valuation of the three methods

**H2: What Drives a Premium Valuation**

*H3: Practice Size and Profitability*
- Larger practices command higher multiples (scale premium)
- EBITDA margins matter more than top-line revenue
- Multi-location practices: premium for operational complexity already solved

*H3: Geographic Value*
- DSOs pay premiums for practices that fill gaps in existing geographic clusters
- Underserved markets vs. saturated urban markets
- SGA example: Southeast density creates cluster value for practices in GA, FL, AL, MS, TN

*H3: Clinical Team and Associate Leverage*
- Practices dependent on the owner-dentist are riskier (and valued lower)
- Practices with productive associates and hygienists command premiums
- Succession readiness: can the practice operate without you for 30 days?

*H3: Technology and Facility*
- Modern equipment reduces capital investment needed post-acquisition
- Digital workflows, CBCT, intraoral scanners signal forward-thinking practice
- Facility condition and lease terms factor into valuation

*H3: Growth Trajectory*
- Practices with growing revenue and patient counts are worth more
- Stagnant or declining practices face valuation discounts
- Referral patterns and new patient flow matter

**H2: DSO Partnership Valuation vs. Private Sale Valuation**
- DSOs often pay higher multiples than private buyers (access to capital)
- But DSO deals include equity retention, earn-outs, and ongoing involvement
- Net value comparison: cash at close vs. cash + equity + ongoing income
- Anchor against both extremes: outright sale (all cash, no upside) vs. management services (no cash, just fees)

**H2: Common Valuation Mistakes**
- Overvaluing goodwill without supporting data
- Ignoring lease terms and facility liabilities
- Failing to "normalize" owner compensation in EBITDA calculation
- Comparing multiples across different deal structures (apples to oranges)
- Relying on a single valuation source

**H2: How to Get Your Practice Valued**
- Step 1: Gather financial records (3 years of tax returns, P&Ls, production reports)
- Step 2: Engage a dental-specific advisor or CPA
- Step 3: Get multiple perspectives (broker, DSO, independent appraiser)
- SGA's approach: three-step process (Connect, Engage, Partner) with honest, transparent valuation

**H2: Frequently Asked Questions**
- Q: What is the average EBITDA multiple for a dental practice in 2026?
- Q: How long does a dental practice valuation take?
- Q: Should I improve my practice before getting a valuation?
- Q: How do DSO valuations compare to private buyer offers?
- Q: What is "seller's discretionary earnings" vs. EBITDA?
