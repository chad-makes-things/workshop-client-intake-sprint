## FAQ Section

**Section Header:** What Early-Career Dentists Ask Us (And the Real Answers)

**Q1: How is SGA different from the corporate DSO I am already at? DSO is DSO.**
We understand the skepticism. You have been burned. Here is the structural difference: your current DSO hired you to produce. SGA pairs you with a mentor through the Individual Growth Engine, gives you Pikos Institute access for world-class implant training, offers Class A shareholder equity from day one, and measures your success by clinical growth -- not just collections. No corporate DSO offers an integrated education platform, 1:1 mentorship, and same-day equity eligibility. Those are not promises on a job posting. They are the architecture of the program.

**Q2: What does "Class A shareholder from day one" actually mean? Is it real?**
It is real and it is specific. You are eligible to invest in SGA as a Class A shareholder from your hire date. This gives you an ownership stake in the broader SGA platform -- not just the practice you work in. As the platform grows (100+ practices, Thurston Group backing, three verticals), your equity appreciates. This is the same model that has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 Thurston Group platforms. We provide written documentation of the equity structure, and existing associates are available as references.

**Q3: Will I actually do complex cases or is this just more bread-and-butter production?**
SGA's Individual Growth Engine exists specifically to expand your clinical scope. You are paired with a mentor who performs the procedures you want to learn -- implants, full-arch, complex restorative. The Pikos Institute gives you hands-on surgical training from Dr. Michael Pikos. And SGA practices are structured to support mentored complex cases, not just high-volume bread-and-butter. One of our associates went from Class IIs to implant cases within his first 18 months.

**Q4: Most SGA locations are in smaller towns. I am not sure I want to move to Hinesville or Hazelhurst.**
Some of our locations are in smaller communities, and some are in metro areas across Arizona, California, Michigan, Minnesota, Texas, Nevada, and Florida. We match associates with locations that fit their lifestyle preferences. Many associates who initially hesitated about smaller markets discovered something unexpected: lower cost of living, stronger community connection, higher earning potential (less competition), and a pace of life that actually lets you enjoy the career you are building. That said, we will never pressure you into a location that does not work for you.

**Q5: What does the mentorship actually look like on a daily basis?**
Your mentor is an experienced SGA partner clinician -- not a corporate trainer, not a regional manager. The relationship includes regular case review, chair-side observation for complex procedures, treatment planning collaboration, and career development conversations. The cadence is tailored to your needs: some associates meet weekly, others work alongside their mentor daily during their first months. Dr. Cavendish described the experience: "To be able to do a complex case with somebody who is also working at a very high skill level -- it is fun."

**Q6: What benefits does SGA offer from day one?**
Full benefits package from your start date: 401(k) with employer match through Voya, Cigna health insurance, PTO, CE stipend, and Pikos Institute/4Front Symposium access. No probation period. No waiting period for benefits. You also receive a signing bonus ($15,000-$30,000) and relocation assistance ($5,000-$10,000) for applicable positions.

**Q7: How much will I actually earn?**
Base salary ranges from $160,000 to $240,000 depending on experience, plus a 28-32% production bonus on collections above a defined threshold. With the equity pathway, Pikos Institute access ($6,500-$12,000/year value), clinical mentorship ($12,000-$24,000/year value), and full benefits, the total compensation package is $37,000-$70,000 above what a comparable corporate DSO position offers. SGA is competitive on base pay and dominant on career development value.

**Q8: Can I really build toward partnership or is this just a recruiting line?**
We provide a written career pathway document that maps specific milestones for years 1, 3, 5, and 7. Equity participation begins from day one (Class A shares). The pathway is not vague -- it includes clinical skill progression, production expectations, equity accumulation benchmarks, and leadership development milestones. Named associates who have progressed through this pathway are available as references.

**Q9: What if SGA is not what was promised during the first 90 days?**
SGA offers a 90-day mutual evaluation period. If the experience does not match what was communicated during recruiting, you can exit with no financial penalty. Signing bonuses are structured with a reasonable vesting schedule -- not a punitive clawback. We also conduct 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day check-in surveys. Spencer Pierce, VP of People and Culture, describes the onboarding experience: "After onboarding is completed, there is an abundance of relief."

**Q10: I have $340K in student debt. How does the equity pathway help with that?**
The equity pathway does not replace debt payments -- but it builds a parallel wealth-creation vehicle that a salary-only position cannot. While you pay down loans, your Class A shareholder position appreciates as the SGA platform grows. Over a 5-7 year horizon, the combination of competitive salary, production bonuses, and equity participation creates a financial trajectory that pure W-2 income at a corporate DSO cannot match. The Thurston Group's $4B+ returned to partners across 8 platforms demonstrates that the equity model generates real returns.

**Q11: What is the patient volume like? Will I have enough patients from day one?**
SGA provides a guaranteed minimum patient base supported by centralized marketing and scheduling optimization. You will not sit in an empty chair. The volume is designed to be clinically appropriate -- enough to build momentum and earn strong production bonuses without the 25-30 patient-per-day grind that characterizes corporate DSO schedules. Your scheduling reflects clinical judgment, not a template imposed from corporate.
