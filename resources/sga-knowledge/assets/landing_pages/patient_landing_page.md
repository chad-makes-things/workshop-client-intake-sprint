## FAQ Section

**Section Header:** Your Questions, Answered Honestly

**Q1: How much do dental implants cost?**
Full-mouth dental implants (both arches) range from $40,000 to $55,000 depending on the complexity of your case. Single implants start at $3,500. All-on-4 (per arch) ranges from $22,000 to $30,000. All of our pricing is all-inclusive -- surgery, prosthetics, follow-up, and aftercare. No hidden fees. We provide your exact treatment cost before you commit to anything.

**Q2: Can I really get new teeth in one day?**
Yes. In many cases, implants are placed and temporary prosthetics are fitted in a single visit. You walk in with failing teeth or dentures and walk out the same day with fixed teeth that function immediately. Final permanent prosthetics are placed after healing (typically 3-6 months), but you have functional, natural-looking teeth from day one.

**Q3: I am terrified of the surgery. What should I expect?**
You are not the first person to feel this way. Most of our patients share this concern before their procedure. Sedation options are available to keep you comfortable throughout. Your surgical team is Pikos Institute-trained -- the gold standard in implantology -- which means precise, efficient, and predictable procedures. We provide a step-by-step walkthrough before your appointment so there are no surprises. Janice was 87 when she had her implants placed. She said it was "life changing." You can do this.

**Q4: I have been told I do not have enough bone for implants. Am I still a candidate?**
Possibly yes. Our team is trained in advanced techniques including zygomatic implants, which anchor in the cheekbone rather than the jawbone. These techniques were developed for patients who have been told they are "not candidates" by other providers. A free virtual consultation with our team can determine your options. Many patients who were turned away elsewhere have been successfully treated here.

**Q5: How do I afford this on a fixed income?**
We understand this is a significant investment. That is why we offer multiple financing options: 0% interest for 12-24 months through CareCredit and Proceed Finance (with qualifying credit), extended financing at competitive rates, in-house payment plans for deposits, and insurance coordination to maximize any existing benefits. Monthly payments can start as low as $199 depending on your treatment plan. Many patients tell us the monthly payment is less than what they were spending on denture adhesive, replacement dentures, and the foods they had to avoid.

**Q6: I live far from Springfield / the practice. How do I manage travel?**
Many of our patients travel from out of state. We minimize trips through careful scheduling -- your consultation can happen virtually from home, and the surgical procedure is typically completed in a single visit. We provide hotel recommendations, coordinate appointment scheduling to minimize travel days, and offer virtual follow-up appointments when appropriate. If something feels wrong after surgery, our team is available by phone and text -- even at 2 AM.

**Q7: What is the difference between All-on-4 and snap-in dentures?**
All-on-4 gives you permanent, fixed teeth that function like natural teeth. They do not come out. You brush them like regular teeth. Snap-in dentures are removable but click into implants for stability -- a significant upgrade from traditional dentures but not as permanent as All-on-4. Snap-in dentures cost less and can be a good option if full All-on-4 is not in your budget right now. Your consultation will help you determine which option fits your situation and goals.

**Q8: How long do dental implants last?**
The implants themselves -- the titanium posts placed in your jawbone -- are designed to last a lifetime. We back them with a lifetime warranty on the implant hardware. The prosthetic components (the visible teeth) carry a 5-10 year warranty with replacement options. With proper care, many patients keep the same prosthetics for 15-20 years or longer.

**Q9: Will my new teeth look natural?**
Yes. Your prosthetics are custom-designed using digital technology to match your facial structure, skin tone, and desired aesthetic. Dr. Olson offers a digital smile preview so you can see what your new teeth will look like before committing to treatment. Patients consistently describe their new teeth as looking and feeling "totally natural."

**Q10: I have been researching for months and getting wildly different quotes. How do I know who to trust?**
This is one of the most common frustrations we hear. Price opacity in the implant space is a real problem. Here is how we approach it differently: (1) All-inclusive pricing -- your quote covers everything, no surprise bills. (2) We publish our price ranges publicly (see above). (3) Dr. Olson has built his practice on transparency -- 138,000 YouTube subscribers trust him specifically because he answers the questions other providers avoid. (4) 3,390+ five-star reviews from patients who went through this exact decision. (5) Your virtual consultation is free -- you learn your options and exact pricing before committing to anything.

**Q11: What is the Pikos Institute and why does it matter to me?**
The Pikos Institute is the world's premier implantology training center, founded by Dr. Michael A. Pikos -- a globally recognized pioneer in dental implant surgery. When we say your surgical team is "Pikos Institute-trained," it means they have been trained by one of the best in the world. For you, this means more precise procedures, better outcomes, and the ability to handle complex cases that other providers may not be equipped for. It is the difference between a general mechanic and a Formula 1 engineer working on your car.

**Q12: What happens after my surgery? What is the recovery like?**
Most patients return to normal activities within 2-3 days. You will receive a detailed recovery guide, dietary recommendations (soft foods for the first few weeks), and a direct phone/text line to our clinical team. If something feels wrong at 2 AM, you text that number and get a response. Follow-up visits are included in your treatment fee. Your final permanent prosthetics are typically placed 3-6 months after surgery, once healing is complete.
