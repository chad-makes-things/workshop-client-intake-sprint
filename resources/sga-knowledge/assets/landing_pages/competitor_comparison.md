## Honest Assessment: Where Competitors May Be Better

**Section Header:** Where We Are Honest About What Others Offer

We believe in transparency. Here is where certain competitors may have advantages over SGA:

### Scale and Geographic Reach
Heartland (1,800+ practices in 38 states), Aspen (1,300+ in 48 states), PDS (1,000+ in 24 states), and MB2 (800+ in 41 states) all have larger footprints than SGA today. If your primary criterion is being part of the biggest network, SGA is not the largest. We are growing -- and deliberately. We would rather have 100 practices that fit our culture than 800 that dilute it.

### National Brand Recognition (Patient Acquisition)
Aspen Dental is the most recognized consumer dental brand in the US. If you want patients who specifically search for a national brand name, Aspen provides that. SGA's model keeps your local brand, which means patient acquisition depends on your practice's reputation and SGA's centralized marketing support -- not a national TV campaign. For most practice owners, that is a feature, not a bug. But it is worth knowing.

### Pure Practice Ownership (No Equity Dilution)
PDS Health's owner-clinician model is the only major DSO where the dentist retains 100% ownership of their individual practice. If you want full ownership with management support and no equity sale, PDS is structurally designed for that. The tradeoff: you get no equity in a larger platform, no "second bite of the apple," and no upside beyond your own practice's performance. For some dentists, that tradeoff is worth it.

### Sheer Volume of Support Staff
Heartland's 1,800-practice infrastructure means deeper bench strength in procurement, HR, legal, and technology. A single-location practice at Heartland has access to a support machine that SGA's 100-practice network cannot yet fully match in every functional area. However, we would argue that having 100+ peers who know your name is more valuable than having 1,800 practices where you are a number.

**Why we share this:** Because trust is built on honesty, not on pretending competitors have no strengths. If a competitor is genuinely better for your situation, we would rather you know that now than discover it after signing.

## FAQ Section

**Section Header:** "How Is SGA Different?" -- The Questions You Are Already Asking

**Q1: Every DSO says "clinical autonomy." How is SGA's claim any different?**
Every DSO claims it. We agree -- the phrase has become meaningless. Here is what makes SGA's version structural rather than aspirational: (1) Clinical autonomy is contractual, written into the partnership agreement. (2) Our CCO, Dr. Mitch Ellingson, is a practicing dentist who personally experienced the private-to-group transition. His job is protecting clinical independence. (3) SGA uses a "dentists teach dentists" model -- clinical direction comes from the clinical advisory board (practicing partners), not corporate managers. (4) Our Glassdoor and partner testimonials are consistent with the claim, unlike competitors where employee reviews tell a different story. We encourage you to read Heartland's Indeed reviews alongside our partner testimonials and draw your own conclusions.

**Q2: MB2 calls themselves a "DPO, not a DSO." Is that a real difference?**
"DPO" (Dental Partnership Organization) is a marketing term, not a legal or structural designation. MB2's model is functionally similar to SGA's -- both use JV equity partnerships where dentists retain ownership. The actual differences are: (1) SGA has the Pikos Institute and 4Front Symposium; MB2 has no education platform. (2) SGA is backed by Thurston Group with a disclosed, verifiable 38-year track record; MB2's PE backers are undisclosed. (3) SGA offers three specialty verticals; MB2 is general dentistry only. The name on the category matters less than what is inside it.

**Q3: Heartland has 1,800 practices. Isn't bigger better?**
Bigger means more purchasing power, more support staff, and more geographic options. On those dimensions, Heartland has an advantage. But bigger also means less personal attention, more standardized processes, and a higher likelihood of feeling like a number. Heartland employee reviews on Indeed and Glassdoor repeatedly cite manager override of clinical decisions, production pressure, and a corporate culture that does not match the marketing. SGA's 100+ practice network is large enough for operational scale and small enough that our CCO knows partners by name. The question is whether you want to be one of 1,800 or one of 100.

**Q4: PDS lets me keep 100% ownership. Why would I give up equity to SGA?**
PDS's owner-clinician model is genuinely differentiated -- you own your practice and PDS charges management fees. The tradeoff: you get no equity in a larger platform. Your financial upside is limited to your single practice's performance. SGA's JV model gives you a liquidity event (60-70% upfront), ongoing equity in a multi-state platform, and the "second bite of the apple" as the combined entity grows. Thurston has returned $4B+ across 8 platforms through this model. For dentists who want both a liquidity event and long-term upside, SGA's model is structurally superior. For dentists who want zero equity dilution and are willing to forgo platform upside, PDS is the better fit.

**Q5: Aspen Dental has the most brand recognition with patients. Does that matter?**
It depends on your practice. If you are starting from scratch and need national brand recognition to fill chairs, Aspen's model works. But you are not starting from scratch. You have an established practice with a name your community knows and trusts. SGA preserves that name. Your patients chose Dr. Hendricks, not a logo. The question is whether you want to replace your brand with theirs or keep yours and add operational support behind it. Also worth noting: Aspen Dental has 1.2 stars from 311 reviews on Sitejabber and has faced multiple state attorney general investigations. National brand recognition is not always positive recognition.

**Q6: Guardian Dentistry is also in the Southeast and claims to be "partner, not proprietor." How is SGA different?**
Guardian has strong messaging and direct geographic overlap with SGA in the Southeast. The structural differences: (1) Guardian is family office-backed with limited scale capital; SGA is backed by Thurston Group with a $350M credit facility. (2) Guardian has no education platform; SGA has the Pikos Institute and 4Front Symposium. (3) Guardian operates one model (general dentistry network); SGA offers three verticals including specialty and implantology. (4) Guardian's track record is 7+ years; Thurston's is 38 years across 8 platforms. Guardian is a credible competitor with good values. SGA offers a structurally deeper partnership.

**Q7: What about SALT Dental Partners for specialty practices?**
SALT is the most credible specialty-focused competitor. They serve pediatric dentists, orthodontists, and oral surgeons specifically. If your practice is purely pediatric or orthodontic, SALT is worth evaluating. For periodontists, oral surgeons, and implantologists, SGA's MODIS vertical offers something SALT does not: the Pikos Institute, Dr. Michael Pikos as a founding partner, capital investment in surgical infrastructure ($250K-$750K), and an integrated referral network between general SGA practices and MODIS specialists. **Q9: What if SGA gets sold to a bigger DSO? Does my equity protect me?**
This is a legitimate concern and one we address directly. The Thurston Group has been in business for 38 years. Their model is long-term compounding, not quick flips. However, every PE firm eventually has exit events. In a Thurston exit, partners historically benefit through equity appreciation -- the "second bite of the apple." The $4B+ returned to partners across 8 platforms represents real exit proceeds paid to real people.
