## The Second Bite of the Apple

**Section Header:** Why the Equity Model Matters More Than the Multiple

Most practice owners fixate on the EBITDA multiple. We understand why. It is the clearest number in the conversation.

But here is what the multiple misses:

**Scenario A: Outright sale (Heartland / DCA model)**
- You sell your practice for 8x EBITDA
- You receive 100% at close
- Your involvement with the platform's growth ends that day
- If the DSO doubles in size over the next 5 years, you do not participate

**Scenario B: JV equity partnership (SGA model)**
- You sell your practice for 7-9x EBITDA
- You receive 60-70% at close (meaningful liquidity event)
- You retain 30-40% equity in the combined SGA platform
- As SGA grows -- acquiring more practices, driving organic revenue, investing in technology and education -- your equity appreciates
- When a future liquidity event occurs (Thurston has done this 8 times), your retained equity converts to additional cash. This is the "second bite of the apple."

**Thurston Group has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 platforms.** That $4B includes both the first bite (upfront liquidity) and the second bite (equity appreciation over the hold period). Partners who stayed invested in the platform compounded their returns beyond the initial transaction.

**The math that matters:** A slightly lower upfront multiple with 30-40% equity in a growing platform can significantly outperform a higher upfront multiple with no ongoing equity. Your financial advisor can model this. We encourage that conversation -- with your advisor in the room, not ours.

## FAQ Section

**Section Header:** Partnership Economics: Your Questions Answered

**Q1: What EBITDA multiples does SGA typically pay?**
SGA operates in the 7-9x EBITDA range for community and group practices, with higher multiples (9-11x) for strategically significant or high-performing multi-location groups. Specialty practices typically fall in the 6-8x range but may command premiums for practices filling key vertical gaps. We are transparent about these ranges because we believe informed sellers make better partners. Your financial advisor is welcome at every stage of the valuation conversation.

**Q2: What percentage do I get upfront versus equity?**
Community practices typically receive 60-70% at close with 30-40% retained in SGA platform equity. Group practices may negotiate 55-65% upfront with 35-45% equity, reflecting the higher total deal value. These ratios are negotiable based on practice size, performance, strategic value, and the seller's liquidity needs. There is no one-size-fits-all formula.

**Q3: How does the equity actually appreciate? What drives it?**
Your equity in SGA appreciates as the combined platform grows in value. Value growth comes from: (1) new practice partnerships that expand the network, (2) organic revenue growth across existing practices, (3) operational efficiencies from centralized services, and (4) the strategic value of SGA's three-vertical architecture and education platform. The Thurston Group's $4B+ returned to partners across 8 platforms demonstrates this model at work -- the returns come from both the initial liquidity and the ongoing equity appreciation.

**Q4: What is the "second bite of the apple" and is it real?**
The "second bite" refers to the future value realization of your retained equity. When a future platform-level event occurs (capital event, refinancing, or eventual exit), your 30-40% equity position converts to additional cash -- often at a significantly higher valuation than when you initially partnered. This is how Thurston partners have historically generated returns that exceed the initial transaction value. It is real, it is the core of the model, and prior Thurston platform partners are available to speak about their experience.

**Q5: What does SGA's management fee cover? How much is it?**
SGA charges a management fee that covers all centralized services: billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing, recruiting, technology, and CE/development. The fee is a percentage of practice revenue and is detailed transparently during the evaluation process. When compared to the $297K-$575K annual cost of hiring these services independently, the management fee represents significant value. Your financial advisor can evaluate the net economics.

**Q6: What happens if I want to retire in 3-5 years?**
This is one of the strongest use cases for an SGA partnership. You get a liquidity event now (diversifying your wealth while values are strong), you retain equity that continues appreciating, and you continue practicing as long as you want with full operational support. When you are ready to reduce hours, SGA has the recruiting infrastructure to bring in your successor -- and the mentorship model to develop them. Your practice does not depend solely on you anymore. Your patients and staff are cared for. Your equity position continues generating returns. This is the succession plan.

**Q7: Can I negotiate the deal terms?**
Yes. Every partnership is individually structured based on practice size, performance, market position, strategic value, and the seller's priorities. The ranges on this page are guidelines, not fixed offers. Bring your attorney and financial advisor. We expect that and encourage it. The goal is a deal structure where both parties have strong incentive alignment for the long term.

**Q8: How long from first conversation to close?**
Typically 4-8 months. The process follows three stages: Connect (introductory conversation, 2-4 weeks), Engage (detailed evaluation with financial and operational diligence, 8-16 weeks), and Partner (valuation agreement, legal documentation, closing, 4-8 weeks). SGA does not rush. This is a career-defining decision.

**Q9: What do my staff get out of this? Will their benefits change?**
Benefits are upgraded, not downgraded. Your team receives: Cigna health insurance, Mutual of Omaha supplemental benefits, 401(k) through Voya with employer match, PTO, paid holidays, and CE stipends. All existing staff are retained through the transition. The Practice Management Pathways Program provides career development for non-clinical team members. SGA's 85% staff retention rate reflects a genuine commitment to the people inside your practice. Your office manager is not losing her role -- she is getting a team behind her.

**Q10: What if I am not happy with the partnership after the first year?**
We design partnerships for the long term, but we also build in mutual accountability. The partnership agreement defines clear expectations for both parties -- what SGA delivers and what the partner contributes. If support is not materializing as promised, the contract provides recourse. That said, the best risk reversal is talking to current partners. Ask Dr. Godat, Dr. Cavendish, or anyone in the network whether the first year matched expectations. Their answers are our strongest assurance.

**Q11: What makes SGA's equity model different from MB2's partial equity model?**
Both SGA and MB2 use JV equity structures. The differences: (1) SGA's equity is in the combined platform -- your value grows with all 100+ practices and three verticals, not just your individual practice. (2) SGA is backed by Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+ returned, 8 platforms, disclosed track record). MB2's PE backers are undisclosed. (3) SGA offers the Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium, and Individual Growth Engine as part of the partnership. MB2 has no education platform. (4) SGA provides named references from current and prior Thurston platform partners. The equity structure may look similar on paper. The track record, education, and transparency are not.

**Q12: I have heard DSOs manipulate EBITDA calculations to pay less. How does SGA handle valuation?**
This is a legitimate concern. The industry has earned it. SGA uses transparent, standard valuation methodology based on normalized EBITDA. We walk through every adjustment with you and your financial advisor. There are no hidden management fees deducted before calculating your multiple. No manipulation of carried notes to deflate the effective multiple. Your advisory team reviews the math independently. If the numbers do not work for you, we part as professionals. Thurston Group's reputation across 38 years and 8 platforms depends on fair dealing. That is not platitude -- it is self-interest. Partners talk. The dental world is small.

**Q13: What happens to my commercial lease?**
Lease arrangements are addressed individually during the evaluation process. Common structures include: SGA assumes the lease directly, SGA enters a sublease arrangement, or (in cases where the seller owns the real estate) a separate real estate purchase or lease agreement. Your real estate situation is factored into the overall partnership structure. If you own your building, that can be an additional value component.
