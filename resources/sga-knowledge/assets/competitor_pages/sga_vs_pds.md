## The Ownership Model: The Critical Distinction

This is where PDS and SGA diverge most sharply. Both claim "owner-dentist" positioning, but the mechanics are fundamentally different.

### PDS: You Own Your Practice, PDS Manages It

Under PDS's model, the dentist owns their individual practice. PDS does not buy it. Instead, PDS provides management services -- billing, HR, compliance, marketing, technology -- for a management fee. The dentist retains ownership, operational control, and clinical autonomy over their specific practice.

**What this means financially:**
- No upfront liquidity event. You do not sell your practice to PDS.
- Your financial upside is tied to your individual practice's performance.
- PDS charges a management fee out of practice revenue.
- You do not own equity in PDS as a company. Your asset is one practice.

**What this means practically:**
- You are genuinely an owner-operator. The practice is yours.
- But your wealth is concentrated in a single asset -- your practice.
- You pay for support rather than receiving investment.

### SGA: You Sell a Portion, Retain Equity in the Platform

Under SGA's model, the dentist sells a portion of their practice equity to SGA and retains ownership in the combined platform.

**What this means financially:**
- Upfront liquidity event: 60-70% of agreed valuation paid at close.
- Retained equity: 30-40% rolled into the SGA platform.
- Your financial upside is tied to the entire SGA platform's growth -- not just your individual practice.
- No management fee. SGA's operational costs come from the partnership structure.

**What this means practically:**
- You get a life-changing cash event while staying clinically active.
- Your wealth is diversified across a multi-practice platform.
- You participate in the appreciation created by every practice in the SGA network.

### The Financial Framing

Think of it this way:

- **PDS:** You own 100% of one practice and pay fees for support.
- **SGA:** You own equity in a platform of 60-125+ practices, received significant cash at close, and your equity grows as the platform grows.

PDS is a management arrangement. SGA is an investment partnership. PDS charges you for support. SGA makes you a co-owner of something larger.

Neither model is inherently superior. But the financial trajectories are different, and that difference compounds over years.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How is PDS's "Owner Clinician" model different from SGA's equity partnership?

At PDS, you own your individual practice and pay PDS a management fee for back-office support. You do not sell any portion of your practice, and you do not receive equity in PDS. At SGA, you sell a portion of your practice (receiving 60-70% in cash at close) and retain equity in the combined SGA platform. PDS is a management arrangement. SGA is an investment partnership with shared ownership.

### Does PDS offer a liquidity event?

No. PDS's model does not involve purchasing your practice. Your wealth remains in your individual practice. SGA provides a significant upfront liquidity event (60-70% of practice valuation in cash) plus ongoing equity in the growing platform.

### Which model creates more long-term wealth?

It depends on the assumptions. If your individual practice is a high-growth outlier and you retain 100% ownership (PDS model), your single-practice wealth could exceed a diversified platform stake. But most practices are not high-growth outliers. SGA's platform equity model diversifies your financial outcome across 60-125+ practices, and the Thurston Group has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 platforms using this approach. Diversification and platform-level compounding generally favor the equity partnership model over a 5-10 year horizon.

### Can I access the Pikos Institute through PDS?

No. The Pikos Institute is part of the MODIS/SGA ecosystem. PDS offers continuing education programs, but nothing comparable to a world-class implantology training institute with Dr. Michael Pikos as a founding partner. Any dentist can attend Pikos Institute courses independently, but SGA partners receive integrated access as part of the platform.

### What about PDS's dental-medical integration?

PDS's Mouth-Body Connection -- integrating blood pressure screening, A1C testing, and disease detection into dental visits -- is genuinely innovative and not available in SGA's model. If dental-medical integration is central to your clinical vision, PDS is the only DSO building this at scale. SGA's clinical innovation focus is on implantology education and peer collaboration rather than medical integration.

### Is PDS a better choice because it is not PE-backed?

PDS is founder-led with minority PE investment, which some dentists find reassuring. However, "PE-backed" is not inherently negative -- it depends on the PE firm. The Thurston Group has a 38-year track record specifically in dental and healthcare, with $4B+ returned to partners. They are not a first-time fund experimenting. The question is not "is there PE involvement?" but "who is the PE partner and what is their track record?"
