## The "Dentist-Founded" Question

Both MB2 and SGA lean into "founded by dentists" as a trust signal. This deserves honest treatment.

**MB2:** Founded in 2007 by Dr. Reza Khorashahi (known as "Dr. K") and a group of dentists in Texas. The founding story is genuine -- dentists built MB2 because they wanted an alternative to corporate DSOs. MB2 has grown from that founding group to 800+ practices in 41 states. The "dentist-founded" DNA is authentic.

**SGA/Gen4:** Gen4 Dental Partners was co-founded by Dr. Mitch Ellingson, a practicing dentist, alongside CEO Lamonte Jensen. Dr. Ellingson serves as Chief Clinical Officer and remains clinically active. The MODIS vertical was built around Dr. Michael Pikos, a pioneer in implantology. The "clinician-led" claim is structural -- Dr. Ellingson sits in the C-suite, and Dr. Pikos is a founding partner.

**The honest assessment:** Both organizations have legitimate "founded by dentists" credentials. The question is not who was founded by a dentist. It is what that founding ethos looks like today at scale. Does the clinician voice still shape decisions, or has it become a marketing artifact?

At MB2, with 800+ practices across 41 states, the founding dentist's vision must be filtered through a massive operational layer. At SGA, with a smaller but growing footprint, Dr. Ellingson still makes clinical advisory decisions and Dr. Pikos still teaches at the institute that bears his name. Scale changes the proximity between founding vision and daily reality. This is not a criticism of MB2 -- it is a structural observation about what happens at 800 practices versus 125.

## Who Is MB2 Dental?

MB2 Dental was founded in 2007 and has grown into the self-described "largest Dental Partnership Organization (DPO)" in the United States. With 800+ practices across 41 states (milestone reached September 2025) and 1,700+ dentists in its network, MB2 has experienced rapid growth.

MB2's positioning is explicitly anti-DSO. They coined "DPO" (Dental Partnership Organization) to create categorical distance from traditional dental support organizations. The tagline is "Power to the Partners." The model: partners sell a portion of their practice equity, retain significant ownership and clinical control, and access MB2's 500+ person support team.

MB2 also operates the Carabelli Club -- an affiliate and networking program with 275+ members in its first year -- designed as an on-ramp for dentists not yet ready to partner.

## Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners consolidates three Thurston Group-backed entities:

- **SGA (original):** Community dentistry network across the Southeast
- **Gen4 Dental Partners:** Collaborative general and specialty practices nationally
- **MODIS Dental Partners:** Implantology and specialty vertical, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos and the Pikos Institute

The model is a joint-venture equity partnership: selling dentists receive 60-70% upfront liquidity while retaining 30-40% as equity in the combined SGA platform. Clinical autonomy is contractual. Practices retain their names, teams, and cultures. The Thurston Group has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 dental and healthcare platforms over 38 years.

## Deal Structure Comparison

### MB2 Dental

MB2's partial equity model allows partners to sell a portion of their practice equity while retaining significant ownership and clinical control. MB2 invests alongside partners. The minimum practice size for partnership is $1.25M in revenue with 4+ operatories.

MB2 does not publicly disclose specific deal multiples, equity split percentages, or the identity of its financial sponsors. The ownership structure behind MB2 is not transparently documented in public materials.

### SGA Dental Partners

SGA's JV equity model follows a documented structure:

- **60-70% upfront liquidity** at close
- **30-40% retained as equity** in the combined SGA platform
- **Ongoing clinical compensation** (base + production bonus)
- **Platform equity appreciation** as SGA grows

The financial sponsor is named: the Thurston Group, founded by Patrick J. Haynes III, with a 38-year track record, $4B+ returned to partners, and 8 dental/healthcare platforms built (Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management, SGA, Gen4, MODIS, ARC Health, US Orthopaedic Partners).

### The Transparency Difference

This is one of the sharpest distinctions between the two organizations. SGA's financial backing is completely transparent -- named firm, named principals, verifiable returns across multiple platforms. MB2's financial backing is opaque.

Why does this matter? Because the entity behind the partnership determines what happens during hold periods, at recapitalization events, and at eventual exits. When dentists report regret after DSO sales, the financial structure is often the source: "The money, they have a clawback or they had some type of provision that they did not know about, or the equity did not turn out to be what they thought."

Knowing who is behind the platform -- and what their track record looks like -- is not a nice-to-have. It is due diligence.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between a DSO and a DPO?

The honest answer: the structural differences are minimal. Both DSOs and DPOs (as MB2 defines them) can operate JV equity partnerships where dentists retain ownership and clinical autonomy. "DPO" is primarily a branding distinction MB2 created to differentiate from the negative connotations of "DSO." SGA acknowledges the DSO label while redefining it through the "4th generation" model -- arguing that the issue is not what you call the organization, but how it actually operates.

### Who is behind MB2 Dental's financing?

MB2's financial sponsors and ownership structure are not publicly identified. This is not unusual for private companies, but it contrasts with SGA's fully transparent backing by the Thurston Group -- a firm with named principals, a 38-year history, and $4B+ in verifiable returns.

### Does MB2 have an education or clinical development program?

MB2 does not operate a proprietary clinical institute, a structured peer-collaboration symposium, or a formal 1:1 mentorship program comparable to SGA's offerings. MB2 provides business support (HR, accounting, finance, procurement, marketing, recruiting) and fosters community among its 1,700+ dentists, but clinical education is not positioned as a core differentiator.

### I have been told MB2 and SGA are essentially the same model. Are they?

The financial structures are similar -- both involve partial equity sales with retained ownership. But the experience after close is different. SGA provides an integrated education platform (Pikos Institute, 4Front, Individual Growth Engine), three clinical verticals, and a named PE sponsor with 38 years of verifiable dental track record. MB2 provides a large peer network and business support. The mechanics of the deal may look similar on paper. The post-deal reality is where they diverge.

### What is MB2's Carabelli Club?

The Carabelli Club is MB2's affiliate and networking program for dentists who are interested in partnership but not yet ready to commit. It had 275+ members in its first year. It is a smart on-ramp that SGA does not currently replicate. SGA's equivalent entry point is a confidential introductory conversation -- a more traditional approach but without the formal community structure.

### Which is better for a mid-career practice owner like me?

If you are a mid-career practice owner -- say, 15-25 years in, doing $1.5M-$3M in revenue -- both MB2 and SGA are viable options. The deciding factors are:

1. **Do you want clinical growth or purely operational support?** Clinical growth: SGA. Operational support: either.
2. **Do you care who is behind the PE investment?** Transparency: SGA. Comfortable with opacity: MB2 is fine.
3. **Do you have specialty ambitions?** Implantology/specialty: SGA. General dentistry: either.
4. **Does network size matter more than network depth?** Size: MB2. Depth of engagement: SGA.

The best approach is to talk to partners at both organizations. Ask them what their experience has been 2-3 years after close. That is where the truth lives.
