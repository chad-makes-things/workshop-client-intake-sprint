## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best DSO to partner with?

There is no single best DSO. The right organization depends on your specialty, your financial goals, your clinical ambitions, and your tolerance for different ownership structures. A mid-career general dentist wanting equity and clinical growth might thrive at SGA. A new graduate wanting patient volume might do well at Aspen. A periodontist wanting specialty infrastructure belongs at SGA/MODIS. A pediatric dentist belongs at SALT. This guide is designed to help you match your priorities to the right organization.

### Are all DSOs essentially the same?

No. The deal structures, equity models, education platforms, autonomy protections, and PE sponsors vary significantly. The surface language is similar ("clinical autonomy," "operational support," "brand preservation"), but the mechanics underneath are different. The equity difference alone -- between outright acquisition (Heartland), management services (PDS), JV equity (SGA/MB2), and franchise-like (Aspen) -- changes your financial outcome over a decade.

### How do I evaluate a DSO beyond what is on their website?

Three steps: (1) Ask to speak with 3-5 current partners who have been in the organization for 2+ years. Ask them what changed, what stayed the same, and what they wish they had known. (2) Ask about the PE sponsor: Who are they, what is their track record, what is the typical hold period, and what happens at recapitalization? (3) Have your own attorney and financial advisor review the deal terms independently. Never rely solely on the organization's representations.

### What EBITDA multiples are dental practices selling for in 2026?

Mid-market dental practice EBITDA multiples generally range from 6-9x, with high-performing multi-location groups reaching 9-12x. The market ceiling is approximately 12x except in extraordinary cases. Regional cluster premiums of 0.5-1.5x above category may apply when a practice fills a strategic geographic gap for the DSO.

### Should I be concerned about private equity in dentistry?

PE involvement is not inherently good or bad. What matters is the specific PE firm's track record, investment horizon, and alignment with your goals. The Thurston Group (SGA's sponsor) has a 38-year history with $4B+ returned across 8 dental/healthcare platforms -- a verifiable track record of long-term value creation. KKR (Heartland) is a global giant but new to dental. Some PE firms have shorter horizons and more aggressive strategies. Evaluate the specific sponsor, not the category.

### Can I switch DSOs if I am unhappy?

This depends entirely on your contract terms. Most DSO partnerships involve equity sales that are not easily reversible. Before signing, understand: (1) What are the exit provisions? (2) What happens to your equity if you leave? (3) Is there a non-compete? (4) What does the transition look like? These are questions for your attorney, reviewed before you sign.
