SALT focuses on pediatric dentistry, orthodontics, and oral surgery, with 156+ locations across 20+ states and 65+ affiliated brands. ## Why This Comparison Matters

Most DSOs are built for general dentistry. Specialists -- periodontists, oral surgeons, pediatric dentists, orthodontists, implantologists -- have historically been underserved by the DSO market. SALT and SGA are two of the few organizations that explicitly address specialty practitioners, but they do so from different angles and with different clinical focuses.

If you are a specialist evaluating DSO options, this comparison matters because it distinguishes between a pediatric/ortho-focused model (SALT) and an implantology/perio-focused model with broader general dentistry integration (SGA).

## Who Is SALT Dental Partners?

SALT Dental Partners was founded in 2018 and positions itself as "the premier DSO for top-tier pediatric dentists, orthodontists, and oral surgeons." With 156+ practice locations across 20+ states (including DC), 65+ affiliated practice brands, and 233+ doctors, SALT has grown rapidly -- claiming 300% geographic expansion in recent trajectory.

SALT's tagline is "Enhancing Practices. Preserving Brands." The model: SALT acquires specialty practices, preserves their local brand identity and culture, and provides operational support. SALT also engages in de novo development, with 10 new locations planned in 2025.

SALT is doctor-led and targets "top-tier" practices -- signaling selectivity in its acquisitions. The organization has been recognized by Becker's as a "Top DSO to Know in 2025."

## Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners consolidates three Thurston Group-backed entities:

- **SGA (original):** Community dentistry across the Southeast (general and family dentistry)
- **Gen4 Dental Partners:** Collaborative general and specialty practices nationally
- **MODIS Dental Partners:** Implantology and specialty, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos (pioneer in dental implantology) and the Pikos Institute

SGA's deal structure is a joint-venture equity partnership: partners receive 60-70% upfront liquidity and retain 30-40% as equity in the combined SGA platform. Clinical autonomy is contractual. The Thurston Group has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 dental and healthcare platforms over 38 years.

## The Specialty Focus Difference

This is the core distinction. SALT and SGA serve different specialties with different infrastructures.

### SALT: Pediatric, Orthodontic, Oral Surgery

SALT was built from the ground up for pediatric dentists, orthodontists, and oral surgeons. Their operational support is tuned to the specific needs of these specialties:

- **Pediatric practices** have unique scheduling patterns (shorter appointments, higher volume, behavior management considerations), insurance mix (heavy Medicaid in many markets), and staffing requirements (pediatric-trained assistants and hygienists).
- **Orthodontic practices** operate on recurring treatment models (braces, Invisalign), with distinct patient acquisition cycles and long-term case management.
- **Oral surgery practices** require surgical suite standards, anesthesia protocols, and referral network management.

SALT's claim to specialty-specific operational expertise is plausible -- a DSO that only supports these specialties can develop deeper operational knowledge than a generalist DSO trying to serve everyone.

### SGA: Implantology, Periodontics, Multi-Specialty

SGA's specialty focus lives in the MODIS vertical, built around implantology and periodontics:

- **Implantology:** Full-arch cases, zygomatic implants, guided surgery, same-day procedures. Anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos (globally recognized) and Innovative Dental/Dr. Grant Olson (138K YouTube subs, 23M+ views, patented guided implant technology).
- **Periodontics:** Traditional periodontal services plus implant placement and bone grafting.
- **Oral surgery:** Represented in the MODIS network through partners like Coastal Jaw Surgery.

The key structural difference: MODIS is connected to the Pikos Institute, a world-class implantology education center. This means specialty partners in SGA do not just receive operational support -- they gain access to a clinical development platform that advances their skills.

### What This Means for You

- **If you are a pediatric dentist or orthodontist:** SALT is purpose-built for your specialty. SGA does not have a dedicated pediatric or orthodontic vertical.
- **If you are a periodontist or implantologist:** SGA/MODIS is purpose-built for you, with the Pikos Institute as the clinical anchor. SALT does not have a dedicated implantology education platform.
- **If you are an oral surgeon:** Both organizations support oral surgery practices. SGA's MODIS network includes oral surgery (Coastal Jaw Surgery), and SALT lists oral surgery as a core specialty.
- **If you are a general dentist with specialty ambitions:** SGA's three-vertical architecture provides internal pathways to develop implant skills through the Pikos Institute. SALT is primarily focused on practices that are already specialty-focused.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a pediatric dentist join SGA?

SGA's current verticals focus on general dentistry, multi-specialty collaborative practice, and implantology/periodontics. While SGA has not publicly positioned a dedicated pediatric vertical, pediatric practices may fit within certain SGA partner groups. However, if you are specifically seeking a DSO with deep pediatric-specific operational expertise, SALT is more directly aligned with your needs.

### Does SALT have an implant or periodontics focus?

SALT lists oral surgery as a core specialty, which includes some implant procedures. However, SALT does not operate a dedicated implantology vertical, an implant education institute, or a clinical development pathway for implant dentistry comparable to SGA's MODIS/Pikos offering.

### Which is larger -- SALT or SGA?

SALT has more individual locations (156+ vs SGA's 60-125+) and a presence in more states (20+ vs 10-14+). However, SGA's three-vertical architecture and the Thurston Group's financial infrastructure ($350M credit facility) position it for substantial growth. Both organizations are actively expanding.

### I am an oral surgeon. Which should I choose?

Both SALT and SGA support oral surgery practices. The deciding factors for an oral surgeon would be: (1) Do you want access to the Pikos Institute and implantology education? Choose SGA. (2) Do you want to be part of a network primarily composed of pediatric, ortho, and oral surgery specialists? Choose SALT. (3) Do you value a cross-referral network with general dentistry practices feeding complex cases to you? Choose SGA.

### What about SALT's de novo model?

SALT actively develops new practice locations (10+ planned in 2025), which is a growth strategy SGA does not currently emphasize. If opening new locations -- rather than bringing an existing practice into the partnership -- is part of your plan, SALT offers a pathway SGA does not.
