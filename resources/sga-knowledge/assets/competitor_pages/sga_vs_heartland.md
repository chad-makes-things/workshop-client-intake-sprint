Heartland Dental is the largest privately held DSO in the United States, with 1,800+ practices across 38 states, backed by KKR. SGA Dental Partners is a smaller, education-forward partnership platform backed by the Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+ returned to partners). ## Who Is Heartland Dental?

Heartland Dental was founded in 1997 by Dr. Rick Workman and has grown into the largest privately held dental support organization in the country. In 2021, KKR -- one of the world's largest private equity firms -- acquired a majority stake. Today, Heartland supports over 1,800 practices in 38+ states and reported approximately 11.5 million patient visits in 2025.

Heartland positions itself around "The Doctor Difference" -- the idea that its model is doctor-led and clinically independent. The core pitch is operational relief at scale: billing, HR, compliance, procurement, and technology, all handled by a massive support infrastructure so dentists can focus on patients.

## Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners is a dental partnership platform consolidating three Thurston Group-backed entities -- the original SGA network (Southeast community dentistry), Gen4 Dental Partners (collaborative general and specialty practice), and MODIS Dental Partners (implantology and specialty, anchored by the Pikos Institute). The combined platform spans 10-14+ states with a growing national footprint.

SGA's model is a joint-venture equity partnership: selling dentists receive upfront liquidity while retaining an ownership stake in the combined SGA platform. Clinical autonomy is contractual. Practices keep their names, teams, and cultures. The tagline captures the philosophy: "The partnership that makes dentists better."

## Deal Structure Comparison

Understanding how each organization structures its partnership is critical. The economics shape everything that follows.

### Heartland Dental

Heartland's model leans toward outright acquisition. A selling dentist typically receives a cash payment at close and transitions into an employment or management arrangement. The dentist no longer has an equity stake in the broader Heartland platform. The upside ended at the transaction.

For associates, Heartland offers employment with competitive compensation and benefits. There is no publicly disclosed pathway to equity ownership in the Heartland entity.

This model works well for dentists seeking a clean, full-liquidity exit -- particularly those near retirement who want maximum cash at close without ongoing equity complexity.

### SGA Dental Partners

SGA structures deals as joint-venture equity partnerships. A typical community or group practice partnership looks like:

- **60-70% upfront liquidity** at close -- a meaningful, life-changing cash event
- **30-40% retained as equity** in the combined SGA platform -- the "second bite of the apple"
- **Ongoing clinical compensation** (market-rate salary + production bonus, typically 28-32% of collections above threshold)
- **Equity appreciation** as SGA grows through acquisitions and organic performance

The Thurston Group's track record across 8+ dental and healthcare platforms, returning $4B+ to partners, provides the historical evidence that this equity model delivers real value over time.

**The fundamental difference:** Heartland pays you once. SGA pays you at close and continues building value alongside you.

### Market Context

In the current deal environment (2025-2026), mid-market dental practice EBITDA multiples generally range from 6-9x, with high-performing groups reaching 9-12x. Both Heartland and SGA operate within these bands, though the structure of payment differs significantly.

## Where SGA Dental Partners Wins

### 1. Equity Ownership in the Platform

This is the most significant structural difference. SGA's joint-venture model means partners retain equity in the combined platform and participate in its growth over time. Heartland's acquisition model does not offer this. The Thurston Group's $4B+ returned to partners across 8 platforms provides historical evidence that this equity model creates real, compounding wealth.

As one industry advisor noted, seven or eight out of ten dentists report frustration or regret after selling to a DSO -- and the equity structure is often the source. They got cash at close, but the upside stopped there.

### 2. The Education Platform

This is SGA's hardest-to-replicate advantage. The Pikos Institute is globally recognized in implantology education. The 4Front Symposium brings partner clinicians together for annual peer collaboration. The Individual Growth Engine pairs experienced partners with newer clinicians for 1:1 mentorship.

Both SGA and Heartland claim clinical autonomy. The difference is in the evidence.

SGA's autonomy is reinforced by the collaborative governance model -- a clinical advisory board led by practicing dentists, not corporate managers. This is not to say every Heartland dentist's experience is negative. But when the question is "Who controls my clinical decisions?", SGA's peer-led governance model provides stronger structural protection than Heartland's corporate management hierarchy.

### 4. The Thurston Group Track Record

KKR is bigger than Thurston Group. But size is not the same as relevance. Thurston has built this exact model -- PE-backed dental platform with clinical autonomy and equity partnership -- eight times. Across dental and healthcare. Over 38 years. Returning $4B+ to partners.

KKR acquired Heartland in 2021. They are a global PE giant, but their dental-specific track record is one platform. Thurston's track record is eight -- including Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management, and SGA itself.

When a dentist asks "Will the PE firm behind this keep its promises?", Thurston can answer with decades of verifiable, dental-specific evidence. That matters.

### 5. Three-Vertical Architecture

SGA is not just a general dentistry DSO. It operates three complementary verticals:

- **Community dentistry** (SGA original): hometown practices across the Southeast
- **Collaborative general and specialty** (Gen4): national footprint with peer-learning culture

This means a general dentist who joins SGA can, over time, develop implant capabilities through the Pikos Institute -- without leaving the organization. A periodontist can access the marketing infrastructure and patient flow of a national platform. No single-model DSO offers this internal career mobility.

Heartland is a general dentistry platform. If your ambitions stay within general dentistry, that may be enough. If you see yourself growing into implantology, advanced restorative, or multi-specialty practice, SGA provides pathways Heartland does not.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is SGA Dental Partners a DSO?

Technically, yes -- SGA is a dental service organization. But the model is structurally different from what most dentists picture when they hear "DSO." SGA operates a joint-venture equity partnership where dentists retain ownership in the combined platform, keep their practice names and teams, and maintain full clinical autonomy. Gen4 co-founder Dr. Mitch Ellingson has said: "We tell them we are not a DSO. We are not playing by their rules." The 4th-generation collaborative model was built as an explicit rejection of the standardization approaches that define traditional DSOs.

### How does SGA's equity model compare to Heartland's deal structure?

Heartland typically acquires practices outright -- cash at close, with the selling dentist transitioning to an employment arrangement. SGA structures deals as joint ventures: 60-70% upfront liquidity plus 30-40% retained as equity in the SGA platform. The difference is ongoing participation. With Heartland, your financial outcome is fixed at closing. With SGA, your equity appreciates as the platform grows. The Thurston Group has returned $4B+ to partners across 8 platforms using this model.

### Does Heartland Dental really offer clinical autonomy?

Heartland's marketing emphasizes "The Doctor Difference" and states there are no treatment or schedule quotas. However, employee reviews on Indeed and Glassdoor include complaints about non-dentist managers influencing clinical decisions and creating production pressure. At SGA, clinical autonomy is protected through peer-led governance -- a clinical advisory board of practicing dentists, not corporate managers. The distinction is structural: who actually controls clinical decisions day-to-day?

### I am a specialist (periodontist, oral surgeon, implantologist). Which is better for me?

SGA, specifically through the MODIS vertical. Heartland is a general dentistry platform without dedicated specialty infrastructure. MODIS was purpose-built for implantologists and specialty practitioners, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos and the Pikos Institute. MODIS partners receive capital investment for specialty infrastructure (CT scanners, surgical suites, in-house labs), dedicated recruiting for specialist associates, and access to a peer community of elite implantologists. Heartland does not offer a comparable specialty pathway.

### What if I just want to sell and retire?

If you are looking for a clean, full-liquidity exit with no ongoing involvement, Heartland's outright acquisition model may be simpler. SGA's equity model is designed for dentists who plan to remain clinically active and want their financial outcome to compound over time. If your timeline is "sell, transition for 6-12 months, then fully retire," discuss your situation with both organizations -- but understand that SGA's model is optimized for partners who stay engaged.

### How large is SGA compared to Heartland?

Heartland is significantly larger: 1,800+ practices across 38+ states versus SGA's 60-125+ practices across 10-14+ states. If sheer network size is your primary criterion, Heartland wins. However, SGA's growth trajectory is accelerating -- with a $350M credit facility and the consolidation of three platforms under one umbrella. And many dentists find that a smaller, more collaborative network creates a better partner experience than being one of 1,800.

### Can I talk to current SGA partners before making a decision?

Yes. SGA provides direct access to current partners who can speak to their experience. This includes dentists like Dr. Mitchel Godat (Periodontal Associates of Memphis), who has said: "SGA supports us and manages things behind the scenes. Our partnership allows us to focus on changing lives." Hearing from peers who have lived the partnership -- not just the sales team -- is one of the most effective ways to evaluate any DSO.
