# SGA Dental Partners -- Competitive Battle Cards

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Audience:** BD team, leadership, partnership development
**Use case:** Pre-call preparation, competitive objection handling, deal strategy
**Classification:** Internal use only -- do not share with prospects

---

## How to Use These Battle Cards

Each card follows the same structure:
1. **Overview** -- Who they are, what they claim, how big they are
2. **Their Pitch** -- What the prospect heard (or will hear) from them
3. **Their Weaknesses** -- Where the model breaks down
4. **SGA Advantages** -- Where we win head-to-head
5. **When They Say X, We Say Y** -- Specific objection rebuttals and talk tracks
6. **Honest Assessment** -- Where they are genuinely strong (never underestimate a competitor)

**Core rule:** Never trash a competitor by name to a prospect. Instead, draw contrasts against the MODEL. "Some DSOs do X. We do Y. Here is why." Let the prospect connect the dots.

## BATTLE CARD 1: HEARTLAND DENTAL

### Overview

- **Founded:** 1997 by Dr. Rick Workman
- **PE Sponsor:** KKR (acquired 2021)
- **Scale:** 1,800-1,900+ practices across 38+ states
- **Revenue:** $4.2-5B estimated
- **Model:** Primarily acquisition/employment. Dentists join as associates or sell outright. Not a JV equity partnership.
- **Tagline:** "The Doctor Difference"
- **Positioning:** Largest private DSO. Sells scale, resources, and "clinical independence."

### Their Pitch

"We are the largest privately supported dental organization in the US. We offer clinical independence, massive support infrastructure, and the resources of 1,800+ practices. No treatment quotas, no forced product endorsements. We supercharge your independence."

### Their Weaknesses

1. **Autonomy gap between marketing and reality.** Glassdoor and Indeed reviews consistently report manager override of clinical decisions, production pressure, and "crown days" or "Invisalign days" pushing dentists toward higher-revenue procedures. The "Doctor Difference" messaging rings hollow when non-dentist regional managers control daily operations.

2. **No JV equity model.** Heartland acquires practices or hires associates. Partners do not retain equity in a larger platform. The selling dentist gets cash and becomes an employee. No second bite of the apple. No ongoing equity appreciation.

3. **Scale creates impersonality.** At 1,800+ practices, individual partners report feeling like a number. The peer community that SGA offers -- where partners know each other by name and collaborate regularly -- does not exist at Heartland's scale.

4. **Gen2 DNA.** Heartland was built on the standardization model. Despite marketing updates, the operational culture still leans toward "same supplies in the same place in every office." The transition from Gen2 to Gen4 is structural, not cosmetic, and Heartland has not made it.

5. **Lawsuit history.** The Hardin Advanced Dentistry case alleges aggressive business practices that impacted practice autonomy. This is publicly searchable and prospects may already know about it.

### SGA Advantages vs. Heartland

| Dimension | Heartland | SGA |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Equity model | Acquisition/employment | JV equity partnership -- you retain ownership in the platform |
| Autonomy protection | Claimed but disputed | Structural: CCO is a practicing dentist; "dentists teaching dentists" culture |
| Peer community | 1,800 practices, impersonal | 100+ partner clinicians who know each other, collaborate at 4Front |
| Education | Generic CE programs | Pikos Institute + 4Front Symposium + Individual Growth Engine |
| PE sponsor | KKR (financial conglomerate) | Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+, 8 dental/healthcare platforms -- specialized) |
| Post-deal experience | "You become an employee" | You become a partner with equity, a mentor, and a peer community |

### When They Say X, We Say Y

**"Heartland is the largest, most established DSO. Can SGA compete with their resources?"**

> "Heartland has scale. That is real. But scale and partnership are different things. At Heartland, you sell your practice and become an associate in their system. At SGA, you retain equity in a growing platform, keep your name and your team, and get paired with a clinical mentor. The question is not who has more offices. The question is what your life looks like 3 years after signing. Ask Dr. Matt Cavendish -- he joined SGA after 20 years in practice. Revenues up 16.5%. Staff retention at 100%. His words: 'It is fun again.' That is not a Heartland story."

**"Heartland promises clinical independence."**

> "Every DSO promises clinical independence. The difference is in the structure. At Heartland, regional managers -- who are not dentists -- oversee daily operations. At SGA, our Chief Clinical Officer is a practicing dentist who has personally lived the transition from private practice to group. Our clinical advisory board is made up of partner dentists, not corporate executives. Clinical independence at SGA is not a marketing line. It is a governance structure."

**"Heartland has 25+ years of track record."**

> "Heartland has 25 years as a DSO. The Thurston Group has 38 years building healthcare partnerships and has returned over $4 billion to partners. The difference: Heartland's track record is about scale. Thurston's track record is about outcomes for the dentists who partnered with them. We can connect you with partners from Thurston's prior platforms -- Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management -- who can tell you exactly how their equity performed."

### Honest Assessment

Heartland's scale gives them genuine advantages in purchasing power, technology deployment, and career mobility for associates who want to relocate. Their brand recognition in the DSO space is unmatched. Do not dismiss their size -- some prospects find comfort in it. Our counter is not "we are bigger" but "we are better for YOU."

## BATTLE CARD 2: THE ASPEN GROUP (ASPEN DENTAL)

### Overview

- **Founded:** 1998
- **PE Sponsor:** Previously Leonard Green, Ares, American Securities
- **Scale:** 1,300+ Aspen Dental offices; 1,429 total TAG locations across brands
- **Revenue:** $4.2B annualized (first half 2025)
- **Model:** Franchise-like. Dentists operate under the Aspen Dental brand with standardized protocols.
- **Other brands:** ClearChoice (implants), WellNow Urgent Care, Chapter Aesthetic Studio
- **Positioning:** Accessible, affordable dental care for underserved communities

### Their Pitch

"Join Aspen Dental and get a fully built-out, branded practice with high patient volume, national marketing, and a proven operational system. We handle everything from marketing to real estate to technology. You focus on patients."

### Their Weaknesses

1. **Reputation crisis.** 1.2 stars from 311 reviews on Sitejabber. 4,100+ complaints on PissedConsumer with 950,000+ views. Multiple state AG investigations. $1.7M+ in settlements for deceptive practices. Patients report being "upsold over $5K worth of work" and discovering "cavities under 3 out of 4 crowns only 5 months after treatment."

2. **Zero identity preservation.** Every office is an Aspen Dental office. The selling dentist's name disappears. Their local brand disappears. This is the polar opposite of what practice owners who built something from nothing want.

3. **Production pressure culture.** Dentists report being told their "indirect to direct restoration ratio needed to be 2:1" and that patients receive unnecessary treatment recommendations to hit revenue targets. This is well-documented on forums, employee review sites, and in regulatory complaints.

4. **Not a partnership model.** Dentists at Aspen are functionally franchisees operating under a corporate brand with corporate protocols. There is no JV equity, no retained ownership, no platform upside.

5. **Irrelevant to SGA's target.** Aspen targets new grads who want a turnkey practice and patients who need affordable care. They do not compete for the mid-career practice owner who built a reputation in their community. If a prospect is comparing SGA to Aspen, the prospect is not our target.

### SGA Advantages vs. Aspen

| Dimension | Aspen | SGA |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| Brand identity | Replaced with Aspen brand | 100% preserved -- your name stays |
| Clinical autonomy | Standardized protocols | Full clinical independence |
| Equity model | Franchise-like arrangement | JV equity partnership with platform upside |
| Reputation | 1.2 stars (Sitejabber), AG investigations | Partner-first model with named, referenceable partners |
| Patient relationship | Aspen's patients, not yours | Your patients stay your patients |
| Target dentist | New grads seeking turnkey | Established owners seeking partnership |

### When They Say X, We Say Y

**"Aspen has incredible patient volume and marketing. Can SGA match that?"**

> "Aspen drives patient volume through a national consumer brand. That works for Aspen offices. But you did not build your practice for 20 years so it could become an Aspen Dental. Your patients chose YOU, not a brand they saw on a TV commercial. SGA preserves your name, your reputation, and your patient relationships -- and invests in marketing that grows YOUR brand, not ours. Dental Partners of SW Georgia is still Dental Partners of SW Georgia."

**"What about ClearChoice? They are the biggest name in implants."**

> "ClearChoice is a separate consumer brand within the Aspen Group. It is not integrated with Aspen Dental practices. SGA's MODIS vertical is different -- it is an integrated implantology platform where general dentists can refer to specialty partners, learn from the Pikos Institute, and develop implant capabilities within the same organization. ClearChoice is a brand. MODIS is a clinical ecosystem."

### Honest Assessment

Aspen is not a real competitive threat for practice owners. If a prospect mentions Aspen, it is usually because they are conflating all DSOs. This is an education opportunity, not a competitive battle. Use Aspen as a contrast -- "That is exactly the model we were built to be the alternative to."

## BATTLE CARD 3: PDS HEALTH (PACIFIC DENTAL SERVICES)

### Overview

- **Founded:** 1994 by Stephen Thorne
- **PE Sponsor:** Founder-led with minority PE investment
- **Scale:** 1,000+ practices across 24 states
- **Revenue:** $3.1B+ (2025)
- **Model:** "Owner Clinician" -- dentists own their practice, PDS charges management services fees. No platform equity.
- **Positioning:** Integrated dental-medical care platform. "Mouth-Body Connection."

### Their Pitch

"PDS supports Owner Clinicians who own their practice while we handle the business. We are not a DSO -- we are a health care company. Our Mouth-Body Connection integrates medical screening into dental visits, transforming how patients receive care."

### Their Weaknesses

1. **No platform equity.** This is the critical structural difference. PDS dentists own their individual practice. They do NOT own equity in the PDS platform. There is no "second bite of the apple." The dentist pays management fees to PDS and gets no upside from platform growth. SGA partners own equity in a growing multi-state platform.

2. **Not a liquidity event.** PDS's model is a management services arrangement, not an acquisition. A practice owner who needs cash at close to diversify, fund college tuition, or de-risk their net worth does not get that from PDS.

3. **Geographic concentration.** Historically West Coast-heavy. Limited Southeast presence. If a prospect is in SGA's core markets, PDS is geographically weak.

4. **"PDS Health" rebrand confusion.** Moving away from "Pacific Dental Services" to "PDS Health" to support the integrated care narrative has confused the market. Some dentists do not know what PDS does anymore.

5. **Integrated care is complex.** The "Mouth-Body Connection" is an interesting clinical innovation, but it adds operational complexity. Blood pressure screening and A1C testing in a dental office require training, compliance infrastructure, and patient communication that most dentists have not signed up for.

### SGA Advantages vs. PDS

| Dimension | PDS | SGA |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Equity model | Dentist owns individual practice only | JV equity in entire SGA platform |
| Liquidity event | No -- management services arrangement | Yes -- 60-70% cash at close |
| Platform upside | None -- dentist pays fees to PDS | Equity appreciation as platform grows |
| Education platform | Medical integration training | Pikos Institute + 4Front + Individual Growth Engine |
| Specialty vertical | Expanding into specialty | Dedicated implantology vertical (MODIS) |
| Financial sponsor | Founder-led, minority PE | Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+, 8 platforms) |

### When They Say X, We Say Y

**"PDS lets me own my practice. SGA wants me to sell."**

> "PDS uses the word 'own' to mean something specific: you own your individual practice and pay PDS management fees. You have no equity in the PDS platform. SGA uses the word 'own' differently: you sell a portion of your practice equity and receive ownership in the entire SGA platform. Your upside is not limited to your four walls. As the platform grows -- 100+ practices, three verticals, $350M credit facility -- your equity grows with it. The Thurston Group has returned over $4 billion to partners across 8 platforms. That is what platform equity means."

**"PDS is founder-led, which feels more trustworthy than PE-backed."**

> "Stephen Thorne has built something impressive. But 'founder-led' is not inherently safer than 'PE-backed.' The question is: what happens to your equity over time? PDS dentists own their individual practice. If PDS gets acquired or the management company changes terms, the dentist has no platform-level equity to protect. SGA partners own equity in the combined platform, and the Thurston Group has been doing this for 38 years. The track record is not theoretical. Call our partners."

**"PDS has the Mouth-Body Connection. What does SGA have that is innovative?"**

> "PDS is integrating medical screening into dental visits. That is a clinical innovation worth watching. SGA's innovation is different: we built an education platform -- the Pikos Institute, the 4Front Symposium, the Individual Growth Engine -- that makes dentists clinically better every year they are with us. Dr. Cavendish came in after 20 years of practice and learned to do all-on-four procedures. His revenues are up 16.5%. We do not just support your practice. We elevate your skills."

### Honest Assessment

PDS is a respected competitor with a genuinely differentiated clinical narrative (Mouth-Body Connection) and a founder-led culture that dentists trust. The Owner Clinician model appeals to dentists who want to feel like they own their practice. SGA's counter must be structural: platform equity, liquidity event, and education -- not emotional dismissal of a well-run company.

## BATTLE CARD 4: MB2 DENTAL

### Overview

- **Founded:** 2007
- **PE Sponsor:** Undisclosed / Private
- **Scale:** 800+ practices across 41 states
- **Model:** "Dental Partnership Organization (DPO)" -- partial equity sale, brand preservation, clinical autonomy. Functionally similar to SGA's JV model.
- **Tagline:** "Power to the Partners"
- **Positioning:** Anti-DSO. "We are not a DSO. We are a DPO."
- **Minimum criteria:** $1.25M revenue, 4+ operatories

### Their Pitch

"MB2 is the nation's largest Dental Partnership Organization. We are not a DSO. You sell only a portion of your practice, keep your brand, your team, and your clinical autonomy. Join 1,700+ dentists who chose partnership over corporate."

### Their Weaknesses

1. **"DPO" is a marketing distinction, not a structural one.** MB2 calls itself a DPO to create categorical distance from DSOs. But the model -- partial equity sale, operational support, brand preservation -- is functionally identical to what SGA, Guardian, and other JV DSOs offer. The term "DPO" has no legal or regulatory meaning. It is branding.

2. **No education platform.** This is MB2's biggest gap. They have zero CE infrastructure, no training institute, no clinical mentorship program. MB2 is pure business support. SGA's Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium, and Individual Growth Engine have no MB2 equivalent.

3. **No specialty vertical.** MB2 is a general dentistry platform. No implantology focus, no specialty career pathways. Specialists and dentists who want to grow into advanced procedures have no pathway at MB2.

4. **Opaque PE backing.** MB2's financial sponsor is not publicly disclosed. SGA's sponsor -- the Thurston Group -- has a 38-year, $4B+ verifiable track record. When a prospect asks "who is behind MB2?" there is no clear answer. When they ask "who is behind SGA?" there is a 38-year track record.

5. **Thin geographic density.** 800 practices across 41 states means an average of fewer than 20 practices per state. SGA's concentrated Southeast and national presence through targeted verticals creates deeper peer communities and operational density.

6. **Growth-at-all-costs risk.** Going from 0 to 800+ practices in 18 years is impressive but raises questions about partner experience at scale. Is every partner getting the attention they were promised?

### SGA Advantages vs. MB2

| Dimension | MB2 | SGA |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Education platform | None | Pikos Institute + 4Front + Individual Growth Engine |
| Specialty verticals | General dentistry only | Three: community, collaborative, implantology |
| PE track record | Undisclosed sponsor | Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+, 8 platforms) |
| Clinical mentorship | None | 1:1 mentorship through Individual Growth Engine |
| Post-deal growth | Business support only | Clinical skill development + business support |
| Geographic density | Thin (800 across 41 states) | Concentrated + targeted vertical expansion |
| Brand messaging | "DPO, not DSO" (marketing term) | "Get better, not just get bought" (substantive claim backed by education) |

### When They Say X, We Say Y

**"MB2 is the largest DPO. They have 800+ practices and 1,700+ dentists. That peer community is massive."**

> "MB2 has scale. What they do not have is structure. Having 1,700 dentists in a database is not the same as having 100+ clinicians who attend the 4Front Symposium together, collaborate on clinical cases, and challenge each other to think bigger. SGA's peer community is not a number. It is a culture. Dr. Ellingson describes it: 'For a lot of docs, they have been on their own for such a long time that they almost forgot how exciting it is to be with peers who are challenging each other.' MB2 offers a network. SGA offers a community."

**"MB2 says they are not a DSO. They are a DPO. Is that different?"**

> "The term 'DPO' is a marketing distinction MB2 created. There is no legal or structural difference between what MB2 calls a 'DPO' and what other organizations call a 'DSO' or 'IDSO.' The real question is not what they call themselves. The real question is what happens after you sign. At MB2, you get business support. At SGA, you get business support AND the Pikos Institute, the 4Front Symposium, a clinical mentor, and access to three specialized verticals. We do not differentiate through nomenclature. We differentiate through what we actually deliver."

**"MB2 has been growing fast. They must be doing something right."**

> "Rapid growth is a double-edged sword. MB2 went from 0 to 800+ practices. That is impressive acquisition velocity. The question is: what is the partner experience at practice number 750? SGA grows deliberately. The Thurston Group has been at this for 38 years -- they do not race to a number. They build platforms that compound value for partners over time. $4 billion returned is not a growth metric. It is an outcome metric."

### Honest Assessment

MB2 is SGA's most direct competitor structurally. The JV equity model, brand preservation, and clinical autonomy claims are nearly identical. The "DPO" branding is clever and has created market differentiation where there is little structural difference. SGA wins on three dimensions MB2 cannot match: education, specialty verticals, and PE track record transparency. Every conversation with a prospect considering MB2 should steer toward those three areas.

1. **Sovereign wealth fund ownership removes the "partnership" feel.** DCA was acquired by Mubadala, the Abu Dhabi sovereign wealth fund, in January 2025. 2. **No JV equity model.** DCA acquires practices into its alliance. There is no evidence of a JV equity structure where dentists retain platform ownership. The selling dentist gets cash and becomes part of the DCA system.

3. **No education platform.** No clinical training institute, no peer symposium, no mentorship program. DCA provides operational support -- nothing more.

4. **Bland brand identity.** "Shaping the future of dentistry together" is generic. "Go further with an ally" is forgettable. There is no distinctive message that a dentist would repeat to a colleague.

5. **Limited social proof.** DCA's website features practice-type divisions but few individual partner stories, testimonials, or transformation narratives. Difficult to find a named DCA partner who publicly advocates for the experience.

### SGA Advantages vs. DCA

| Dimension | DCA | SGA |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| PE sponsor | Mubadala (sovereign wealth fund) | Thurston Group (specialized healthcare PE, 38 years, $4B+) |
| Equity model | Acquisition (no JV) | JV equity partnership with platform upside |
| Education | None | Pikos Institute + 4Front + Individual Growth Engine |
| Partner advocacy | Limited public testimonials | Named partners who publicly share their experience |
| Specialty approach | Three divisions (structural) | Three verticals with integrated education (cultural + structural) |
| Identity | "Go further with an ally" | "Get better, not just get bought" |

### When They Say X, We Say Y

**"DCA has 30+ years of experience. They have been around longer than SGA."**

> "DCA has operated for 30 years. The Thurston Group has been building healthcare partnerships for 38 years and has returned over $4 billion to partners. Longevity and outcomes are different things. DCA was recently acquired by a sovereign wealth fund from Abu Dhabi. SGA is backed by the same PE firm that built Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, and US Oral Surgery Management. Our sponsor does not just fund dental organizations. They build them."

**"DCA has dedicated divisions for each practice type. That sounds like specialized support."**

> "DCA has organizational divisions. SGA has clinical verticals with integrated education. The difference: DCA sorts practices into categories and provides operational support by type. SGA does that AND connects partners to the Pikos Institute for implantology training, pairs them with clinical mentors, and brings them together at the 4Front Symposium. Having a 'pediatric division' is organizational. Having a peer education ecosystem is transformational."

### Honest Assessment

DCA is a mid-tier competitor that rarely comes up in direct competition with SGA. Their practice-type specialization is genuinely useful structurally. The Mubadala acquisition gives them deep capital. But their brand is bland, their social proof is thin, and they do not compete on the dimensions SGA leads with (education, PE track record, partner community).
