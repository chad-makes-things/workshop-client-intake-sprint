# SGA Dental Partners -- Elevator Pitch Scripts

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Audience:** BD team, leadership, anyone representing SGA in conversation
**Use case:** Conferences, cocktail parties, phone call openers, networking events, impromptu introductions
**Total scripts:** 12 (3 lengths x 4 audiences)

---

## How to Use These Scripts

**Three lengths:**
- **15-second pitch** -- The cocktail party version. Someone asks "What do you do?" and you have one breath.
- **30-second pitch** -- The conference version. You are at a dental conference and someone has stopped at your booth or you are sitting next to them at lunch.
- **60-second pitch** -- The BD call opener. You are on a phone call with a warm lead who has agreed to hear the overview.

**Four audiences:**
- **Practice Owners** -- Mid-career dentists considering a partnership or liquidity event
- **Associates** -- Early-career dentists looking for their next career move
- **Specialists** -- Periodontists, oral surgeons, implantologists evaluating the MODIS vertical
- **Referring Dentists** -- Dentists who may not partner themselves but can refer colleagues or patients

**Rules:**
1. Memorize the structure, not the exact words. Deliver naturally, not robotically.
2. End every pitch with either a question or a clear next step. Never end with a claim.
3. Mirror the listener's language. If they say "my practice," you say "your practice." If they say "sell," you say "partner."
4. Lead with the problem or the proof, never with "SGA is..."
5. The Sage archetype: wise counsel, not aggressive sales. You are sharing, not pitching.

> "I work with SGA Dental Partners. We partner with practice owners who are exhausted by the business side of dentistry but do not want to lose what they built. **Key elements:** Problem (exhausted by business side) + Differentiator (name stays, education) + Trust anchor (38 years, $4B) + Open-ended question to continue the conversation.

**Key elements:** Problem hook (7 out of 10 regret stat) + Category reframe (not a corporate DSO) + Three differentiators (education, peer community, Thurston track record) + Big idea line + CTA.

His revenues are up 16.5%. Staff retention: 100%. He told us: 'It is fun again.'

> Second -- the Thurston Group. Our financial sponsor has been doing this for 38 years. Eight dental and healthcare platforms built. Over $4 billion returned to partners. That is not a projection. It is a track record you can verify.

> Third -- structure. Your name stays on the door. Your team stays in the chairs. You retain equity in the combined SGA platform and your upside does not end at closing.

**Key elements:** Empathy opening + Market problem (regret stat) + Three-pillar structure (education with Cavendish proof, Thurston track record, structural preservation) + Humility (I do not know if we are the right fit) + Soft CTA.

## Pitch Training Notes

### What to Memorize

Do not memorize these scripts word for word. Memorize:
1. The opening hook for each audience (the first sentence)
2. The three differentiators: Education (Pikos + 4Front + IGE), Thurston Track Record (38 years, $4B+, 8 platforms), Structure (name stays, team stays, equity)
3. The Cavendish proof story (20 years, complacency, all-on-four, 16.5%, "it is fun again")
4. The big idea: "Dentists come to SGA to get better, not just to get bought."
5. The closing CTA: "Would it make sense to schedule 45 minutes..."

### What to Practice

1. Deliver each pitch to a timer. The 15-second version must actually be 15 seconds.
2. Record yourself. Listen for corporate jargon, filler words, and any sentence that sounds like a brochure rather than a human being.
3. Practice the 60-second version until you can deliver it while maintaining eye contact (or natural phone conversation cadence).
4. Role-play objections after the pitch. The most common immediate response to any pitch is a version of "How are you different from Heartland / MB2 / the DSO my friend regrets joining?" -- have your one-sentence answer ready.

### The One-Sentence Differentiator

If you can only say ONE sentence about SGA, say this:

> "SGA is the only dental partnership with a built-in clinical education platform -- the Pikos Institute and the 4Front Symposium -- backed by a PE firm that has done this 8 times and returned $4 billion to partners over 38 years."

That sentence contains education (Pikos + 4Front), track record (8 platforms, $4B, 38 years), and implicitly signals the quality of the partner community. It takes 10 seconds. It is unique to SGA. No competitor can say it.
