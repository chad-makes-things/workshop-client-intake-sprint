## How Results Are Delivered

Each respondent sees a results page immediately after submitting their email. The page includes:

1. **Overall score** (e.g., "Your Partnership Readiness Score: 38 / 75") with tier label
2. **Category radar chart** showing all 5 category scores visually
3. **Category-by-category breakdown** with individual scores and one-line interpretations
4. **Tier-specific analysis** (the personalized paragraphs below)
5. **Personalized recommendations** (tied to SGA features, but valuable even without SGA)
6. **Tier-specific CTA**
7. **PDF report download link** (delivered via email with expanded analysis)

The same content is repurposed across three touchpoints:
- **Results page** (immediate, on-screen)
- **Email 1** (Day 0 -- results delivery email with PDF attachment)
- **PDF report** (downloadable, branded, printable -- the "take to your financial advisor" document)

## Tier 1: Foundation Builder (Score 15-30)

### Results Page Headline

### Personalized Analysis

You scored [X] out of 75, which places you in our Foundation Builder category. The fact that you took this assessment puts you ahead of the vast majority of practice owners who never pause to evaluate where they stand. Self-awareness is the first step toward any meaningful change.

Your results suggest your practice is in an earlier stage of development across several key dimensions. That is not a judgment -- it is a starting point. Many of the most successful dental practices in the country went through a foundation-building phase where the owner recognized that clinical excellence alone was not enough to build a sustainable, transferable business.

**Here is what your category scores are telling you:**

- **Practice Performance [X/15]:** There may be opportunities to strengthen your revenue trajectory, improve margin visibility, or tighten patient retention systems. A practice generating solid clinical outcomes but inconsistent financial results usually has a systems problem, not a talent problem.

- **Operational Independence [X/15]:** Your practice likely depends heavily on you personally -- primary producer, decision-maker, and cultural anchor all at once. If you are the practice, the practice cannot exist without you. The weight of that is something you probably feel every Sunday night.

- **Clinical Growth Trajectory [X/15]:** Your clinical development may have plateaued -- not from lack of ambition, but because the business side has consumed the bandwidth you need for clinical growth. The irony: clinical growth is what most practice owners entered dentistry to pursue.

- **Market Positioning [X/15]:** Your competitive awareness and referral networks may have room to strengthen. DSO-backed practices are entering markets with significant budgets and technology investments. The good news: local, relationship-driven practices have inherent advantages -- you just need to leverage them more deliberately.

- **Personal Alignment [X/15]:** Whether you are feeling burned out, financially pressured, or uncertain about the next chapter, these feelings are shared by the majority of practice owners who take this assessment. You are carrying the clinical, business, and emotional weight alone -- usually without a peer who truly understands.

A Foundation Builder score does not mean your practice is failing. It means your practice has untapped potential currently limited by structural constraints -- systems, capital, bandwidth, or strategic clarity. Addressing these constraints is the path to transformation, whether you pursue it independently or with a partner.

### Recommendations

**Priority 1: Reduce owner dependency.** Start documenting your three most critical operational processes this month. If your office manager left tomorrow, could someone else run the front desk from a written playbook? If not, that is your first project. Even basic documentation -- a scheduling protocol, a new-patient intake checklist, a billing workflow guide -- dramatically reduces fragility.

**Priority 2: Get financial clarity.** If you do not know your EBITDA within $50K, schedule time with your accountant this quarter to build a simple P&L dashboard. You cannot improve what you do not measure. Track three numbers monthly: collections, overhead percentage, and patient retention rate. These three metrics will tell you more about your practice's health than any consultant's binder.

**Priority 3: Invest in one clinical growth area.** Choose a single service line you want to develop -- implants, clear aligners, sleep dentistry, whatever excites you clinically -- and commit to one advanced CE course in the next six months. Do not try to expand in five directions. Pick one, go deep, and let it reignite the clinical curiosity that brought you to dentistry in the first place.

**Priority 4: Build one referral relationship this quarter.** Identify one specialist or one GP in your area whose patients overlap with yours and invite them to lunch. A single strong referral relationship can generate 3-5 new patients per month. Over a year, that is 36-60 patients with zero advertising cost.

**Priority 5: Explore the Practice Transition Playbook.** We have assembled a resource library specifically for practices in the Foundation Builder stage, covering topics from financial benchmarking to succession planning fundamentals. These resources are yours to use regardless of whether you ever consider a partnership -- they are designed to help you build the foundation that makes every future option stronger.
