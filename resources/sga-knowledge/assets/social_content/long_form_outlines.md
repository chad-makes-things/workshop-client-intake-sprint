- Gen4: Collaborative, clinician-centric, education-forward -- built from the dentist's perspective, not the investor's
- Include Lamonte Jensen quote: "Most DSOs are built around the synergies of the business. What we wanted to do was make an organization that was clinician-focused and patient-obsessed."

**H2: What the 30% Who Do Not Regret Have in Common**
- Factor 1: They chose a partner whose PE sponsor has a verifiable multi-platform track record (not a first-time fund)
- Factor 2: They retained clinical autonomy in practice (not just in the contract)
- Factor 3: They entered a peer community that made them better, not just better supported

- Weave in SGA proof points naturally: Thurston Group track record ($4B+, 38 years, 8 platforms), Dr. Godat testimonial, Dr. Cavendish transformation (16.5% revenue growth, 100% retention)

### Outline 5: Comparison Content

**Title:** "How to Evaluate a DSO Partnership: The Practice Owner's Complete Framework"

**Target Avatar:** Dr. Mark Hendricks (practice owner)
**Target Keyword:** "how to evaluate a DSO" / "DSO partnership checklist" / "selling dental practice checklist" / "DSO due diligence"
**Awareness Level:** Solution-Aware (actively researching DSO options)
**Estimated Length:** 3,000-3,500 words (blog) -- this is a cornerstone piece

**H2 Outline:**

**H2: Why Most Dentists Evaluate DSOs Wrong**
- The common mistake: evaluating based on the offer letter alone
- What actually determines satisfaction 2+ years post-deal: cultural fit, operational follow-through, peer community, post-deal investment
- The Dentist Advisors data: regret correlates with expectation mismatch, not deal terms

**H2: The 7 Evaluation Dimensions**

**H3: Dimension 1 -- Clinical Autonomy (Substance, Not Slogans)**
- Every DSO claims it. How to verify: ask for named partner references (not corporate testimonials), understand clinical governance structure (who is the CCO? Are they a practicing clinician?), review supply chain policies (do you choose your lab and materials or are they mandated?)
- SGA benchmark: CCO is Dr. Mitch Ellingson, a practicing dentist. Partners choose their labs, materials, and schedules.

**H3: Dimension 2 -- Financial Structure and Equity Model**
- JV equity vs. full buyout vs. management fee arrangements
- Key questions: what happens to your equity if you leave? Are there clawback provisions? What is the mechanism for equity appreciation?
- SGA benchmark: JV equity partnership with ongoing ownership for as long as you remain a partner. Thurston Group: $4B+ returned to partners across 8 platforms.

**H3: Dimension 3 -- PE Sponsor Track Record**
- First-time fund vs. experienced healthcare platform builder
- Key questions: how many dental platforms has the sponsor built? What were the outcomes for partners (not just investors)? What is the hold period?
- SGA benchmark: Thurston Group, 38 years, 8+ dental and healthcare platforms.

**H3: Dimension 4 -- Post-Deal Operational Support**
- Specific functions: billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing, recruiting
- Key questions: who handles each function? What systems do they use? How quickly do they integrate?
- SGA benchmark: iSolved payroll, PeopleOps team, centralized billing and compliance

**H3: Dimension 5 -- Education and Clinical Development**
- CE stipend vs. education platform
- Key questions: what mentorship programs exist? What CE opportunities are available? Is there a structured path for clinical skill development?
- SGA benchmark: Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium, Individual Growth Engine

**H3: Dimension 6 -- Brand and Culture Preservation**
- Name retention, team retention, patient communication
- Key questions: has any practice been rebranded? What is the staff retention rate? How is the acquisition communicated to patients and team?
- SGA benchmark: all practices retain local names, 85% staff retention rate

**H3: Dimension 7 -- Peer Community and Collaboration**
- Partner network quality, collaboration mechanisms
- Key questions: how do partners interact? Is there a clinical advisory board? Can I attend a partner event before committing?
- SGA benchmark: regular partner calls, clinical advisory board, 4Front Symposium, "dentists teaching dentists" culture

**H2: Red Flags That Should Stop the Conversation**
- No named references available within 24 hours
- Vague equity terms ("we'll work it out during integration")
- Recent PE sponsor with no healthcare track record
- Mandatory supply chain or lab vendors
- Rebranding of any acquired practice

**H2: Green Flags That Signal a Real Partnership**
- Named partners willing to take your call
- Specific staff retention numbers
- Practicing clinician in clinical leadership
- Transparent equity mechanics
- Post-deal transformation stories (Year 2+, not just closing day)

**CTA:** "Download the Practice Partnership Assessment -- a self-evaluation framework you can use with any DSO, including SGA. And when you are ready for a direct conversation, schedule a confidential call with our partnership team."
