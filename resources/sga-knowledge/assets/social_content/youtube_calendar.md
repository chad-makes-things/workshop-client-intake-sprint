### Shorts Production Notes

- **Extraction workflow:** During Video 1-8 editing, editor marks Short-worthy moments with timecodes. Shorts are cut as a separate batch after long-form editing is complete.
- **Export format:** Vertical 9:16, under 60 seconds. Export CLEAN versions without watermarks for cross-posting to TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- **Captions:** Burned-in captions on all Shorts (85%+ of mobile viewers watch without sound). Use bold, high-contrast text. 2-3 words per screen.
- **Hooks:** Every Short must have a visual and/or text hook in the first 1 second. No logos, no intros, no "hey guys" -- straight into the content.
- **CTAs:** Verbal CTA in last 3 seconds: "Follow for more" or "Full video on my channel." Do NOT use "link in bio" (that is TikTok/IG language, not YouTube).
- **Posting cadence:** 4-5 Shorts per week, staggered from long-form publish days. Post Shorts on Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat to fill gaps between Tue/Thu long-form publishes.

## SEO and Discovery Notes

- **Titles:** Every title includes the primary keyword. Use specific numbers, years, or emotional language. Avoid generic titles like "Dental Implant Video."
- **Descriptions:** Primary CTA link (YourSmileDestination.com) in the FIRST line. Cost guide lead magnet link in the SECOND line. Full keyword-rich description below the fold.
- **Tags:** Include 5-10 relevant tags per video. Mix broad ("dental implants") with specific ("full arch dental implants cost 2026") and brand ("Innovative Dental," "Dr. Grant Olson").
- **Chapters:** Add timestamps to every long-form video. This improves SEO (chapters appear in search results) and retention (viewers can jump to relevant sections).
- **Pinned comments:** Pin a CTA comment on every video: "Ready to start your smile journey? Book a free virtual consult: YourSmileDestination.com"
- **End screens:** Every video ends with a subscribe button + suggested next video (keep viewers in session). Suggested video should be the most relevant related content (transformation videos suggest other transformations; education videos suggest the cost guide video).
- **Cards:** Add one card per video at the 50% mark pointing to a relevant companion video.
- **Playlists:** Organize into: "Patient Transformations," "Dental Implant Education," "Behind the Scenes," "Live Q&As," "For Dentists" -- each playlist auto-advances to keep session time high.
