## A/B TEST PLAN

### Test 1: Hook Type -- Practice Owners

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Ad** | Variation 1 "Sunday Night" (emotional/situational) | Variation 2 "Horror Stories" (fear/stat) |
| **Hypothesis** | The situational hook ("Sunday night, kitchen table") will outperform the statistical hook ("7 out of 10") on engagement rate because it creates a visceral, personal recognition moment. The statistical hook will drive more clicks because it introduces a cognitive gap (what did the other 3 do?). |
| **Success Metric** | CTR > 1.0%, CPL < $100 |
| **Duration** | 14 days, minimum 3,000 impressions per variation |

### Test 2: CTA Type -- Practice Owners

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **CTA** | "Learn More" (primary: schedule conversation) | "Download" (transitional: practice partnership assessment) |
| **Hypothesis** | The transitional CTA will generate 2-3x more leads at lower CPL. The primary CTA will produce fewer but higher-intent leads. Measure both volume and downstream conversion to sales conversation. |
| **Success Metric** | Transitional CPL < $75, Primary Conversation Rate > 25% |
| **Duration** | 21 days |

### Test 3: Creative Format -- Patient Acquisition

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |

## CAMPAIGN NOTES

### Facebook-Specific Best Practices
- Mobile-first creative: 4:5 aspect ratio for maximum feed real estate on mobile (where 85%+ of Facebook engagement occurs)
- First 125 characters of Primary Text must stand alone as the hook -- everything after is hidden behind "See More"
- Video content: add captions (85% of Facebook video is watched without sound)
- Frequency cap: 2-3 impressions per person per week for B2B, 4-5 per week for B2C (higher frequency needed for patient conversion)

### B2B Practice Owner Notes
- Run ads during evening and weekend hours (Thursday-Sunday) when practice owners are scrolling at home, feeling the weight of admin burden
- Use Facebook Lead Forms (not external landing page) for lower friction -- auto-fill reduces drop-off by 30-50%
