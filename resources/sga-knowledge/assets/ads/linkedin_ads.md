## A/B TEST PLAN

### Test 1: Hook Type (Awareness Stage)

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Ad** | Variation 1 "Regret Stat" | Variation 2 "QuickBooks" |
| **Hook** | Fear/problem-focused (7 out of 10 regret) | Relief/aspiration-focused (QuickBooks line) |
| **Hypothesis** | Fear-based hooks will generate higher CTR but lower lead quality. Relief-based hooks will attract warmer prospects who are further along in their decision. |
| **Success Metric** | CTR > 0.8%, Lead Form Completion Rate > 15% |
| **Duration** | 14 days minimum, 500 impressions per variation minimum |

### Test 2: CTA Type (Engagement Stage)

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **CTA** | Primary: "Schedule a Confidential Conversation" | Transitional: "Download the Practice Partnership Assessment" |
| **Hypothesis** | The transitional CTA (Document Ad, Variation 5) will generate 2-3x more leads at a lower CPL, but the primary CTA will produce higher-quality leads measured by sales team conversion rate. |
| **Success Metric** | CPL < $150 for transitional, Conversation Rate > 20% for primary |
| **Duration** | 21 days, track downstream conversion to actual sales conversations |

### Test 3: Ad Format (Specialist Audience)

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Format** | Single Image (Variation 1 "Infrastructure Gap") | Carousel (Variation 4 "Specialist Community") |
| **Hypothesis** | Carousel will generate higher engagement rate (multiple touchpoints per impression), but single image will generate more lead form completions (less cognitive load). |
| **Success Metric** | Engagement Rate > 2%, Lead Form Completions > 10 per week |
| **Duration** | 14 days, minimum 1,000 impressions per variation |

### Test 4: Thought Leader Ad vs. Company Page Ad

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Source** | Thought Leader Ad from Lamonte Jensen (Variation 3) | Company page Sponsored Content (Variation 1) |
| **Hypothesis** | Thought Leader Ads will generate 2x engagement and lower CPL because they appear as organic content from a named individual, which builds trust faster with a skeptical dentist audience. |
| **Success Metric** | CPL difference > 30%, Engagement Rate > 1.5% |
| **Duration** | 21 days, matched budgets |

### Test 5: Authority Anchor (Specialist Audience)

| Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Authority** | Pikos Trust Bridge (Variation 2) -- clinical authority | PE Fear / Thurston Track Record (Variation 3) -- financial authority |
| **Hypothesis** | For specialists (periodontists, oral surgeons), clinical authority (Pikos) will outperform financial authority (Thurston) on CTR and lead quality, because clinical credibility is the primary trust signal for this audience. |

## CAMPAIGN NOTES

### Frequency Management
- Cap frequency at 4 impressions per person per week to avoid fatigue in a small, high-value audience
- Rotate creative every 3-4 weeks; refresh copy angles monthly

### Retargeting Layer
- Build a retargeting audience from: website visitors (sgadental.com, gen4dental.com, modisdental.com), lead form openers who did not submit, LinkedIn Company Page visitors
- Retarget with Variation 4 (Carousel/Proof) and Variation 5 (Document Ad) -- mid-to-bottom funnel formats

### Lead Nurture Handoff
- All leads from LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms should flow into CRM within 1 hour
- Business development team (Marcus Morosin for specialists, appropriate contact for general practice owners) should make personal outreach within 24 hours of form submission
- Lead nurture email sequence supplements but does not replace personal outreach

### Reporting Cadence
- Weekly: Impressions, CTR, CPL, Lead Form Completion Rate
- Bi-weekly: Lead quality scoring (sales team feedback), downstream conversation rate
- Monthly: Cost per qualified conversation, pipeline value attribution
