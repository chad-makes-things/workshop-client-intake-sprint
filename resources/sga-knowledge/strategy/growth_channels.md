## Strategic Overview

SGA operates in a trust-first market. The dental practice acquisition decision cycle runs 6-18 months. The specialist partnership decision is even more deliberate. The implant patient decision carries surgical and financial weight that no ad creative can shortcut.

That reality shapes every growth channel in this plan. The channels recommended here are not designed to generate immediate conversions. They are designed to build referral density, warm qualified audiences, and install SGA inside the professional conversations that eventually become partnership inquiries and patient consultations.

The core thesis: SGA's most credible growth engines are dentists who have already partnered, trusted intermediaries (dental CPAs, brokers, attorneys) who are already in the room when a dentist makes a transition decision, and a professional community that makes SGA impossible to ignore in the dental circles that matter.

## Section 1: Referral Program Design

### 1.1 Program Architecture: Three Referral Tracks

SGA requires three distinct referral programs, not one. Each track serves a different referrer, a different referred party, and a fundamentally different value exchange.

**Track A: Partner Physician Referral (B2B -- Practice Owner to Practice Owner)**
**Track B: Associate Dentist Referral (B2B -- Associate to Associate)**
**Track C: Implant Patient Referral (B2C -- Patient to Patient)**

These must be tracked, messaged, and incentivized separately. What motivates a practice owner referring a peer is entirely different from what motivates a patient who loved their smile transformation.

---

### Track A: Partner Referral Program (Practice Owner to Practice Owner)

**The Economic Case**

A single successful practice acquisition generates $500K-$2M in deal value for SGA and represents $500K-$3M in lifetime revenue from the acquired practice group. At those deal values, a $10,000-$25,000 referral incentive is not generosity -- it is the most efficient B2B acquisition tool available. Dental brokers charge 8-12% of deal value for the same introduction. SGA can intercept that spend and redirect it to the people with the most social proof: existing partners.

**Incentive Structure**

| Referral Outcome | Referrer Reward | Referred Dentist (After Close) |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| Intro meeting held (qualified referral) | $500 donation to referrer's charity of choice | None at this stage |
| Partnership agreement signed | $10,000 direct payment or $15,000 equity credit in SGA platform | Welcome package + accelerated onboarding |
| Referred practice completes first year as a partner | $5,000 additional bonus payment | Recognition at 4Front Symposium, featured in partner spotlight |

**Why equity credit as an alternative:** Many established practice owners are less motivated by cash than by compounding ownership. Offering $15,000 in SGA equity credit instead of $10,000 cash serves two purposes -- it increases the referrer's stake in SGA's growth and deepens their alignment with the platform's success.

**Referral Mechanism**

The mechanism must match the medium through which dentists actually communicate. Practice owners talk to other dentists at conferences, on golf courses, and through peer CE events -- not through online referral dashboards.

- Personalized referral URLs: `sgadental.com/referred-by/[partner-name]` -- clean, shareable in any medium
- Unique QR codes printed on partner business cards, included in SGA partner onboarding kit
- Direct handoff option: partner gives SGA business development the referred dentist's name and contact info; SGA business development follows up, attribution tracked manually
- Partner portal (Phase 2, once 20+ active referrers exist): simple dashboard to track referral status and reward accrual

**Referral Ask Timing**

The referral ask should never be deployed at the beginning of the partner relationship. It belongs at these moments:

1. The 90-day post-onboarding check-in -- when the partner has experienced the "relief moment" (the first payroll cycle handled by iSolved, the first HR question answered by PeopleOps)
2. The 4Front Symposium and Pikos Institute events -- in-person conversations at a high-enthusiasm moment
3. After a positive NPS survey response (score of 9-10) -- these self-identified promoters are 3x more likely to refer when asked immediately
4. At the one-year partnership anniversary -- reinforced by recognition of their growth metrics

**Viral Coefficient Target**

For B2B referral programs with high-value, low-frequency decisions:

- Referral rate target: 15-20% of active partners attempt a referral in any given year (this is strong for a B2B high-consideration product)
- Referrals per referrer: 1.5 average (some partners will refer one, a handful will become super-referrers who bring in 3-5 practices)
- Conversion rate: referral intro to signed partnership: 30-40% (significantly higher than cold outreach; warm intro with peer credibility collapses the sales cycle)
- Estimated K-factor contribution: 0.06-0.08 (not viral in the consumer app sense, but meaningful -- at 100 partners, this generates 6-8 new partners per year through referral alone, compounding as the partner base grows)

### Track C: Implant Patient Referral (Patient to Patient)

**Economic Case**

Full-arch implant treatment runs $25,000-$60,000 per patient. A single referred patient generates $1,000-$3,000+ in net contribution after referral reward. The referred patient also has higher treatment acceptance rates (peer recommendation removed the credibility barrier) and higher 5-star review rates.

**Incentive Structure**

Two-sided program. Both the referrer and the referred patient receive value.

| Referral Outcome | Referrer Reward | Referred Patient Reward |
|-----------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Referred patient books a consultation | $100 credit toward referrer's next treatment or whitening | $200 off consultation fee |
| Referred patient accepts treatment | $500 additional credit (toward future treatment, family members' treatment, or take-home whitening kit) | $500 off treatment plan |

**Why "treatment credit" over cash:** For dental patients, credits toward their own future care or their family's care have higher perceived value than equivalent cash. A $500 whitening-kit credit feels like receiving something premium, not being paid $500. It also drives loyalty and return visits.

**Mechanism**

- Post-treatment text message: "Your smile is just the beginning. Every person you refer gets $200 off their consultation, and you get $500 in care credits. Share your journey: [personalized link]"
- Practice front desk hands patient a referral card at checkout ("Share this with someone who deserves a smile like yours")
- Email at 30 days post-treatment: "You've had your new smile for a month. How does it feel? If someone you know has been putting this off, your story might be what moves them forward."
- Dr. Olson video testimonial outreach: patients who appear in transformation videos receive a VIP referral code with enhanced benefits ($750 credit instead of $500) -- their video already functions as a referral; the program amplifies it

**Viral Coefficient Target**

- Referral rate target: 20-25% of implant patients refer at least one person within 12 months (implant transformation creates strong emotional motivation to share)
- Referrals per referrer: 1.3 average
- Conversion rate (referred lead to booked consultation): 35-45% (peer-referred leads are higher quality, lower anxiety)
- Estimated K-factor contribution: 0.08-0.10 (meaningful contribution to patient acquisition, reduces paid media dependence over time)

### 1.2 Referral Program Infrastructure

**Platform Recommendation:** For B2B (Tracks A and B), implement with a lightweight CRM-integrated solution -- a custom Webflow or HubSpot page per partner with a tracked URL is sufficient at the current scale. For B2C (Track C), use a dedicated referral platform: **Friendbuy** (best-in-class for high-value healthcare patient referrals) or **ReferralCandy** if e-commerce-adjacent integrations are preferred.

**Tracking and Attribution Setup:**

- Track A (Partner): CRM field in HubSpot/Salesforce ("Referral Source") linked to partner name; partner liaison manually attributes introductions
- Track B (Associate): DentalPost profile or LinkedIn UTM + ATS integration (Greenhouse or Lever) with referral source field
- Track C (Patient): Friendbuy or custom Webflow referral page with unique URL per patient; integrate with practice management software (Dentrix, Eaglesoft) for treatment acceptance attribution

**Fraud Prevention (Track C):** Require referred patient to actually complete a consultation before referrer receives the $100 credit. Require treatment acceptance before $500 credit is issued. No self-referrals possible if email/phone verification is required at booking.

## Section 2: Partner/Affiliate Program Architecture

### 2.1 Strategic Context

SGA's professional intermediary ecosystem is different from a standard affiliate program. The partners SGA needs are not content creators or review bloggers. They are trusted advisors who are physically present in the room when a dentist makes a practice transition decision.

The dental practice transition ecosystem includes:
- Dental practice brokers (DDSmatch, Henry Schein Dental Practice Transitions, ADS Transitions, Choice Transitions)
- CPAs specializing in dental practices (national firms: ADCPA, Gorczyca Orthodontics CPA network; regional specialists)
- Dental attorneys (practice acquisition, partnership agreements, non-competes)
- Dental supply reps (Patterson, Schein, Benco -- have relationships with every dentist in their territory)
- Dental coaches and practice management consultants (Spear Education network, Tony Robbins Business Mastery dental cohort, Scheduling Institute consultants)
- Dental-specific financial planners (The Dentist Money Show network, Dentist Advisors)

Each of these intermediary types has a fundamentally different relationship to the referral, different compliance considerations, and different incentive structures.

### 2.2 Commission Structure

**Critical distinction:** Dental attorneys and CPAs are licensed professionals with strict referral fee regulations under state bar rules and CPA ethical guidelines. Direct cash commissions for referrals may be prohibited or restricted for these partners. The commission structure must be segmented by partner type.

**Partner Type A: Dental Practice Brokers**

Commission model: **Flat fee per qualified introduction that closes**

| Milestone | Payment |
|-----------|---------|
| Introduction to a qualified practice owner (who enters SGA due diligence process) | $1,000 flat fee |
| Practice partnership signed | $5,000 flat fee (this is a co-brokerage fee, distinct from broker's commission from the seller) |
| Practice completes first year of partnership | $2,500 additional |

Rationale: Brokers are accustomed to fee-based relationships. The total of $8,500 per close is competitive. It does not interfere with the seller-side commission that the broker earns from the dentist client. Position this as: "We pay for the warm introduction. You still earn your full commission from the seller."

**Partner Type B: Dental Coaches and Practice Management Consultants**

Commission model: **Percentage-based referral fee (5-8% of first year's management services revenue from acquired practice)**

| Partner Tier | Commission | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Registered partner | 5% of Year 1 management services revenue | $0 threshold |
| Active partner (3+ referrals/year) | 7% of Year 1 revenue | 3 closed partnerships |
| Elite partner (6+ referrals/year) | 8% + co-marketing budget allocation | 6 closed partnerships |

Consultants are already advising dentists on operational improvements. The natural progression of "your practice is now performing well -- what does your transition plan look like?" is where SGA fits. Commission must be meaningful enough to motivate a redirect from "sell to a broker" to "contact SGA first."

**Partner Type C: Dental Supply Reps (Patterson, Schein, Benco)**

Commission model: **Gift-and-recognition model** (direct cash referral fees to individual supply reps may violate employer policies)

- Supply reps who facilitate SGA introductions receive SGA Partner Events invitations (4Front Symposium access as a guest), recognition as "SGA Technology Partners" in practice marketing materials, and a $500 donation to the rep's chosen charity per closed partnership.
- Alternative: Negotiate a referral arrangement at the corporate partner level (Patterson or Schein as a company relationship, not individual rep), with a bulk supply discount or preferred vendor agreement for SGA practices as the exchange.

**Partner Type D: Dental-Specific Financial Advisors and CPAs**

Commission model: **Education and event partnership model** (referral fees to CPAs for referring clients are ethically restricted in most states; the structure must be a business development relationship, not a commission)

- Co-host CE-credit webinars: "The DSO Tax and Equity Planning Workshop" -- SGA provides DSO expertise, the CPA firm provides tax and estate planning content. Both parties present their value. Qualified dentists in attendance become warm prospects for both.
- CPA firm receives SGA co-branding on the webinar, promotion to SGA's partner network, and a featured slot in SGA's "Trusted Advisors" directory on the partner portal.
- No direct referral fee; the business development value is the co-promotion and network access.

**Partner Type E: Dental Attorneys**

Same ethical constraint structure as CPAs. Use educational co-presentation model: "The Practice Transition Legal and Partnership Planning Workshop."

## Section 3: Community Growth Strategy

### 3.1 Platform Selection

The right platform for SGA's professional community is not determined by what is trending. It is determined by where dentists already are, and whether the community can serve a single primary business purpose with measurable outcomes.

**Running the Community Validation Formula:**

For a dental professional community focused on practice owners and associates:
- Size of target market: 6/10 (202,000 licensed dentists in the US; approximately 30,000-50,000 active on professional platforms; a significant but not massive addressable community)
- Level of competition: 3/10 (a few Facebook groups exist for dental professionals; Dentaltown is the legacy forum; LinkedIn is fragmented; no organized DSO-centric peer community exists)
- Level of passion: 8/10 (dentists have strong professional identity; the "protecting dentistry" narrative resonates deeply; professional isolation is a real and acknowledged pain)

Demand score: (6/3) x 8 = 16. Strong opportunity. The gap in organized DSO-centric peer community for clinician-owned practice dentists is real and unoccupied.

**Platform Recommendation: Two-Tier Architecture**

| Tier | Platform | Audience | Purpose (SPACE) |
|------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Primary (inner circle)** | Private LinkedIn Group ("The SGA Partner Network") | Current SGA partners, invited prospects in evaluation | Acquisition/Advocacy -- peer-to-peer conversations that collapse the B2B trust cycle |
| **Secondary (open community)** | Dedicated Facebook Group ("The Dental Practice Independence Forum") | Any practice owner or dentist interested in sustainable independent-spirit dentistry | Acquisition (top of funnel) -- content-led thought leadership community that attracts SGA's target prospect organically |

**Why not Discord or Circle?** The Dr. Mark Hendricks persona (45-60 year old practice owner) is not navigating Discord. Facebook Groups have 70%+ adult penetration and dentists in this demographic already use Facebook for professional content consumption. For the inner-circle function, LinkedIn Groups are native to where these dentists already are for professional networking.

**Why not a proprietary platform (Mighty Networks, Circle)?** Not yet. Building an owned community platform requires critical mass before the cost and complexity is justified. Start where the audience already lives. Graduate to a proprietary platform when the community has 1,000+ active members and the network effects are established.

**SPACE Model Alignment:**

Primary purpose: **Acquisition and Advocacy** -- the community's primary business function is to convert engaged members into partnership inquiries and create advocates who recruit their peers.

This means every community metric is evaluated against this purpose. Raw member counts are vanity. The metrics that matter: partnership inquiries attributed to community engagement, member-to-member referral introductions, and engagement rate among members who are within 12 months of a transition decision.

### 3.2 Community Value Proposition

Why would a mid-career practice owner invest time in an SGA-affiliated community? The honest answer is: they would not join an SGA community. They would join a community that serves their actual needs, and SGA facilitates that community.

**Community positioning: Not an SGA sales channel. A peer network for dentists who believe that the profession should be run by dentists.**

"The Dental Practice Independence Forum is a private community for dental practice owners who are determined to practice on their own terms -- whether that means remaining independent, partnering thoughtfully, or building toward a transition that doesn't sacrifice everything they built."

This positioning serves SGA's long game: the community is genuinely valuable regardless of whether members partner with SGA. Members who don't partner become advocates who send referrals. Members who do partner arrived pre-convinced.

**Membership criteria for open Facebook Group:**
- Licensed dentist or dental practice owner
- Practice owners preferred; associates and office managers accepted
- No DSO sales pitches or recruiting (from anyone, including SGA)

**Membership criteria for private LinkedIn Group:**
- Current SGA partners (automatic invitation at onboarding)
- Prospects in SGA's BD pipeline (invited at the "Engage" phase of their evaluation process)
- Alumni from Pikos Institute and 4Front Symposium (invited post-event)

### 3.5 Community-to-Partnership Conversion Paths

The community's job is to move a dentist from "SGA-curious" to "actively evaluating partnership" without a hard sales push. The conversion paths are designed to feel like natural progression, not a funnel.

**Path 1: Facebook Group to Partner Story Request**
- Dentist engages with a partner story post
- Community manager sends a personal DM: "I noticed you've been reading about Dr. [PARTNER NAME]'s story. Would it be helpful to talk directly to them?"
- Introduction facilitated; BD team follows up with the prospect's permission

**Path 2: LinkedIn Group to Business Development Call**
- Prospect invited to the LinkedIn inner-circle group at the "Engage" phase of BD outreach
- Exposure to peer conversations builds pre-sales trust over 30-90 days
- BD team monitors engagement signals (comments, post reactions) to identify when a prospect is in high-consideration mode
- At the right moment: "You've been part of our inner circle for a while -- I'd love to set up a call with Dr. [PARTNER] in your specialty to share their experience directly."

**Path 3: Monthly Live Q&A to Personal Conversation**
- Dentist attends a live Q&A session and asks a question
- Community manager captures the name and follows up with a personalized resource: "Based on the question you asked tonight about [topic], I thought this case study of Dr. [PARTNER] might be relevant."
- Follow-up email introduces BD contact as a resource for further questions

**Path 4: Ambassador-Facilitated Introduction**
- Ambassador recognizes a community member as a potential fit (by geography, specialty, or expressed pain points)
- Ambassador makes a personal introduction: "I've been following your comments here for a few weeks. I'm an SGA partner -- would you be interested in talking about my experience?"
- This is the most powerful path because it is peer-to-peer with no SGA involvement at the initiation

## Section 5: Channel Integration Matrix

### 5.1 How the Growth Channels Work Together

The four growth channels are not independent programs. They form a reinforcing system where each channel feeds the others:

```
REFERRAL PROGRAMS
(Partners refer peers; patients refer patients)
         |
         | Warm introductions enter the system
         v
PARTNER/AFFILIATE NETWORK
(CPAs, brokers, coaches pre-qualify and route prospects)
         |
         | Credibility-validated leads enter BD pipeline
         v
PROFESSIONAL COMMUNITY
(Facebook Group + LinkedIn Group nurture evaluation-stage prospects)
         |
         | Community trust accelerates partnership decisions
         v
INFLUENCER CAMPAIGNS
(KOLs, podcasters, creators reach audiences not yet in the system)
         |
         | New audiences enter the referral and community loops
         ^
         |
         +--(loop back: new partners and patients become referrers)
```

**The Three Loops:**

Loop 1 (Practice Acquisition): Influencer podcast appearance reaches dentist who starts following SGA on LinkedIn. Dentist joins the Facebook community. Community content builds trust over 60-90 days. Dental CPA affiliate introduces SGA at the right moment. Dentist requests an intro to a current partner. Referral track converts the peer conversation to a BD call. Partnership closes. New partner joins the referral program.

Loop 2 (Associate Recruitment): Dental career creator posts about working at an SGA practice. New graduate follows SGA, checks DentalPost profile. Applies for a position. Accepts. 30 days in, receives referral program invitation. Refers two classmates from dental school. Both join SGA practices.

Loop 3 (Patient Acquisition): Linda Dawson sees a micro-influencer's transformation Reel on Facebook. Clicks through to YourSmileDestination.com. Books a virtual consultation. Accepts full-arch treatment. Receives referral program invitation 30 days post-treatment. Refers her sister. Sister refers a coworker. Each referral strengthens the network effect.

### 5.2 Lead Handoff Protocols

**Protocol A: Community to Business Development**

Trigger: Community manager identifies a member who has engaged 5+ times with partner stories, asked a question about the partnership process, or been active in the community for 60+ days.

Handoff point: At virtual consultation, practice team has access to referral source (influencer handle + promo code) to personalize the conversation: "We saw you came across us through [creator's name]. Did their story resonate with what you're experiencing?"

**Protocol D: Referral Link Click to BD Outreach (B2B)**
