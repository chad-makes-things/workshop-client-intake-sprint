# SGA Dental Partners -- Social Media Growth Strategy

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Scope:** YouTube, LinkedIn, Facebook/Instagram, TikTok
**Core Asset:** Dr. Grant Olson's Innovative Dental YouTube channel (138K subscribers, 23M+ views)
**Audiences:** B2C (implant patients, general dental patients), B2B (practice owners, associate dentists, specialist recruits)

---

## Strategic Foundation

SGA Dental operates across three audience segments with fundamentally different platform behaviors. Practice acquisition targets (dentists considering partnership) live on LinkedIn and industry podcasts. Recruitment targets (associate dentists) respond to Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. Patient acquisition targets respond to YouTube transformations, Facebook community content, and local Instagram presence. The unifying asset across all segments is Dr. Grant Olson's content library and personal brand, which gives SGA something no competing DSO possesses: an authentic, high-authority clinical voice with a built-in audience of 138,000 subscribers and 23 million views.

Every platform strategy below is designed around three principles:

1. **Platform-native execution.** What works on YouTube fails on LinkedIn. Each platform gets content shaped for its algorithm and culture.
2. **Owned audience conversion.** Social followers are rented. Every strategy includes a clear path from follower to email subscriber, consultation request, or partnership inquiry.
3. **Content pyramid efficiency.** Dr. Olson's long-form YouTube content is the pillar. Everything else -- Shorts, Reels, TikToks, LinkedIn posts, Facebook clips -- is atomized from that pillar. One production day feeds five platforms.

## YouTube Strategy (Primary Platform)

### The Opportunity

Dr. Grant Olson's Innovative Dental channel is SGA's single most valuable marketing asset. At 138K subscribers and 23M+ views, it already outperforms every DSO's organic content presence. The channel has proven viral mechanics: patient transformation reveals generate deep emotional engagement, educational content captures high-intent search traffic ("dental implant cost," "All-on-4 before and after"), and Dr. O's casual, approachable delivery style ("What's up, guys? This is Dr. O from Innovative Dental") stands in stark contrast to the sterile corporate content that competitors produce.

The strategic question is not whether YouTube works. It is how to evolve a personal clinical channel into a multi-purpose brand engine that drives patient acquisition, dentist recruitment, and practice acquisition simultaneously -- without diluting the authentic voice that made it successful.

### Channel Architecture

Maintain Dr. Grant Olson's Innovative Dental as the primary channel. Do not rebrand it to SGA Dental. The personal brand is the asset. Instead, use the channel as the content flagship that feeds the broader SGA ecosystem. Add a secondary SGA Dental Partners channel for corporate-facing content (partner testimonials, culture videos, recruitment content), but expect it to grow slowly while Dr. O's channel does the heavy lifting.

### Content Pillars (5)

**Pillar 1: Patient Transformation Reveals (40% of content)**
The highest-performing content category on the channel. Full-arch reveals, smile makeovers, and before/after moments. These drive views, shares, and patient inquiries from across the country. The "steak moment" -- where Dr. O grills a steak for a patient after their implant procedure to prove the teeth work -- is the signature format. Optimize these for emotional payoff: show the patient's pain state, the procedure in brief, and then the reveal. End with a clear CTA to YourSmileDestination.com for virtual consultations.

Target formats: 8-15 minute long-form (high watch time, algorithm favor) and 30-60 second Shorts extracted from the reveal moment.

**Pillar 2: Dental Education and Myth-Busting (25% of content)**
Capture high-intent search traffic. Topics like "How much do dental implants actually cost?", "Permanent teeth in 24 hours -- is it real?", "What your dentist isn't telling you about dentures." Dr. O already runs this content effectively (the "permanent teeth in 24 hours is a lie" Short earned strong engagement). This pillar builds authority and captures patients at the research stage.

Target formats: 5-10 minute explainers (search-optimized titles), 15-30 second myth-busting Shorts.

**Pillar 3: Live Interactive Content (15% of content)**
Dr. O has signaled this shift for 2026: "You're going to see a lot less canned videos, edited videos... what you're going to see is a lot of live content." Weekly or biweekly live Q&A sessions where patients can ask questions in real time. This builds trust at scale -- a prospective patient watching Dr. O answer questions live for 45 minutes gets more trust-building than any advertisement could deliver. Archive lives as long-form videos with timestamps.

Target formats: 45-60 minute live streams, clipped into 5-10 Shorts per session.

**Pillar 4: Behind-the-Scenes and Practice Culture (10% of content)**
Office tours, team introductions, day-in-the-life content, technology showcases (CEREC, guided implant surgery, 3D imaging). This content serves dual purposes: patients see the spa-like environment and feel comfortable traveling to Springfield, and dentists see the practice infrastructure and become interested in the MODIS/SGA model.

Target formats: 3-5 minute tours, 15-30 second Shorts of technology in action.

**Pillar 5: Dentist-to-Dentist Content (10% of content)**
Clinical education, case presentations, and "how I approach this case" walkthroughs. This content targets the recruitment and acquisition audiences. When Dr. O walks through a complex full-arch case, every periodontist and implant dentist watching is evaluating both his clinical skill and his practice infrastructure. Monthly "Smiles in the Wild" podcast episodes featuring MODIS partner dentists discussing cases and their partnership experience serve the B2B funnel.

Target formats: 10-20 minute case presentations, podcast-style conversations.

### Shorts Strategy vs. Long-Form Strategy

Long-form (5-20 minutes) is the subscriber conversion engine. YouTube's algorithm rewards watch time, and long-form content builds the deep trust that converts a viewer into a patient who will travel across the country to Springfield. Optimize long-form for search: titles with specific keywords ("How Much Do Full Arch Dental Implants Cost in 2026?"), custom thumbnails with before/after split frames and Dr. O's face showing strong emotion, and descriptions with lead magnet links in the first three lines.

Shorts (15-60 seconds) are the discovery engine. They feed the algorithm's recommendation system, appear on the Shorts shelf, and introduce Dr. O to new audiences who would never search for a 15-minute dental video. Extract the highest-emotion moments from long-form content: the reveal reaction, the first bite of steak, the "permanent teeth in 24 hours is a lie" debunks. Every Short should end with a verbal CTA: "Follow for more transformations" or "Full video on the channel."

Target cadence: 2 long-form videos per week, 4-5 Shorts per week.

### Subscriber-to-Patient Conversion

The conversion path is already proven -- patients find Dr. O on YouTube, visit YourSmileDestination.com, book a virtual consultation, and travel to Springfield. Optimize this funnel:

- **Description links:** YourSmileDestination.com in the first line of every video description. Second link to a "Dental Implant Cost Guide" PDF that captures email addresses.
- **Pinned comments:** "Ready to start your smile journey? Book a free virtual consult: [link]" pinned on every transformation video.
- **End screens:** Subscribe button plus a card linking to the most relevant next video (keep viewers in session).
- **Comment trigger:** In live streams, announce "Type SMILE in the chat and we'll send you our free implant cost guide" -- capture emails through a ManyChat or equivalent integration.
- **Retargeting:** Build custom audiences from YouTube viewers for Meta and Google retargeting campaigns driving to consultation bookings.

### Subscriber-to-Dentist-Recruit Conversion

The Pillar 5 (dentist-to-dentist) content serves as top-of-funnel for recruitment. Dentists watching case presentations are self-selecting as clinically ambitious -- exactly the profile SGA wants. Conversion tactics:

- **Description links:** "Interested in practicing with a team like this? Explore MODIS partnership: [link]" in every clinical education video.
- **Partner spotlight series:** Monthly videos featuring MODIS partner dentists discussing what changed after joining. Position these as peer testimonials, not recruitment ads.
- **Conference content:** Record talks from the 4Front symposium and Pikos Institute courses. Post as long-form content. Dentists who engage with this content are pre-qualified recruitment leads.

## LinkedIn Strategy (B2B -- Practice Owners and Dentists)

### The Opportunity

LinkedIn is the only platform where SGA can target dentists by job title, practice ownership status, specialty, geography, and career stage. DSOs are dramatically underinvesting on LinkedIn relative to the opportunity. Most dental industry LinkedIn activity is organic thought leadership from individual consultants, not systematic campaigns from DSOs. SGA can own this space by deploying the personal brands of its leadership -- Lamonte Jensen, Dr. Mitch Ellingson, Pete Wong, and Dr. Grant Olson -- as the primary content vehicles, supported by the SGA Dental Partners company page.

The audience here splits into two segments: practice owners evaluating partnership (the acquisition funnel) and dentists evaluating career moves (the recruitment funnel). Both segments browse LinkedIn professionally, are receptive to thought leadership, and respond to peer stories over corporate messaging.

### Content Pillars (4)

**Pillar 1: Industry Thought Leadership (35% of content)**
Lamonte Jensen and Dr. Mitch Ellingson publish perspective pieces on the DSO industry, practice transitions, and the future of dentistry. This is not content marketing -- it is genuine thought leadership that positions SGA as the most informed voice in the space. Topics: "Why 7 out of 10 dentists regret their DSO sale (and what we learned from it)," "The 4th generation of dental consolidation," "What medicine's consolidation teaches us about dentistry's future," "Why the ADA president spoke at a DSO event for the first time."

Format: LinkedIn native text posts (1,200-1,500 characters for optimal algorithm performance), occasional long-form articles for evergreen topics. Posting from personal profiles, not the company page -- LinkedIn's algorithm favors personal content 5-10x over company content.

**Pillar 2: Partner Stories and Proof (25% of content)**
Named partner testimonials with specific numbers. Dr. Matt Cavendish's story (revenues up 16.5%, EBITA beating plan, 100% staff retention) is the template. These are not promotional posts -- they are narrative case studies that speak to the exact fears and desires of the target audience. Format: carousel posts with before/after metrics, short video testimonials (60-90 seconds native upload), and quote graphic posts.

**Pillar 3: Clinical Innovation Showcases (20% of content)**
Dr. Grant Olson shares clinical content from YouTube repackaged for a professional audience. A patient transformation that plays as emotional content on YouTube becomes a clinical case presentation on LinkedIn. Dr. Michael Pikos shares implantology thought leadership. This content attracts specialist dentists (periodontists, oral surgeons) who are the MODIS acquisition targets.

**Pillar 4: Culture and Recruitment (20% of content)**
Day-in-the-life content from SGA practices, CE opportunity highlights (Pikos Institute, 4Front symposium), associate career pathway explanations, and employee testimonials. The Gen4 promise -- "We want to take each individual where they want to go so that it's not just a job; it is a career" -- is the messaging anchor. This content targets Dr. Priya Nair (the associate dentist persona) and her peers.

### Posting Frequency and Timing

Personal profiles (Jensen, Ellingson, Olson, Wong): 3-4 posts per week each. Post between 7-8 AM local time on Tuesday through Thursday for peak engagement. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistency -- posting 3x per week for 12 weeks will produce significantly more reach than posting daily for 4 weeks and stopping.

Company page: 2-3 posts per week. Company page content should amplify personal posts (reshare with commentary) and host job listings, event announcements, and formal press releases.

### Employee Advocacy Program

SGA has 60+ practice locations with hundreds of dentists and team members. An employee advocacy program turns this into a distribution network. The program structure:

1. Create a shared content library (Google Drive or a tool like GaggleAMP) with pre-written LinkedIn posts and suggested copy that practice partners can personalize and share.
2. Monthly "content drops" tied to SGA initiatives: a new partner announcement, a 4Front symposium recap, a clinical innovation showcase.
3. Recognize the most active advocates -- feature their posts in internal communications, invite them to participate in content creation.
4. Start small: recruit 10-15 engaged partners as founding advocates. Scale based on results.

The goal is not forced corporate sharing. It is equipping dentists who already want to build their professional brand with content that happens to reinforce SGA's narrative.

### Company Page vs. Personal Brand Strategy

Personal brands drive 80% of the LinkedIn strategy. Lamonte Jensen is the "strategic visionary" voice. Dr. Mitch Ellingson is the "clinical protector" voice. Dr. Grant Olson is the "clinical innovator" voice. Pete Wong is the "community builder" voice. The company page aggregates and amplifies but does not lead.

This mirrors how LinkedIn's algorithm works: personal content gets 5-10x the organic reach of company content. It also mirrors how the target audience makes decisions -- dentists trust other dentists, not corporate brands.

### Follower-to-Lead Conversion

- **Profile optimization:** Each leader's LinkedIn profile includes a custom "Featured" section with a link to the SGA partnership inquiry page or a gated resource ("The Practice Owner's Guide to DSO Partnerships").
- **Comment engagement:** When a dentist comments on a thought leadership post with a question about transitions, respond publicly (builds social proof) and then follow up via DM with a personalized message and relevant resource.
- **LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms:** For paid Thought Leader Ads, use Lead Gen Forms (6-10% conversion rate, double external landing pages) to capture partnership inquiries directly.
- **InMail for acquisition targets:** Identified high-value practice owners receive personalized InMail from Marcus Morosin (MODIS VP Business Development) or the SGA business development team. The message references specific content they engaged with: "I noticed you commented on Dr. Ellingson's post about clinical autonomy. Would love to share how that plays out in practice -- happy to connect you with a partner in your area."

## Facebook/Instagram Strategy (Local Patient Acquisition)

### The Opportunity

Facebook and Instagram serve patient acquisition at the local practice level. Facebook reaches 70%+ of US adults and is the primary social platform for the Linda Dawson persona (the 64-year-old implant patient considering full-arch treatment). Instagram reaches the younger demographic and is the discovery platform for dental transformation content. The strategic challenge is managing social presence across 60+ practice locations while maintaining quality and brand coherence.

SGA's competitive advantage here is Dr. Grant Olson's content library. Repurposing his YouTube transformation content -- the reveals, the before/after moments, the steak grilling -- into Meta-native formats gives SGA authentic, high-emotion creative that no templated corporate dental ad can match.

### Central Brand vs. Local Pages

Operate a **hub-and-spoke model**. The SGA Dental Partners Facebook and Instagram pages serve as the central hub for brand-level content, recruitment posts, and the MODIS implant vertical. Each local practice maintains its own Facebook page (and Instagram where appropriate) for community-specific content, reviews, and patient engagement.

The central pages post: Dr. Olson transformation content, recruitment and career content, major announcements, and educational content.

Local pages post: team photos, community involvement (Little League sponsorships, school dental health days), patient reviews and testimonials, appointment availability, seasonal promotions, and office updates.

Content flows one direction: central creates high-production assets (transformation videos, educational graphics, branded templates) that local offices customize and post. Local offices do not need to create video content from scratch -- they receive a monthly content kit with ready-to-post assets.

### Content Pillars (5)

**Pillar 1: Transformation Content (30%)**
Before/after photos and videos from implant and cosmetic cases. On Facebook, these post as native video (60-90 seconds) with patient story captions. On Instagram, they post as Reels (15-30 seconds of the reveal moment) and carousels (swipeable before/after comparisons). The emotional arc is consistent: show the pain, show the process briefly, reveal the result.

**Pillar 2: Patient Education (20%)**
Myth-busting, procedure explanations, cost transparency, and FAQ content. "3 things your dentist wants you to know about implants." "What actually happens during a dental cleaning." This content serves the Linda Dawson persona who is researching options on Facebook and needs simple, trustworthy information. Format: short video (under 60 seconds), infographic carousels, text posts with a clear CTA.

**Pillar 3: Community and Culture (20%)**
Local practice teams at community events, staff birthdays and milestones, holiday posts, practice anniversary celebrations. This is the content that makes a dental practice feel like a neighbor, not a corporation. This pillar is exclusively local -- the central brand page does not post birthday content. Format: photo posts, short casual video, Facebook Stories.

**Pillar 4: Social Proof and Reviews (20%)**
Patient testimonial videos, Google review highlights (screenshot + story behind it), "Patient of the Month" features. SGA practices collectively have thousands of reviews -- surfacing these on social media reinforces the trust that drives appointments. Innovative Dental's 3,390+ reviews are the benchmark. Format: quote graphics, short video testimonials (30-60 seconds), carousel posts featuring multiple reviews.

**Pillar 5: Conversion Content (10%)**
Direct calls to action. "Book your free consultation." "New patient special this month." "Call us today." This content is used sparingly so it does not overwhelm the feed, but it appears consistently so followers know how to take the next step. Format: story ads, single image posts with clear CTA buttons, boosted posts targeting local audiences.

### Reel/Short-Form Video Strategy

Instagram Reels and Facebook Reels are the discovery engines on Meta platforms. The strategy mirrors YouTube Shorts: extract the highest-emotion 15-30 second moments from Dr. Olson's long-form content and post natively.

Reel formats that perform in dental:
- **The Reveal:** Patient sees their new smile for the first time. Camera on their face. Reaction is the content. No narration needed.
- **The Before/After Split:** Side-by-side or transition effect showing the transformation. Text overlay with context ("20 years with dentures --> permanent teeth in one day").
- **The Myth Bust:** Dr. O looks at camera, states a common myth, debunks it in under 30 seconds. Direct, confident, slightly provocative.
- **The Process Timelapse:** Sped-up footage of a procedure from start to finish. Satisfying to watch. Demystifies the process for anxious patients.

Target cadence: Central brand pages post 4-5 Reels per week. Local practice pages post 1-2 Reels per week (using central content kit assets).

### Review Generation Integration

Social media and reviews form a reinforcing loop. After a patient appointment, the practice sends a follow-up text/email asking for a Google review. If the patient leaves a positive review, a second message invites them to share their experience on the practice's Facebook page or tag the practice on Instagram. The social team monitors for these mentions and reshares them, creating a continuous stream of social proof.

For implant patients specifically: Dr. Olson's team already captures video testimonials. These become Reels, story posts, and Facebook posts that demonstrate real outcomes to other prospective patients.

### Follower-to-Patient Conversion

- **Facebook Lead Ads:** Run local campaigns with before/after creative targeting adults 35-65 within a 15-mile radius of each practice. Lead form captures name, phone, and "What dental concern are you most interested in addressing?" Cost benchmark: $50-$80 per lead for general dentistry, $90-$130 for implant inquiries.
- **Instagram DM automation:** When a user comments on a transformation Reel with a question about cost or availability, auto-reply with a link to the consultation booking page. ManyChat or a similar tool handles the initial response; practice staff follows up personally.
- **Story CTAs:** Weekly Instagram and Facebook Stories with "Tap to book" link stickers pointing to the practice's online scheduling page.
- **Bio links:** Every local practice Instagram bio includes a Linktree or equivalent with: Book Appointment, See Our Reviews, Meet Our Team, New Patient Special.

### Posting Frequency and Timing

Central brand pages: 5-7 posts per week across Facebook and Instagram combined. Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 11 AM-1 PM and 7-9 PM local time (when patients browse during lunch and evening).

Local practice pages: 3-5 posts per week on Facebook, 2-3 on Instagram. Best times vary by market -- start with the general benchmarks above and optimize based on Insights data after 30 days.

## TikTok Strategy (Awareness and Recruitment)

### The Opportunity

TikTok is the highest-reach, lowest-cost discovery platform for dental transformation content. Dental before/after videos consistently go viral on TikTok -- the platform's algorithm rewards completion rate and shares, both of which dental reveals optimize for naturally. The audience skews younger (18-34), making TikTok ideal for two purposes: reaching younger implant patients and future patients who are building awareness now, and recruiting associate dentists (the Dr. Priya Nair persona) who consume TikTok daily.

SGA does not need to create TikTok-original content. The YouTube Shorts pipeline produces content that cross-posts directly to TikTok with minimal adaptation. The key difference is tone: TikTok rewards rawness, speed, and a slight edge of humor or provocation. The polished YouTube aesthetic gets dialed back. Dr. O's natural casual style ("What's up, guys?") already fits TikTok's culture.

### Content Pillars (4)

**Pillar 1: Transformation Reveals (40%)**
The same reveal moments extracted from YouTube, but formatted for TikTok's vertical full-screen experience. Use trending sounds when they fit naturally. No forced trend participation -- a genuine smile reveal does not need a dance trend layered on top.

**Pillar 2: Dental Myth-Busting and Hot Takes (25%)**
"Permanent teeth in 24 hours is a lie." "Stop doing this to your teeth." "Your dentist isn't telling you this." Slightly provocative, authoritative, fast-paced. These drive comments (agreement and disagreement both boost the algorithm) and shares. Dr. O delivers these direct-to-camera in under 30 seconds.

**Pillar 3: Day-in-the-Life and Behind-the-Scenes (20%)**
"A day at a dental practice that looks nothing like a dental practice." Office tours of Innovative Dental's spa-like environment. Team interactions. The CEREC machine carving a crown in real time. This content serves recruitment by showing associate dentists what it looks like to work at an SGA practice.

**Pillar 4: Dentist Career Content (15%)**
"What they don't teach you in dental school." "Day in the life of a new associate dentist." "Why I left corporate dentistry." This content targets Dr. Priya Nair and her peers directly. Feature associate dentists from SGA practices sharing authentic experiences. This is recruitment content disguised as career advice content -- the most effective kind.

### Hashtag Strategy

Primary hashtags (use on every post): #DentalImplants #SmileMakeover #DentalTransformation #Dentist #InnovativeDental

Secondary hashtags (rotate based on content): #AllOn4 #DentalImplantCost #NewTeeth #SmileReveal #BeforeAndAfter #DentistLife #DentalSchool #DSO #DayInTheLife

Trending hashtags: Participate only when the trend naturally fits. A trending sound that works with a reveal moment is worth using. A forced dance trend attached to a clinical video is not.

### Cross-Posting from YouTube Shorts

The production pipeline runs: Long-form YouTube video --> extract 4-5 short clips --> edit as YouTube Shorts (with YouTube watermark/format) --> re-export without watermark for TikTok and Reels. TikTok's algorithm penalizes content with other platforms' watermarks. Always re-export clean versions.

### Follower-to-Lead Conversion

TikTok's conversion infrastructure is less mature than Meta's. The path is:

1. **Bio link:** TikTok allows one bio link. Use a Linktree-style page with: "Book a Free Virtual Consult," "Download Our Implant Cost Guide," and "Careers at SGA Dental."
2. **Comment engagement:** When users ask "How much does this cost?" in comments (they will, on every transformation video), reply with a comment that includes context and directs them to the bio link. Pin helpful cost-related comments.
3. **TikTok Lead Generation ads:** For the implant vertical, run lead gen campaigns targeting 35-55 adults interested in dental care, dentures, or cosmetic procedures. Creative: the best-performing organic transformation Reels repurposed as paid ads.

### Posting Frequency and Timing

5-7 TikToks per week. Post between 7-9 AM and 6-8 PM for peak engagement. Consistency matters more than volume -- 5 posts per week every week outperforms 15 posts one week and none the next.

## Cross-Platform Content Production System

### The Content Pyramid

Dr. Grant Olson's weekly production schedule generates content for every platform:

```
PILLAR: 2 long-form YouTube videos per week (10-20 min each)
    |
    |--> 4-5 YouTube Shorts (extracted reveal moments, myth busts)
    |--> 4-5 TikToks (re-exported without watermark, TikTok-native captions)
    |--> 4-5 Instagram Reels (same clips, IG-optimized aspect ratio and captions)
    |--> 4-5 Facebook Reels (cross-posted from IG with platform-native upload)
    |--> 2-3 LinkedIn video clips (clinical case angle for professional audience)
    |--> 5-10 still images (thumbnail frames, before/after stills for carousels)
    |--> Quote graphics (patient testimonial quotes pulled from video)
```

One production day yields 25-35 pieces of platform-specific content. This is sustainable because the creation cost is front-loaded in the YouTube recording, and repurposing is a systematic editing workflow, not new creative development.

### Weekly Production Schedule

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Plan: Review analytics, select topics for the week, outline scripts |
| Tuesday | Record: Film 2 long-form videos and any standalone Shorts |
| Wednesday | Edit: Long-form editing, Short/Reel extraction, thumbnail creation |
| Thursday | Distribute: Schedule all platform content, write captions, set up ads |
| Friday | Engage: Heavy engagement day -- reply to all comments across platforms, DM follow-ups, community management |

### Engagement Optimization Playbook (All Platforms)

**First 30 minutes after posting:** Reply to every comment. YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn algorithms all boost content that generates early engagement. Dr. O or a dedicated community manager should be available for the first half hour after each post goes live.

**Daily engagement routine (30 minutes total):**
- Reply to all new comments across platforms
- Respond to DMs (prioritize patient inquiries and professional networking)
- Engage on 5-10 posts from target accounts (dental influencers, industry leaders, potential partners)
- Reshare any user-generated content that tags SGA or Innovative Dental

**Weekly engagement actions:**
- Feature one patient comment or review in a Story post
- Respond to any negative comments or reviews within 24 hours with empathy and a resolution path
- Identify the top 3 commenters of the week and engage with their profiles directly

### Viral and Shareability Mechanics

The content formats that drive organic virality across platforms:

1. **The Reveal Reaction:** Patient sees new smile for the first time. Universally shareable because the emotional reaction transcends dental interest. People share it because it makes them feel something.
2. **The Myth Bust:** "Permanent teeth in 24 hours is a lie." Provocative claims drive shares from people who agree (validation) and people who disagree (debate). Both boost algorithm signals.
3. **The Steak Moment:** Dr. O grilling a steak for a patient post-procedure. Visually unexpected (a dentist at a grill), emotionally powerful (proving the implants work with real food), and narratively complete in 15 seconds.
4. **The Hidden Problem Reveal:** 3D imaging reveals hidden infection or decay that standard X-rays missed. The "gross-out" factor drives shares and saves. The educational value justifies the attention.
5. **Tag-a-Friend Prompts:** "Tag someone who deserves a new smile" on transformation posts. Simple mechanic that expands reach through personal networks.
