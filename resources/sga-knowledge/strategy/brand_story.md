## Part 1: StoryBrand BrandScripts

### BrandScript A: Practice Owners

**THE CHARACTER**

A mid-career dentist who built a thriving practice from nothing. They are proud, community-rooted, and fiercely protective of their name, their team, and the way they practice. They want financial security, operational relief, and a peer community -- without surrendering the identity they spent decades building.

*Aspirational identity:* A respected clinician who is free to focus on the craft, financially secure through a diversified portfolio, and part of a collaborative network of high-performing peers.

*What they want:* A liquidity event and operational partnership that amplifies their practice instead of absorbing it.

**THE PROBLEM**

*External problem:* The business side of dentistry is crushing them. Payroll, HR, compliance, insurance claims, supply ordering, recruiting, technology upgrades -- they are the dentist, the HR department, the IT guy, and the office therapist. They did not go to dental school for any of it.

*Internal problem:* Isolation and quiet fear. They feel like they are falling behind -- younger dentists know digital workflows they have never touched, and they cannot afford the time or money to retool alone. Underneath the confidence, they are tired. They have started googling "sell dental practice" at midnight and wondering whether the best years are behind them. As one practice owner put it: "I'm the dentist, the HR department, the IT guy, and the therapist for my whole staff. I didn't sign up for any of that."

*Philosophical problem:* Dentists should not have to choose between clinical excellence and business survival. A career dedicated to patient care should not end in burnout, declining valuation, and an exit that feels like surrender.

*The villain:* The broken DSO model -- cookie-cutter corporate dentistry that promises support and delivers standardization, production quotas, and the slow erasure of everything a practice owner built. "Seven or eight out of 10 are pretty bugged or annoyed or frustrated or regretful. It's not what they thought."

**THE GUIDE**

*Empathy:* "We know what it feels like to carry the weight of a practice alone. To spend Sunday nights fixing the schedule because your office manager quit. To wonder if there is a way out that does not mean selling your soul. We have been on your side of this conversation, and we built SGA because we refused to add to the regret statistics."

*Authority:*
- The Thurston Group: 38 years, 8+ dental and healthcare platforms, $4B+ returned to partners
- 100+ partner practices across 10+ states
- 85% staff retention rate (no competitor publishes this metric)
- Pikos Institute: world-renowned implantology education center
- 4Front Symposium: annual peer-learning event for partner clinicians
- Dr. Mitch Ellingson, CCO and practicing dentist: "I've personally experienced it. I've been through the transition of going from private-practice dentist to working inside a group, and it is really hard."

**THE PLAN**

*The SGA Partnership Path:*

1. **Explore.** Schedule a confidential conversation with our partnership team. No brokers, no pressure. Just a direct conversation between dentists about what a partnership could look like for your specific practice. We evaluate mutual fit -- not just financials, but culture, clinical philosophy, and vision.

2. **Partner.** Walk through a transparent, three-step evaluation: connect, engage, and determine mutual fit. Receive an honest valuation. Structure a joint-venture equity partnership where you retain an ownership stake in the combined SGA platform -- your upside does not end at closing.

3. **Thrive.** Keep your name, your team, your patients, your way of practicing. SGA handles billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing, and recruiting behind the scenes. Get paired with a clinical mentor through the Individual Growth Engine. Attend the 4Front Symposium and connect with 100+ peer clinicians. Focus on what you became a dentist to do.

**CALL TO ACTION**

*Direct CTA:* Schedule a Confidential Conversation

*Transitional CTA:* Download the Practice Partnership Assessment -- a self-evaluation tool that helps practice owners determine whether they are ready for a partnership conversation and what questions to ask any DSO.

**FAILURE**

If they do not act, here is what awaits:

- Burnout accelerates. The 50-hour weeks become 55, then 60. The joy of clinical work fades further. The Sunday-night dread becomes a daily companion.
- Practice valuation declines. Without capital investment in technology and facilities, the practice becomes less competitive and less valuable every year. The exit they imagined at 60 becomes a fire sale at 65.
- Staff leaves for DSOs. The best hygienists and associates get recruited away by organizations that offer benefits, 401(k) plans, and career development paths a solo practice cannot match.
- Isolation deepens. No peer community. No mentorship. No one to call when a complex case lands on the chair or when a staffing crisis hits. Just them, alone, carrying everything.
- The profession consolidates around them. Corporate DSOs continue acquiring practices in their market. Their referral network shrinks. Their patients start seeing ads for offices with newer technology and longer hours. The question shifts from "should I partner?" to "can anyone still afford to partner with me?"

**SUCCESS**

*External success:* Revenue growth through operational efficiency, technology investment, and marketing support. Financial security through a diversified equity position in a multi-state platform. Time reclaimed -- evenings and weekends belong to family, not QuickBooks.

*Internal success:* Rejuvenation. The spark comes back. As Dr. Matt Cavendish described it after 20 years: "When you've been in dentistry for 20 years like I have, sometimes it's not as fun as it used to be. So to be able to do a complex case like this, with somebody who is also working at a very high skill level -- it is fun." Pride in belonging to something larger without losing what they built. The knowledge that their legacy is protected.

*Status success:* Recognized as a clinical leader within a network of high-performing peers. Not a cog in a corporate machine, but a respected partner whose voice shapes the direction of the organization. Dr. Ellingson's promise: "We're going to ask you to lead more. We're going to amplify your voice. Your ideas are going to influence everyone."

**ONE-LINER**

*Problem:* Most dentists who sell to a DSO end up frustrated and regretful.
*Solution:* SGA is a partnership of dentists invested in each other -- backed by a PE firm that has done this 8 times and returned $4B+ to partners.
*Result:* You keep your name, your team, and your way of practicing -- and you get the operational support, peer community, and equity upside you cannot build alone.

*Spoken version:* "Most dentists who partner with a DSO regret it within 18 months. SGA was built by dentists who heard that number and refused to add to it. We give practice owners operational relief, a peer community of 100+ clinicians, and equity in a platform backed by 38 years of proven healthcare partnerships -- without touching their name, their team, or their clinical autonomy."

### BrandScript B: Associate Dentists

**THE PROBLEM**

*External problem:* Their current associateship is a production treadmill. 25-30 patients a day. 15 minutes per patient. No time for complex cases, no mentorship, no clinical development. They feel like a tooth mechanic, not a doctor.

*Internal problem:* Frustrated ambition and professional isolation. "I didn't go through four years of dental school plus residency to do nothing but Class IIs all day." They crave mentorship, complex cases, and someone to bounce ideas off of. They miss the collegial environment of residency. They see peers thriving on social media and wonder what they are doing wrong.

*Philosophical problem:* A dental career should be a path to mastery and ownership, not a dead-end job with a stethoscope. Young dentists deserve mentorship, not just a production quota.

**THE GUIDE**

*Empathy:* "We know what it feels like to be three years into your career and already wondering if this is all there is. To watch your skills atrophy while the schedule fills with bread-and-butter cases and no one asks what you want to learn next. You deserve more than 'competitive compensation and a great team.'"

*Authority:* Individual Growth Engine mentorship program. Pikos Institute access for implant training. Partner clinicians who teach, not just supervise. Immediate equity investment opportunity as a Class A shareholder -- not a vague "pathway to partnership" but a defined structure from day one. Associates who have transformed: "He relearned how to do implants and now he loves his profession, his patients, everything."

1. **Connect.** Apply to SGA and have a conversation about your clinical goals, not just your production capacity. We match associates with practices and mentors based on where you want your career to go.
2. **Grow.** Get paired with a clinical mentor. Access the Pikos Institute for advanced training. Expand your case mix beyond bread-and-butter into implants, complex restorative, and full-arch work. Invest in the company as a Class A shareholder from day one.
3. **Lead.** Build toward partnership with a defined timeline and real equity. Become the dentist you trained to be, inside an organization that measures success by clinical growth, not just production numbers.

*Direct CTA:* Explore Associate Opportunities

*Transitional CTA:* Watch: "How SGA Associates Build Careers, Not Just Paychecks" -- a video series featuring associate-to-partner journeys inside the SGA network.

**FAILURE**

Without a change: more years on the production treadmill. Skills atrophy. The debt stays. The dream of ownership fades into something that feels permanently out of reach. They watch peers who found the right environment flourish while they grind through another week of Class IIs and prophy checks with no mentorship, no growth, and no equity.

**SUCCESS**

Clinical mastery that expands every year. A mentor who invests in their development. The financial trajectory of an equity partner, not an hourly employee. A community of peers who challenge them and celebrate their wins. The pride of becoming the dentist they envisioned on the first day of dental school.

**ONE-LINER**

"Your career is not a production number. SGA pairs early-career dentists with clinical mentors, world-class CE through the Pikos Institute, and a real equity pathway -- so you can build toward the dentist you trained to be, not just the one the schedule demands."

## Part 2: Cult Branding Blueprint

### Brand Belief System

**What SGA stands FOR:**

- **Dentist-led care.** Clinical decisions are made by clinicians, not by MBAs in boardrooms. The people who do the work lead the work.
- **Continuous improvement.** "Improve 1% every day." Excellence is not a destination but a daily discipline, built through peer collaboration and relentless learning.
- **Community roots.** A dental practice is not a franchise unit. It is a community institution with a name, a team, and relationships that matter. Those things are sacred.
- **Clinical excellence through education.** The Pikos Institute, the 4Front Symposium, the Individual Growth Engine -- these are not benefits. They are the soul of the platform. Dentists come to SGA to get better.
- **The long game.** 38 years and counting. Building something that lasts, not flipping something for a quick return.

**What SGA stands AGAINST:**

- **Cookie-cutter corporate dentistry.** "Same supplies in the same place in every office." The Gen2 model that standardizes away everything that made a practice special.
- **Production quotas over patient care.** "The pressure to produce starts impacting the ethics of practice." When suits in boardrooms dictate treatment plans, patients suffer and dentists lose their professional identity.
- **Extractive PE models.** Load up on debt, squeeze margins, flip in three years, leave the dentists holding the bag. "The money, they have a clawback or they had some type of provision that they didn't know about."
- **The medicine trap.** "In medicine, as it consolidated it became less about the doctors and patients, and more about the organization." SGA exists to prevent this from happening to dentistry.
- **Isolation disguised as independence.** Solo practice is sold as freedom but experienced as loneliness. True freedom comes from having peers who challenge you and infrastructure that supports you.

---

### Community Identity Markers

**Language:**

- *"Fourth Generation"* -- Not just a name. A thesis. Gen1 was accounting. Gen2 was standardization. Gen3 was fragmentation. Gen4 is collaboration. Using this language signals that you understand the industry's evolution and have chosen the side of progress.
- *"Partnership of dentists invested in each other"* -- The double meaning is intentional. Financially invested (equity) and personally invested (mentorship, collaboration, peer accountability).
- *"Keep Smiling... it looks good on you"* -- Dr. Olson's signature sign-off. Casual, warm, human. The anti-corporate phrase. When a partner dentist uses it, they are signaling membership in a community that values authenticity over corporate polish.
- *"Get better, not just get bought"* -- The primary brand message, used by partners to explain why they chose SGA over competitors.
- *"Dentists teaching dentists"* -- The cultural DNA phrase. Describes the mentorship model and distinguishes SGA from DSOs where corporate trainers deliver standardized programs.
- *"Clinical freedom surrounded by the best of everything"* -- The tagline, used as both a promise and an identity marker.

**Symbols:**

- *The "4" in Gen4* -- The generational numbering itself is a symbol. It implies that anyone operating a Gen2 or Gen3 model is already behind. Partners who internalize this framework become natural evangelists because they have language to explain why their choice was forward-looking.
- *The Pikos Institute* -- Not just a training center. A pilgrimage site. For implant-focused dentists, attending Pikos is a credential, a rite of passage, and a community entry point.

**Rituals:**

- *4Front Symposium* -- The annual gathering. This is where the "goose bumps" happen -- where "senior clinicians brainstorming together naturally elevated the ideas together." The symposium functions as the tribal gathering: reinforcing shared identity, celebrating clinical wins, introducing new partners into the community, and generating the peer-to-peer energy that no corporate conference can replicate.
- *Individual Growth Engine pairings* -- The one-on-one mentorship ritual. When a new partner is matched with a clinical mentor, it is an initiation into the collaborative culture. Dr. Cavendish's story -- observing his first all-on-four, learning from Dr. Shanahan's lab relationships -- is the archetype.
- *Partner calls and clinical advisory board* -- Regular touchpoints that maintain community connection between annual events. The cadence creates habit, and habit creates belonging.

### Insider/Outsider Dynamic

**What makes SGA partners feel special vs. other DSO dentists:**

SGA partners are not employees. They are not franchisees. They are equity owners in a platform that was designed -- by dentists, for dentists -- to be the antithesis of corporate consolidation. The insider identity rests on several pillars:

1. **"We are not a DSO."** Dr. Ellingson's declaration -- "We tell them we're not a DSO. We're not playing by their rules. We are here to change dentistry" -- is the foundational insider statement. Every SGA partner can repeat this line and mean it. It draws a clear boundary: we are on this side; corporate dentistry is on that side.

2. **Access to Pikos and 4Front.** These are not open to the public in the way SGA partners experience them. The education platform creates exclusivity -- a partner's clinical trajectory accelerates in ways a solo practitioner or a Heartland associate simply cannot access.

3. **The "voice" promise.** "The main reason doctors come to Gen4 is because they realize they're still going to have a voice." In most DSOs, dentists are managed. In SGA, they are consulted, elevated, and asked to lead. That distinction is felt daily.

4. **The Thurston track record.** Partners know they are backed by a firm with $4B+ in returns across 8 platforms. This is not a first-time fund experimenting with dental. This knowledge -- shared among insiders, unknown to most outsiders -- creates confidence and a sense of being on the winning side.

5. **Named proof stories.** Dr. Godat, Dr. Cavendish, Dr. Olson -- these are not anonymous testimonials. They are named, visible, contactable partners. Insiders can call them. Outsiders can only read the quotes.

**The outsider experience (what other DSO dentists face):**

"Decisions being made about what your production is supposed to be by people wearing suits in a boardroom." Staff turnover. Brand erasure. Clawbacks and equity provisions they did not fully understand. "I wish I would have never done this" -- said by a dentist with $10 million in cash. The contrast is vivid and SGA partners are reminded of it regularly, not through SGA's marketing, but through the industry's own horror stories.

### Sacred Cows

What competitors do that SGA refuses to do:

1. **Rebrand acquired practices.** Aspen makes every office an Aspen office. SGA's answer: "Dental Partners of SW Georgia is still Dental Partners of SW Georgia. After 5 years. After 10 years." The local name is sacred.

2. **Dictate clinical protocols from corporate.** Heartland's Glassdoor reviews tell the story: "Focus too much on profit hidden behind 'crown days' or 'Invisalign days' pushing doctors to focus on higher cost treatment." SGA's answer: full clinical autonomy, protected by a CCO who is a practicing dentist.

3. **Treat the deal as the finish line.** Every DSO sells the transaction. Nobody tells the story of Year 2, Year 3, Year 5. SGA's answer: the post-deal transformation narrative -- Cavendish's rejuvenation, Godat's refocused career, the associate who "relearned how to do implants and now he loves his profession."

4. **Let non-clinicians lead clinicians.** Dr. Ellingson names the elephant: "Doctors are worried Operations are going to lead them." SGA's structural answer: dentists teach dentists, the clinical advisory board shapes policy, and the CCO is a practicing clinician who has personally lived the private-to-group transition.

5. **Flip and exit.** The PE fear -- "they load up on debt, squeeze margins, and flip the company in 3-5 years" -- is addressed by Thurston's 38-year track record. This is patient capital, not quick-flip capital.

## Part 3: Brand Story Assets

### Founder Story

**The Three Attempts**

The story of SGA Dental Partners does not start with a launch party or a press release. It starts with a pattern that took 37 years to recognize.

Patrick J. Haynes III founded the Thurston Group in 1988. Over the next three decades, the firm built healthcare platforms the way most private equity firms do -- acquire, optimize, scale, return capital. They were good at it. More than $4 billion returned to partners across the firm's history. Smile Doctors. US Endodontics Partners. US Oral Surgery Management. Platform after platform, each one teaching the Thurston team something about how dental practices actually work -- and how consolidation usually fails.

The first lesson came from South Georgia Dental Management, the firm's earliest dental investment. Community dentistry in small-town Georgia. They learned that a dental practice is not a widget. It is a community institution with a name that matters, a team that holds it together, and patient relationships built over decades. Strip those away and you have nothing worth buying.

The second lesson came from watching the industry repeat the same mistakes. Gen1 DSOs were accounting machines -- efficient, soulless. Gen2 was the cookie-cutter era: same supplies, same place, every office. Efficient and dehumanizing. Gen3 was buying groups and consortiums with better intentions but no real scale. By the time Thurston launched Gen4 Dental Partners in August 2021, the thesis had crystallized: the entire model was broken, and fixing it required starting from the dentist's perspective, not the investor's.

Gen4's founding team was deliberately unconventional. Lamonte Jensen had been a Marine Corps infantry officer with multiple combat tours in Iraq before earning his MBA at the University of Chicago and consulting at McKinsey. He then spent years at Spear Education, seeing from the inside what dentists actually struggled with. Dr. Mitch Ellingson had owned multiple practices in Phoenix and founded Spear Practice Solutions. He had personally lived the transition from solo practice owner to group leader. "I've been through the transition of going from private-practice dentist to working inside a group," Ellingson said, "and it is really hard. It's way harder than anybody anticipates."

They were so convinced this represented a generational shift that they put it in the company name. Gen4. The fourth generation of dental consolidation. Clinician-centric, patient-obsessed, built around peer learning instead of top-down management. By the end of its second year, Gen4 had grown to 90 practices across 12 states with 165 clinicians and 1,365 employees. Dentists were hungry for this model.

But Thurston saw a gap Gen4 alone could not fill. Implant dentistry was booming, and dentists had nowhere to get world-class hands-on training AND business support under one roof. So in September 2023, they partnered with Dr. Michael A. Pikos -- a pioneer in implantology, a globally recognized educator, a name that carries weight in every implant study group in the country -- to launch MODIS Dental Partners. MODIS stood for MODern ImplantologiStS, and the name itself was a statement: this is not your father's dental implant practice.

Three entities. Built independently. Each solving a different piece of the puzzle. SGA had the Southeast community dentistry network. Gen4 had the collaborative doctor culture and national footprint. MODIS had the specialty vertical, the education platform, and clinical innovators like Dr. Grant Olson, who had built a YouTube following of 138,000 subscribers from Springfield, Missouri without a media company behind him.

Now they are one. Not because a boardroom decided to merge spreadsheets, but because 37 years of building, learning, and rebuilding finally produced all three pieces of what dental consolidation was always supposed to be: community reach, collaborative culture, and clinical innovation.

As Lamonte Jensen put it: "What I would like to do is live up to our name. I would like to be on the cutting edge of DSOs -- of dentistry, really. I would like to gather a group of very smart, very successful dentists together who like to collaborate so we can change the game a little bit."

They have been changing it for 37 years. SGA is simply what it looks like when all the lessons come together.

Dentistry is the last great profession. And we intend to keep it that way.

### Dr. Grant Olson Spotlight Narrative

**The Anti-Corporate Dentist**

Grant Olson was supposed to be an accountant.

Growing up in Springfield, Missouri -- population 170,000, four hours from the nearest major city -- the path seemed straightforward. Numbers. Spreadsheets. A sensible career in a sensible town. Then somewhere between the debits and the credits, he discovered dentistry. And dentistry discovered a person who would refuse, at every turn, to practice it the way anyone expected.

Dr. Olson did not leave Springfield. He could have. With his clinical credentials -- one of only 300 CEREC Mentors worldwide, a pioneer of patented guided implant technology, a practitioner whose case complexity rivals the best university programs -- the celebrity-dentist track was open. Los Angeles. New York. A faculty position. A national chain.

Instead, he built Innovative Dental in his hometown. And he built it like nothing the dental industry had seen before.

Walk into Innovative Dental and you do not see a dental office. You see a spa. A concierge greets you and walks you through a "care itinerary." The experience is designed around the patient's emotional state, not the clinical workflow. And behind the warmth is technology that would make most dental schools jealous.

Then he turned on a camera.

No media company. No production team. No PR agency. Just a dentist from Springfield talking directly to people who were scared, ashamed, and desperate for honest information about their teeth. "What's up, guys? This is Dr. O from Innovative Dental." Casual. Direct. Human.

138,000 subscribers. 23 million views. 3,390+ five-star reviews. Patients who fly from across the country -- Josh, a wedding photographer from Florida who had suffered for eight years with failing teeth, found Dr. O on YouTube, and flew to Springfield for 11 implants in a single visit.

The moment that captures who Dr. Olson is: after Josh's procedure, Dr. O grilled him a steak. A medium-rare New York strip, perfectly seasoned, fresh off the grill. Because when you have spent eight years unable to eat the foods you love, the first bite of a real steak is not a clinical outcome. It is a life moment.

"Implant Dentistry is about giving people back all of life's moments," Olson has said. "If you have to think about your teeth, those moments aren't there. I want to dedicate my life to helping my patients and a team rally around the cause of making sure everybody lives full every one of life's moments."

When MODIS Dental Partners launched in September 2023, Dr. Olson and Innovative Dental were among the founding partners. Not because MODIS bought his practice. Because the partnership gave Innovative Dental the infrastructure -- capital, recruiting, marketing support, operational relief -- to scale what Dr. Olson had built without diluting it. The name stayed. The team stayed. The spa-like patient experience stayed. The YouTube channel kept rolling.

In 2026, Olson is moving to live, interactive content. "You're going to see a lot less canned videos, edited videos... what you're going to see is a lot of live content. That authentic interaction, the face to face." It is the next evolution of the same philosophy that started in a dental chair in Springfield: treat people like humans, talk to them directly, and never let the business of dentistry eclipse the craft of it.

He could be anywhere. He chose Springfield. He chose to stay close to the community that shaped him. He chose to build something that proves you do not need a corporate brand, a big-city address, or a private equity mandate to change thousands of lives.

He signs off every video the same way:

"Keep smiling... it looks good on you."

That is not a tagline. It is a worldview. And it is the SGA story told through one person.
