## 1. Positioning Statement

**FOR** mid-career dental practice owners who are exhausted by the business side of dentistry but terrified of losing everything they built,
**WHO** need a liquidity event, operational relief, and a path forward that does not require selling their soul to a corporate machine,
**SGA Dental Partners IS THE** dental partnership platform
**THAT** makes dentists better -- not just better supported -- through peer collaboration, world-class continuing education, and a three-vertical architecture spanning community dentistry to advanced implantology,
**UNLIKE** corporate DSOs that standardize practices into interchangeable units, or management companies that charge fees without offering equity upside,
**WE** are backed by the Thurston Group's 38-year, $4B+ track record across 8 dental and healthcare platforms -- and our partners keep their name, their team, and their way of practicing while owning equity in something far larger than any solo practice.

### One-Liner (Internal Use)

SGA is the dental partnership platform where dentists come to get better, not just get bought.

## 2. Brand Personality Framework

### Archetype Profile

**Primary Archetype: Sage** -- SGA's deepest differentiator is knowledge. The Pikos Institute, the 4Front Symposium, the peer mentorship model, Dr. Olson's 138K-subscriber education channel. This is not a company that acquires practices. It is a company that elevates practitioners. The Sage archetype communicates wisdom, earned authority, and the belief that knowledge shared freely is the most durable competitive advantage.

**Secondary Archetype: Caregiver** -- The Sage provides the knowledge. The Caregiver provides the protective instinct. SGA exists to shield dentists from administrative burden, financial vulnerability, and professional isolation. "You built it. We protect it." The Caregiver archetype shows up in how SGA treats its partners: preserving their names, their teams, their cultures. Not absorbing. Surrounding.

### Personality Dimensions

| Dimension | SGA's Position | What It Sounds Like | What It Does NOT Sound Like |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Authority** | Earned, not claimed | "38 years. 8 platforms. $4B returned." | "We are the premier dental organization." |
| **Warmth** | Peer-to-peer, not parental | "You have been on your own long enough." | "We will take care of everything for you." |
| **Confidence** | Quiet, evidence-backed | "Our partners can tell you how it worked out." | "We guarantee the best results in the industry." |
| **Directness** | Calls out the elephant | "7 out of 10 dentists regret their DSO sale. Here is why we built something different." | "We offer a superior partnership experience." |
| **Ambition** | Profession-level, not company-level | "Dentistry is the last great profession run by the people who do it. We intend to keep it that way." | "We are the fastest-growing DSO in America." |

### If SGA Were a Person

- They would be described as: experienced, straightforward, protective, intellectually curious, allergic to corporate theater
- They would speak like: a senior partner at a firm who has seen every mistake and wants to help you avoid them -- direct but not condescending, confident but not boastful
- Their heroes would be: the dentist who mentored a colleague and changed their career trajectory; the surgeon who publishes everything they know so others can learn
- They would never: use jargon to obscure simple ideas, make promises they cannot back with names and numbers, treat a practice owner like a line item on a spreadsheet

## 3. Three-Tier Messaging Hierarchy

### Tier 1: Primary Message (The Big Idea)

**"Dentists come to SGA to get better, not just to get bought."**

This is the brand's strategic center of gravity. Every DSO in the market sells the transaction -- the liquidity event, the back-office relief, the operational support. These are table stakes. SGA sells the transformation that happens AFTER the deal closes: the clinical growth, the peer community, the rejuvenation of a career that was going stale.

This message was built to solve a specific strategic problem: "clinical autonomy" is category wallpaper. Every DSO from Heartland to MB2 now claims it. At Schwartz Level 4 sophistication, the market needs mechanism, not claims. "Get better, not just get bought" introduces a new frame -- it repositions the conversation from "will I lose control?" to "will I actually grow?"

### Tier 2: Secondary Messages (Supporting Pillars)

#### Pillar 1: The Education Platform

**Message:** "The only dental partnership with a built-in path from general dentistry to advanced implantology."

This is SGA's hardest-to-replicate differentiator. The Pikos Institute is globally recognized in implantology education. The 4Front Symposium brings partner clinicians together for peer-to-peer collaboration. The Individual Growth Engine pairs experienced doctors with newer partners for clinical mentorship. No competitor has anything remotely comparable.

**Proof Points:**
- Pikos Institute: world-renowned implantology training center, Dr. Michael A. Pikos as founding partner
- 4Front Symposium: annual peer learning event where "senior clinicians brainstorming together naturally elevated the ideas together -- that is where the goose bumps come from"
- Individual Growth Engine: Dr. Matt Cavendish's story -- 20 years in practice, "maybe was in a spot of complacency," then paired with a mentor, observed his first all-on-four, revenues up 16.5% TTM, staff retention at 100%
- Dr. Grant Olson: 138K YouTube subscribers, 23M+ views, one of 300 CEREC Mentors worldwide, patented guided implant technology
- The career pathway: a general dentist can join through the community vertical, upskill through the Pikos Institute, and develop implant capabilities -- all within SGA

## 4. Key Differentiators

### Differentiator 1: Education as the Platform's Soul (Not a Benefits Line Item)

**Type:** Product / Purpose differentiation
**Defensibility:** Very high -- competitors cannot replicate the Pikos Institute or build a comparable peer education model overnight

Every DSO offers a CE stipend. SGA offers an education ecosystem: the Pikos Institute for hands-on implantology training, the 4Front Symposium for annual peer collaboration, the Individual Growth Engine for one-on-one clinical mentorship, and Dr. Grant Olson's content platform as a living classroom. The difference between "we give you $1,500 a year for CE" and "we pair you with a mentor, send you to learn from Dr. Michael Pikos, and connect you with 100+ peers who will challenge your clinical thinking" is the difference between a benefit and a culture.

**Research backing:** Competitive landscape analysis confirms no DSO competitor offers an integrated education platform. MB2 has zero CE infrastructure. Heartland offers generic CE. PDS has medical integration training but not clinical excellence development. SALT has no education offering. Guardian has no education offering.

### Differentiator 2: The Thurston Group's Serial Dental Platform Building

**Type:** Authority / Trust differentiation
**Defensibility:** Absolute -- no other PE firm in dental has this track record

KKR backs Heartland but has no dental-specific narrative. Mubadala backs DCA but is a sovereign wealth fund with no healthcare identity. MB2's backers are opaque. Guardian is family office-backed with limited scale capital. Only Thurston Group can say: "We have built this exact model 8 times across dental and healthcare, returned $4B+ to our partners, and have been doing this for 38 years."

**Research backing:** Voice of customer data shows "PE fear" as a top-3 objection. The Dentist Advisors podcast reports "seven or eight out of 10 are frustrated or regretful" after DSO sales. Thurston's verifiable multi-platform track record is the strongest counter-proof available in the market.

### Differentiator 3: Three-Vertical Architecture

**Type:** Structural / Product differentiation
**Defensibility:** High -- requires years of acquisition and integration to replicate

SGA is not a general dentistry platform with implants bolted on. It is three purpose-built verticals -- community dentistry, collaborative general and specialty practice, and advanced implantology -- unified under one ownership and operational platform. This creates internal career pathways (a general dentist can develop implant skills through the Pikos Institute), cross-referral networks (general practices can refer complex cases to specialty partners), and market coverage no single-model DSO can match.

**Research backing:** Feature comparison matrix shows no competitor offers this structural breadth. SALT Dental addresses specialty (peds/ortho/oral surgery) but has no general dentistry vertical. Aspen has ClearChoice for implants but as a separate brand with no integration pathway. DCA has three divisions but no education or clinical development connecting them.

### Differentiator 4: The Post-Deal Transformation (What Happens in Year 2, 3, 5)

**Type:** Narrative / Personality differentiation
**Defensibility:** Moderate -- requires consistent proof over time, but first-mover advantage is strong

The biggest messaging gap in the DSO market: nobody tells the story of what happens AFTER the deal closes. Every DSO sells the transaction. SGA can sell the transformation. Dr. Cavendish's story is the template: bored after 20 years, joined Gen4, paired with a mentor, learned all-on-four procedures, revenues up 16.5%, staff retention at 100%, and -- most importantly -- "it is fun again." That arc, repeated across dozens of partners, becomes an unassailable competitive narrative.

**Research backing:** Competitive landscape gap analysis identifies "post-deal transformation story" as the #1 messaging gap. No competitor website shows longitudinal partner development stories.

## 5. Tagline Options

Arranged from polished-professional to bold-distinctive. All draw from voice-of-customer language and research insights.

### Professional Range

1. **"Clinical freedom surrounded by the best of everything."**
Rationale: SGA's existing tagline. It works. Preserves the autonomy promise while elevating the support infrastructure. Risk: "clinical freedom" is becoming category wallpaper.

2. **"Built by dentists who refused to build another DSO."**
Rationale: Directly channels Dr. Ellingson's founding ethos -- "We tell them we are not a DSO. We are not playing by their rules." Positions SGA as the anti-category brand. Mechanism-forward at Schwartz Level 4.

3. **"The partnership that makes dentists better."**
Rationale: Distills the primary message into seven words. Shifts the frame from "what you keep" (autonomy, name, team) to "what you gain" (clinical growth, peer community, education). Hard for any competitor to claim without the education platform to back it up.

### Challenger Range

4. **"Your practice. Your name. Our platform behind it."**
Rationale: Addresses the #1 fear (losing identity) in the headline. Possessive language mirrors how dentists talk about their practices ("my practice," "my baby," "my team"). Simple, specific, and structurally honest.

5. **"Where dentists come to get better, not just get bought."**
Rationale: The primary brand message as a tagline. Provocative because it names the thing competitors do (buy practices) and explicitly rejects it. Forces a new frame on the category.

6. **"38 years. 8 platforms. $4B returned. Your turn."**
Rationale: Pure authority play. For a market at Schwartz Level 4, specificity beats sloganeering. This line does not ask you to believe a claim -- it gives you numbers you can verify. Best suited for B2B acquisition contexts rather than broad brand use.

### Bold Range

7. **"Dentistry is the last great profession. We are keeping it that way."**
Rationale: Channels Dr. Ellingson's "last great profession" framing. Elevates SGA from a company selling partnerships to a movement defending the profession. Creates ideological alignment: you do not just join SGA, you join the cause.

8. **"They built DSOs for investors. We built one for dentists."**
Rationale: Direct competitive positioning. Names the category tension (investor-driven vs. clinician-driven) and picks a side. Aggressive but defensible given Thurston's track record and the Gen4/MODIS founding narratives.

9. **"Not a DSO. A partnership of dentists invested in each other."**
Rationale: Uses SGA's own language from the product brief. The "invested in each other" double meaning (financial equity + personal commitment) is elegant. Risks the "not a DSO" framing that MB2 has already pioneered with "DPO."

10. **"You built something worth protecting. So did we."**
Rationale: Speaks to the practice owner's identity as a builder. Establishes mutuality -- SGA is not a buyer swooping in; it is a fellow builder offering to combine forces. Emotionally resonant without being sentimental.

### Recommendation

**Primary tagline (brand-level):** "The partnership that makes dentists better." -- It is ownable, differentiated, and backed by the education platform. It reframes the category conversation.

**B2B acquisition tagline:** "Built by dentists who refused to build another DSO." -- For the skeptical practice owner who has seen every DSO pitch.

**B2C patient tagline (MODIS vertical):** Retain Dr. Olson's organic sign-off as the patient-facing brand voice: "Keep smiling... it looks good on you." -- Casual, warm, human. The opposite of corporate dental language.

## 6. Tone of Voice Guidelines

### Universal Voice Principles

SGA's voice is the same across all audiences. The TONE shifts. Think of it as one person speaking to different people in different contexts -- they do not change who they are, they adjust how they say it.

**Voice constants:**
- Evidence over claims ("Our partners can tell you" vs. "We are the best")
- Specific over generic ("85% staff retention" vs. "great culture")
- Direct over hedged ("Here is how it works" vs. "We believe we offer")
- Peer-level over parental ("You have been doing this long enough to know" vs. "Let us show you a better way")

---

### B2B Tone: Dentist / Practice Owner Audience

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. Respectful of their expertise. Frank about the industry's problems. Backs every claim with a name and a number.

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| "You did not go to dental school to chase insurance claims at midnight." | "Are you tired of administrative headaches?" |
| "7 out of 10 dentists regret their DSO sale. We built SGA because we heard that number and refused to add to it." | "Unlike other DSOs, we truly value clinical autonomy." |
| "Dr. Godat kept his name, his team, and his schedule. Ask him what changed. He will tell you: QuickBooks stopped calling him to the back office." | "Our partners enjoy the freedom to practice their way." |
| "Thurston Group has done this 8 times. $4B returned. Those are not projections. That is history." | "We are backed by a world-class private equity partner." |
| "Dental Partners of SW Georgia is still Dental Partners of SW Georgia." | "We preserve your local brand identity." |

**Key principle:** Mirror their language. Dentists say "my practice," "my team," "my patients." Never say "your asset," "the practice," or "the business." It is always theirs.

**Objection-handling tone:** Acknowledge the fear before countering it. "You have heard the horror stories. So have we. That is exactly why we built something different." Never dismiss skepticism. It is earned and legitimate.

---

### B2C Tone: Patient Audience

**Tone:** Warm, human, un-clinical. Speaks to the emotional experience of dental problems, not the technical details. Uses sensory language (eating a steak, smiling in a photo, laughing without covering your mouth).

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| "If you have to think about your teeth, those moments are not there." | "We offer comprehensive dental implant solutions." |
| "Linda was afraid to smile at her grandkids' birthday party. Three months later, she was the one blowing out the candles." | "Our patients experience improved quality of life outcomes." |
| "Your first meal with new teeth? Scott chose a medium-rare New York strip. What will you choose?" | "Our implant procedures restore full chewing function." |
| "You have spent years putting everyone else first. This is for you." | "Schedule your complimentary consultation today." |
| "3,390+ patients have trusted Dr. O with their smile. Here is what they say." | "We have an exceptional track record of patient satisfaction." |

**Key principle:** Lead with the before-state the patient is living in right now. They know the pain -- name it precisely and they feel understood. Then show the after-state through someone who looks like them.

**Fear-handling tone:** Never minimize. Dental anxiety is real, financial anxiety is real, surgical anxiety is real. "You are not the first person to feel this way. Here is what Josh felt before his procedure -- and here is what he said after eating his first steak."

---

### Recruitment Tone: Associate Dentist Audience

**Tone:** Career-forward, growth-oriented. Speaks to ambition, not just job features. Treats the associate as a future partner, not a production unit.

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| "Your career is not a production number." | "We offer competitive compensation packages." |
| "You did not survive four years of dental school to do nothing but Class IIs all day." | "We provide a wide variety of clinical opportunities." |
| "At SGA, associates do not just fill chairs. They get paired with mentors, attend the Pikos Institute, and build toward partnership." | "We value your professional development." |
| "One of our associates relearned how to do implants and now he loves his profession, his patients, everything." | "We support continuing education with generous CE stipends." |
| "You can invest in the company immediately as a Class A shareholder. That is not a recruiting line. Here is how it works." | "Pathway to partnership available." |

**Key principle:** The associate has been burned by vague promises. Be specific. Name the mentorship program. Name the CE opportunities. Describe the equity pathway with enough detail that they can evaluate it, not just hope for it.

## Brand Guidelines Summary

### Do

- Lead with evidence, not claims. Names. Numbers. Verified outcomes.
- Mirror the language dentists actually use: "my practice," "my team," "my patients."
- Acknowledge DSO skepticism head-on before trying to counter it.
- Tell the post-deal story -- what happens in Year 2, Year 3, Year 5.
- Use Dr. Ellingson, Lamonte Jensen, Dr. Pikos, and Dr. Olson as human symbols of the brand.
- Give the brand a first-class ticket: premium presentation, no discount language, no desperation.

### Do Not

- Claim "clinical autonomy" without immediately backing it with a specific example or named partner.
- Use generic DSO language: "world-class support," "premier dental organization," "we value our partners."
- Lead with the transaction (deal terms, multiples, liquidity event) before establishing the cultural fit.
- Discount or run price promotions that signal "not desirable at full price."
- Make promises you cannot substantiate with a phone call to a current partner.
- Treat practice acquisition like a sales funnel. It is a trust funnel. The timeline is 6-18 months.

### Ogilvy's Brand Test

- [x] **Sharp definition:** SGA is not trying to be everything to everyone. It is the education-forward, clinician-led, Thurston-backed partnership platform. It explicitly rejects the corporate DSO model.
- [x] **Consistency across touchpoints:** Voice principles, personality dimensions, and proof points are defined for every audience and context.
- [x] **Distinctiveness from competitors:** Education platform, Thurston track record, three-vertical architecture, and post-deal transformation narrative are all unoccupied territory.
- [x] **Authenticity:** Every claim can be backed by named partners, published numbers, or verifiable institutional credentials.
- [x] **Longevity:** "The partnership that makes dentists better" works whether SGA has 60 practices or 600. The education and collaboration culture scales.
- [x] **First-class presentation:** The brand personality rejects discount culture, corporate theater, and vague promises. Premium positioning throughout.
