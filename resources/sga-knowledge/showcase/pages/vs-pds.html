SGA Dental Partners vs PDS Health

Both platforms champion practice ownership. But "ownership" means very different things here.

Owner vs Partner Liquidity Event Mouth-Body Connection Management Fee vs Equity

TL;DR

PDS Health (Pacific Dental Services) and SGA Dental Partners are the two DSOs most frequently compared by dentists who value practice ownership and clinical autonomy. PDS operates a management services arrangement where dentists own their individual practices and pay PDS a management fee. SGA operates a joint-venture equity model where dentists receive upfront liquidity and retain equity in the combined SGA platform. PDS is larger (1,000+ practices, $3.1B+ revenue) and offers a unique dental-medical integration play. Choose PDS if you want to own your individual practice with management support. Choose SGA if you want to own equity in a growing multi-vertical platform while getting better as a clinician.

Why This Comparison Matters

This is the comparison that matters most for a specific type of dentist: the practice owner who wants operational support but refuses to become an employee of a corporate entity. Both PDS and SGA speak directly to this person. Both use language about clinical autonomy, practice ownership, and dentist-centricity. But the mechanics are different — and those mechanics determine your financial outcome, your clinical future, and your day-to-day experience.

S

Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners consolidates three Thurston Group-backed entities: SGA (community dentistry, Southeast), Gen4 Dental Partners (collaborative general and specialty, national), and MODIS Dental Partners (implantology and specialty, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos). SGA's model is a joint-venture equity partnership: dentists receive 60–70% upfront liquidity while retaining 30–40% as equity in the combined platform. Clinical autonomy is contractual. The Thurston Group has deployed this playbook across 8 dental and healthcare platforms over 38 years, returning $4B+ to partners.

Who Is PDS Health?

PDS was founded in 1994 by Stephen Thorne and has grown into the third-largest DSO in the United States, with 1,000+ practices across 24 states and $3.1B+ in annual revenue (2025). PDS rebranded to "PDS Health" to reflect its expansion beyond traditional dental services. The "Mouth-Body Connection" — integrating dental and medical screening (blood pressure, A1C testing) into dental offices — represents PDS's push to position itself as a healthcare company. PDS welcomed 83 new practice owners in 2025. The model is founder-led with minority PE investment.

The Ownership Model: The Critical Distinction

PDS: You Own Your Practice, PDS Manages It

What This Means Financially

  • ● No upfront liquidity event — you do not sell your practice to PDS
  • ● Financial upside tied to your individual practice's performance
  • ● PDS charges a management fee out of practice revenue
  • ● You do not own equity in PDS as a company

What This Means Practically

  • ● You are genuinely an owner-operator — the practice is yours
  • ● Your wealth is concentrated in a single asset: your practice
  • ● You pay for support rather than receiving investment

SGA: You Sell a Portion, Retain Platform Equity

What This Means Financially

  • ✓ Upfront liquidity event: 60–70% at close
  • ✓ Retained equity: 30–40% in the combined SGA platform
  • ✓ Financial upside tied to the entire SGA platform's growth
  • ✓ No management fee

What This Means Practically

  • ✓ Life-changing cash event while staying clinically active
  • ✓ Wealth diversified across a multi-practice platform
  • ✓ You participate in the appreciation of every practice in the network

PDS Model

You own 100% of one practice and pay fees for support.

SGA Model

You own equity in a platform of 60–125+ practices, received significant cash at close, and your equity grows as the platform grows.

Where SGA Dental Partners Wins

1

Upfront Liquidity — The Event PDS Cannot Offer

SGA provides what many practice owners are searching for: a meaningful cash event. When two kids are in college and a financial advisor says to diversify, SGA delivers 60–70% of practice valuation in cash at close. PDS does not provide this.

2

Platform Equity vs. Single-Practice Ownership

Under SGA, a partner owns equity in the combined platform. As SGA acquires more practices and grows organically, every partner's equity appreciates. Under PDS, a dentist owns one practice. The difference between a small business and a diversified investment.

3

The Education Platform

Dr. Matt Cavendish: 20 years in practice, hitting a wall of complacency, then paired with a Gen4 mentor. Observed his first all-on-four. Revenues grew 16.5% TTM. "It is fun again." PDS provides management services. SGA provides clinical transformation.

4

Operational Relief Without a Management Fee

Under PDS, the dentist pays a management fee for back-office support. Under SGA, operational support (billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing) transitions to SGA at no separate fee. PDS charges you for support; SGA provides it as part of the equity partnership.

5

Implantology and Specialty Vertical

MODIS was purpose-built for implantologists and specialty practitioners, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos. PDS's specialty model is expanding but nascent. SGA's is established, with dedicated infrastructure and an integrated education institute.

Where PDS Health Wins

1

The Owner Clinician Model Is Genuine

Dentists genuinely own their practices. They are not employees. They are not franchisees. They own the entity and PDS manages the back office. For dentists who want to say "I own my practice" in the most literal sense, PDS delivers.

2

The Mouth-Body Connection

PDS's integration of medical screening into dental visits is genuinely innovative. No other DSO is pursuing dental-medical integration at this scale. If the future of dentistry is at the intersection of dental and medical care, PDS is the only platform building toward that vision.

3

Founder-Led Stability

Stephen Thorne founded PDS in 1994 and has led it for over 30 years. This continuity provides cultural stability that PE-backed organizations — where sponsors cycle every 5–7 years — cannot always match.

4

Scale Without PE Control

PDS has grown to 1,000+ practices with only minority PE investment. It is not controlled by a PE fund with an exit timeline. For dentists whose primary objection is "private equity scares me," PDS provides a structural answer.

5

West Coast Geographic Strength

PDS's geographic strength is historically West Coast-heavy (California, Arizona, Nevada). If you practice in Western states, PDS has deeper regional infrastructure than SGA.
