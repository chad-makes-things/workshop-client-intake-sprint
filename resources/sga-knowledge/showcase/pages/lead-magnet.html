Why This Assessment Exists

You did not go to dental school to manage payroll disputes at midnight.

Yet here you are -- reviewing financials on a Sunday night, fielding calls from your office manager about a staffing crisis, and wondering if you will ever feel the way you felt when you first opened your doors. Somewhere between the pride of what you built and the weight of carrying it alone, a question started forming:

Is there a better way to do this?

You are not the first practice owner to ask that question. Hundreds of dentists -- owners of thriving, community-rooted practices -- have sat exactly where you are sitting right now: proud of what they built, exhausted by what it demands, and uncertain about what comes next.

This assessment is not a sales pitch. It is a diagnostic tool -- the same framework that experienced dental practice advisors use to help owners evaluate their readiness for a partnership conversation. It will help you:

  • See your practice clearly across six dimensions that matter in any transition
  • Identify your strengths and the gaps that may be costing you value, time, or clinical fulfillment
  • Understand your readiness tier -- whether you are in a building phase, an exploration phase, or ready for a serious conversation
  • Ask the right questions of any DSO, partner, or advisor who approaches you

About This Tool

The assessment takes 10-15 minutes. Your results are yours -- no one sees them but you. This tool was designed to be genuinely useful regardless of whether you ever speak to SGA or anyone else. A practice owner who completes this assessment and decides to stay solo will still walk away with a clearer picture of where they stand. That is the point.

How to Use This Assessment

For each question, circle or note the score (1-5) that best describes your current situation. Be honest -- this is for you, not for a buyer. Aspirational answers help no one.

Scoring Scale

1 Significant concern / major gap
2 Below where I need to be
3 Adequate but not strong
4 Solid position
5 Exceptional / clear strength

After completing all six categories, total your score and find your tier in the Results Interpretation section.

Tier 1: Foundation Building

You are earlier in the journey. Your practice may have significant areas that need strengthening before a partnership conversation would serve you well -- or you may simply not be emotionally ready, and that is perfectly fine.

This score does not mean partnership is not in your future. It means the smartest move right now is to invest in the fundamentals.

Your Recommended Next Steps

  1. Shore up your financials. Work with a dental-specific accountant to establish your EBITDA baseline.
  2. Reduce owner dependency. Document your systems. Cross-train your team.
  3. Address technology gaps. Prioritize technology that directly impacts patient experience and clinical capability.
  4. Build your peer network. Join a study club, attend conferences, or find a mastermind group.
  5. Subscribe to industry education. Learn about different partnership models, deal structures, and valuation methodologies.
51-79 Points

Tier 2: Exploring

You have a solid foundation with clear room for growth. You may be in the "midnight Googling" phase -- searching "sell dental practice" late at night, not yet ready to pick up the phone but increasingly aware that something needs to change.

This is the most important phase. The decisions you make now will determine whether your eventual transition is one of the 3 in 10 that go well or one of the 7 in 10 that end in frustration.

Your Recommended Next Steps

  1. Get a baseline practice valuation from an independent dental practice appraiser, not a broker.
  2. Talk to dentists who have been through it. Ask about year two, not just closing day.
  3. Understand the models. Corporate DSOs, management service organizations, and joint-venture equity partnerships are fundamentally different.
  4. Identify your non-negotiables. What must be preserved in any partnership?
  5. Evaluate your personal financial picture. How much liquidity do you need? What does diversification look like for you?
80-110 Points

Tier 3: Ready to Talk

Your practice is well-positioned, your financial house is in order, and you are emotionally ready. You are not looking for a rescue -- you are looking for an amplifier.

This is a position of strength. You have options, leverage, and the luxury of choosing a partner based on fit rather than necessity.

Your Recommended Next Steps

  1. Have 3-5 confidential conversations with potential partners to understand the range of models, cultures, and deal structures.
  2. Bring your advisors to the table. A good partner will welcome their involvement.
  3. Ask the hard questions. Use the DSO checklist on the next page. Ask for references from partners who joined 2+ years ago.
  4. Visit a partner practice. See how it actually runs 18+ months in.
  5. Negotiate from knowledge. The right partner will respect clarity and conviction.

Questions to Ask Any DSO

The Due Diligence Checklist Every Practice Owner Needs

These questions separate the partners who have built something real from the ones who have built a pitch deck. Use them in any conversation. Their answers -- and especially their willingness to answer -- will tell you everything.

Section A: Track Record and Stability

  1. 1How long has your backing entity been in business, and how many healthcare platforms have they built?
  2. 2How much capital has your backer returned to partners across all investments?
  3. 3Can I speak directly with 3-5 partners who joined 2+ years ago -- partners you did not select for me?
  4. 4What is your total partner count, and what is your partner retention rate?
  5. 5What is your staff retention rate across the network?

Section B: Autonomy and Identity

  1. 6Will my practice keep its name, branding, and community identity? Get this in writing.
  2. 7Who makes clinical decisions -- treatment plans, materials, labs, scheduling, hiring?
  3. 8Will I be required to use specific vendors, labs, or supply contracts?
  4. 9What happens if I disagree with a corporate decision that affects my practice?
  5. 10Can I see the partnership agreement template before I commit to due diligence?

Section C: Financial Structure

  1. 11Walk me through the equity model in detail. What percentage do I retain? How is it valued? What triggers a liquidity event?
  2. 12What management fee does the platform charge, and what does it cover?
  3. 13Are there clawback provisions, earnout penalties, or non-compete implications?
  4. 14What is your expected hold period, and what happens at exit?

Section D: Operational Support

  1. 15What specific back-office functions will you take over, and what is the timeline?
  2. 16What technology investments will you make in my practice, and over what period?
  3. 17How do you handle recruiting when I need a new associate, hygienist, or front desk team member?
  4. 18What continuing education resources are available, and are they included?

Section E: Culture and Community

  1. 19What does the peer community look like? How often do partners interact, and in what format?
  2. 20How do you handle the transition with my existing staff?
  3. 21What career development opportunities exist for my non-clinical team?
  4. 22What does your partner NPS or satisfaction score look like?

Red Flags to Watch For

  • ×They rush the timeline
  • ×They will not provide unscripted references
  • ×They avoid specifics on equity terms
  • ×They dismiss your advisors
  • ×They promise everything will "stay the same"
  • ×Their staff turnover is high or undisclosed

Green Flags That Signal a Real Partner

  • ✓They encourage you to talk to your advisors
  • ✓They provide named, contactable references
  • ✓They articulate what changes and what stays, in writing
  • ✓They have a verifiable multi-platform track record
  • ✓Partners speak with specificity, not just enthusiasm
  • ✓They measure and share retention and outcomes

A Final Note

If you have made it this far, you are already doing something most practice owners never do: examining your situation with honesty and rigor.

Whether your score says "foundation building" or "ready to talk," the act of completing this assessment means you are thinking strategically about your future rather than reacting to whatever crisis arrives next. That alone puts you ahead of the majority of your peers.

Whatever you decide -- stay solo, explore options, or start conversations -- make the decision from a position of knowledge. Not at midnight. Not in a panic. Not because a broker called at the right moment.

You built something worth protecting. The next chapter should be written on your terms.
