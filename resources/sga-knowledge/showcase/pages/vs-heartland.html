SGA Dental Partners vs Heartland Dental

An honest, side-by-side comparison of two fundamentally different DSO models.

TL;DR

Heartland Dental is the largest privately held DSO in the United States, with 1,800+ practices across 38 states, backed by KKR. SGA Dental Partners is a smaller, education-forward partnership platform backed by the Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+ returned to partners). Heartland offers unmatched scale and operational infrastructure. SGA offers a joint-venture equity model, an integrated education platform (Pikos Institute + 4Front Symposium), and a three-vertical architecture spanning community dentistry to advanced implantology. Choose Heartland if you want the resources of the largest DSO network. Choose SGA if you want to own equity in a growing platform while getting better as a clinician.

S

Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners is a dental partnership platform consolidating three Thurston Group-backed entities: the original SGA network (Southeast community dentistry), Gen4 Dental Partners (collaborative general and specialty practice), and MODIS Dental Partners (implantology and specialty, anchored by the Pikos Institute). The combined platform spans 10–14+ states with a growing national footprint. SGA's model is a joint-venture equity partnership: selling dentists receive upfront liquidity while retaining an ownership stake in the combined SGA platform. The tagline captures the philosophy: "The partnership that makes dentists better."

sgadentalpartners.com
H

Who Is Heartland Dental?

Heartland Dental was founded in 1997 by Dr. Rick Workman and has grown into the largest privately held dental support organization in the country. In 2021, KKR — one of the world's largest private equity firms — acquired a majority stake. Today, Heartland supports over 1,800 practices in 38+ states and reported approximately 11.5 million patient visits in 2025. Heartland positions itself around "The Doctor Difference" — the idea that its model is doctor-led and clinically independent, with billing, HR, compliance, procurement, and technology handled by a massive support infrastructure.

Deal Structure Comparison

SGA Dental Partners

SGA structures deals as joint-venture equity partnerships:

  • ✓60–70% upfront liquidity at close — a meaningful, life-changing cash event
  • ✓30–40% retained as equity in the combined SGA platform
  • ✓Ongoing clinical compensation (market-rate salary + production bonus, typically 28–32% of collections)
  • ✓Equity appreciation as SGA grows through acquisitions and organic performance
The fundamental difference: Heartland pays you once. SGA pays you at close and continues building value alongside you.

Heartland Dental

Heartland's model leans toward outright acquisition:

  • ●Cash payment at close
  • ●Transition to employment or management arrangement
  • ●No ongoing equity stake in the Heartland platform
  • ●Financial upside ends at the transaction
Best for: Dentists near retirement who want a clean, full-liquidity exit without ongoing equity complexity.

Mid-market dental practice EBITDA multiples generally range from 6–9x, with high-performing groups reaching 9–12x. Both organizations operate within these bands, though the structure of payment differs significantly.

Where SGA Dental Partners Wins

1

Equity Ownership in the Platform

SGA's JV model means partners retain equity in the combined platform and participate in its growth. Heartland's acquisition model does not offer this. Thurston Group's $4B+ returned to partners validates this model.

2

The Education Platform

Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium, and Individual Growth Engine. Consider Dr. Matt Cavendish: 20 years in practice, paired with a Gen4 mentor, revenues up 16.5% TTM, 100% staff retention. "It is fun again."

3

Clinical Autonomy — Structural, Not Just Claimed

SGA's autonomy is reinforced by a clinical advisory board led by practicing dentists. Heartland's employee reviews tell a more complicated story about manager involvement and production pressure.

4

Three-Vertical Architecture

Community dentistry, collaborative general/specialty, and implantology. A general dentist can develop implant capabilities through the Pikos Institute without leaving the organization.

5

Thurston Group Track Record

38 years, $4B+ returned, 8 dental/healthcare platforms (Smile Doctors, US Endodontics Partners, US Oral Surgery Management). Dental-specific, verifiable, contactable.

Where Heartland Dental Wins

1

Scale and Operational Resources

At 1,800+ practices, Heartland has operational infrastructure that smaller DSOs simply cannot match. Their purchasing power, depth of support staff, and ability to absorb unexpected operational challenges are unparalleled.

2

Geographic Reach

38+ states offers career mobility that SGA's current 10–14-state footprint cannot yet match. For dentists who might want to relocate, Heartland provides more options.

3

Proven Track Record at Scale

Operating since 1997, Heartland has nearly three decades of experience. That longevity means they have seen and solved most operational problems a DSO faces.

4

KKR Capital Access

KKR is one of the largest investment firms in the world. If capital is the bottleneck for technology investment, practice acquisitions, or infrastructure, Heartland has a clear advantage.

5

DSO Brand Recognition

Among dentists evaluating DSO partnerships, Heartland is often the first name that comes up. That recognition carries weight, particularly for those who find comfort in joining the established market leader.
