Scheduling

B2B owner calls: Calendly BD team round-robin (30 min). Specialist calls: Marcus Morosin's personal calendar. B2C implant: practice PMS or per-location Calendly. B2C general: direct PMS link or phone fallback.

Human Handoff Protocol

Immediate Triggers

  • User explicitly asks for a person
  • B2B lead score exceeds 70
  • Frustration or anger detected
  • Patient complaint about care

Handoff Steps

  1. Prepare user (name the person/team, not "sales")
  2. Transfer context (full transcript, score, signals, recommended next step)
  3. Warm intro (when live transfer available)
  4. Fallback (set specific response time if no human available)

Rule: Never leave a qualified lead at a dead end. If no human is available, set a specific expectation and follow through.

Fallback and Recovery System

Every dead end has a recovery path. The bot never leaves a user stranded.

Bot does not understand

Rephrase prompt + 3 button options: dental professional, patient help, talk to a real person.

Frustration detected

Immediate apology + human handoff. Tagged as escalation_frustration for QA review.

Out-of-scope question

Transparent acknowledgment + route to right person. Clinical questions redirected to consultation booking.

2+ min silence

"Still there?" check-in with continue, start over, or email options.

10+ min silence

Summary offer via email. Conversation stays open for return.

Abusive input

One warning + human offer. If continued: close chat with phone/email fallback.

Conversation Tone Reference

Voice Constants (All Flows)

  • Evidence over claims: "Our partners can tell you" not "We are the best"
  • Specific over generic: "85% staff retention" not "great culture"
  • Direct over hedged: "Here is how it works" not "We believe we offer"
  • Peer-level: "You have been doing this long enough to know" not "Let us show you a better way"

B2B vs B2C Tone

B2B (Practice Owners)

Respectful of expertise and fears. Mirror possessive language: "your practice," "your team." Never say "your asset" or "the opportunity." Back every claim with a name and a number.

B2C (Patients)

Warm, human, un-clinical. Lead with the before-state they live in. Use sensory language: eating, smiling, laughing. Never minimize fear or financial anxiety.
