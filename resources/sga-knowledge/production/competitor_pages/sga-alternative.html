How to Evaluate Any DSO (Including Us)

Whether you end up partnering with SGA or one of the alternatives listed here, use this framework to evaluate any DSO:

1

Talk to current partners — not the ones they select for you

Ask for 3–5 partners in similar situations. Ask: What changed since you joined? What stayed the same? What do you wish you had known before signing? Would you do it again?

2

Understand who is behind the capital

Ask: Who is the PE firm, family office, or financial sponsor? What is their track record specifically in dental? What is the typical hold period? What happens at recapitalization?

3

Have your own advisors review the deal

Your attorney and financial advisor should review the partnership agreement independently. Pay attention to: equity mechanics, non-compete provisions, exit provisions, clawback language, and what happens if you want to leave.

4

Visit a partner practice

Go see what the partnership looks like in the real world. Does the practice still feel like the owner's practice? Is the staff happy? Does the dentist seem engaged or demoralized?

5

Ask about the post-deal experience, not just the deal

Every DSO sells the transaction beautifully. The question is what happens in month 6, year 2, year 5. Ask: What does the first 90 days look like? When does the education start? What is the partner retention rate?
