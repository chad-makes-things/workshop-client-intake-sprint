TL;DR

Aspen Dental (part of The Aspen Group) is the most recognized consumer-facing dental brand in America, with 1,300+ offices across 48 states and $4.2B in annualized revenue. SGA Dental Partners is a joint-venture equity partnership platform with an integrated education platform and three clinical verticals, including the MODIS implantology division anchored by the Pikos Institute. These two organizations serve fundamentally different visions of dental partnership. Choose Aspen if you want a turnkey branded practice with built-in patient demand. Choose SGA if you want to keep your name, own equity in a growing platform, and develop clinically alongside peers.

S

Who Is SGA Dental Partners?

SGA Dental Partners is a dental partnership platform built on a joint-venture equity model, backed by the Thurston Group (38 years, $4B+ returned to partners). SGA consolidates three complementary verticals: SGA (community dentistry, Southeast), Gen4 Dental Partners (collaborative general and specialty), and MODIS Dental Partners (implantology and specialty, anchored by Dr. Michael Pikos and the Pikos Institute). Partner dentists receive upfront liquidity while retaining equity in the combined platform. Practices keep their names, teams, and clinical protocols. The model is designed for dentists who want to remain owners, not employees.

A

Who Is Aspen Dental?

Aspen Dental, operating under The Aspen Group (TAG), was founded in 1998 and has become the most visible dental brand in America. With 1,300+ offices across 48 states and $4.2B in annualized net revenue (first half 2025, 8% year-over-year growth), Aspen is the second-largest DSO in the United States. TAG's portfolio extends beyond general dentistry: ClearChoice (implant centers), WellNow Urgent Care, Chapter Aesthetic Studio, and Lovet Pet Health Care. Aspen's model is closer to a franchise than a traditional DSO partnership — dentists operate branded Aspen offices under standardized protocols.

The Implant Platform: ClearChoice vs MODIS/Pikos

For dentists with implantology ambitions, this comparison deserves its own section.

ClearChoice (Aspen/TAG)

ClearChoice is a network of dedicated implant centers within TAG's portfolio. It focuses on full-arch (All-on-4) procedures with strong consumer brand awareness.

Strengths

  • ✓ Established consumer brand in implants
  • ✓ Dedicated implant centers with specialized workflows
  • ✓ Strong direct-to-patient advertising model

Limitations

  • ✗ Completely siloed — general Aspen dentists have no educational pathway in
  • ✗ A referral destination, not a growth pathway for clinicians
  • ✗ No integrated clinical development pipeline
MODIS / Pikos Institute (SGA)

MODIS is SGA's implantology vertical, with Dr. Michael Pikos — a pioneer in dental implantology — as a founding partner. The Pikos Institute provides hands-on training from fundamental to advanced zygomatic implant techniques.

Strengths

  • ✓ Integrated education and clinical development pathway
  • ✓ General dentists can develop implant skills without leaving SGA
  • ✓ Capital investment in specialty infrastructure (CT scanners, surgical suites, in-house labs)
  • ✓ Dr. Grant Olson: 138K YouTube subscribers, 23M+ views

Limitations

  • ✗ Smaller scale than ClearChoice
  • ✗ Less consumer brand awareness for implant-specific patients

The key distinction: ClearChoice treats implant patients. MODIS/Pikos develops implant clinicians. If your goal is to grow your implantology capabilities, SGA's integrated model creates a pathway ClearChoice does not.

Where SGA Dental Partners Wins

1

You Keep Your Name and Identity

An Aspen partner operates under the Aspen Dental brand. An SGA partner operates under their own name — the one their patients know, their community trusts, and they spent years building.

2

Genuine Clinical Autonomy

Aspen operates standardized protocols. SGA's clinical autonomy is contractual — dentists choose their materials, labs, schedules, and treatment plans. Multiple sources have raised concerns about overtreatment pressure within the Aspen system.

3

Platform Equity Ownership

An Aspen dentist owns an Aspen-branded practice. An SGA partner owns equity in the entire SGA platform — a combined entity backed by a $350M credit facility with growth across three verticals.

4

Education Platform

At SGA, education is the platform's soul, not a benefits checkbox. Partners develop clinically — learning implantology from Dr. Michael Pikos, collaborating at 4Front, receiving 1:1 mentorship. No part of the Aspen model is designed to make dentists better clinicians.

5

Consumer Reputation

Aspen's brand carries documented baggage: 1.2 stars on Sitejabber, 4,100+ complaints on PissedConsumer, and $1.7M+ in settlements from AG investigations. SGA practices carry their own reputations — built by you, protected by you.

Where Aspen Dental Wins

1

Consumer Brand Power

Aspen Dental is the most recognized dental brand in America. National TV advertising, digital campaigns, and 1,300+ locations create patient awareness no other DSO can match.

2

Patient Volume

Aspen's marketing machine drives high patient volume to every office. TAG reported 20,000+ professionals across its network and $4.2B in annualized revenue — that patient flow is real.

3

Turnkey Practice Model

Aspen's de novo model means they can build a practice from scratch — location, buildout, equipment, staffing, marketing — and hand a dentist the keys to a fully operational office.

4

Multi-Brand Portfolio

TAG's portfolio (Aspen, ClearChoice, WellNow, Chapter, Lovet) creates cross-referral opportunities across dental, implants, urgent care, and aesthetics — a breadth unique in the market.

5

Community Access Focus

Aspen offices typically offer extended hours including Saturdays. The "breaking down barriers" mission serves populations that many private practices do not prioritize.
