Practice Owners

Mid-career dentists considering a partnership or liquidity event

15 sec Cocktail Party
"I work with SGA Dental Partners. We partner with practice owners who are exhausted by the business side of dentistry but do not want to lose what they built. The short version: your name stays, your team stays, you get equity in a growing platform, and our education program makes you a better clinician. We are backed by a PE firm with 38 years and $4B in partner returns. Do you know anyone in that situation?"
Problem + Differentiator Trust anchor (38yr, $4B) Open question to continue
30 sec Conference
"Seven out of ten dentists who sell to a DSO end up frustrated or regretful. That is not our stat -- that is what the Dentist Advisors podcast found. SGA Dental Partners was built by people who heard that number and refused to add to it.

We are a dental partnership platform -- not a corporate DSO. Practice owners keep their name, their team, and their clinical autonomy. They get a meaningful liquidity event plus equity in a multi-state platform. And what makes us different: the Pikos Institute for world-class implant education, the 4Front Symposium for peer collaboration, and we are backed by the Thurston Group -- 38 years, $4 billion returned to partners, 8 platforms built.

Dentists come to SGA to get better, not just to get bought. If that resonates, I would love to set up a confidential conversation."
Problem hook (7/10 regret) Category reframe 3 differentiators Big idea line + CTA
60 sec BD Call Opener
"Dr. [Name], thank you for taking the time. I will be direct.

The Dentist Advisors podcast reports that seven or eight out of ten dentists who sell to a DSO end up frustrated. The autonomy erodes, the culture changes, the equity does not perform. That is why SGA Dental Partners exists.

SGA is not a corporate DSO. We are built around three things no competitor offers together.

First -- education. The Pikos Institute, the 4Front Symposium, and a 1:1 mentorship program. One partner, Dr. Matt Cavendish, was 20 years in and 'in a spot of complacency.' Through mentorship, he learned all-on-four procedures. Revenues up 16.5%. Staff retention: 100%. He said: 'It is fun again.'

Second -- the Thurston Group. 38 years. Eight platforms. Over $4 billion returned to partners.

Third -- structure. Your name stays. Your team stays. You retain equity and your upside does not end at closing.

I do not know if SGA is the right fit. But if you are carrying the weight of the business side and wondering whether there is a way to get back to the dentistry -- that is a conversation worth having.

Would it make sense to schedule 45 minutes?"
Empathy opening 3-pillar structure Cavendish proof story Humility + Soft CTA

Pain identification IGE + Pikos + Equity Big idea line

Referring Dentists

Dentists who may not partner but can refer colleagues or patients

15 sec Cocktail Party
"I work with SGA Dental Partners -- backed by a PE firm with 38 years and $4 billion in partner returns. If you know any practice owners burning out on the business side but do not want to lose their name, team, or autonomy -- or associates stuck on a production treadmill -- I would love an introduction. We treat referrals like gold."
30 sec Conference
"Quick question -- do you know any colleagues drowning in the business side of dentistry? The ones who spent last Sunday doing payroll instead of being with family?

SGA Dental Partners: practice owners keep their name, team, autonomy. Liquidity event, platform equity, Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium. Thurston Group -- 38 years, $4 billion returned.

I am not asking you to sell anything. If someone comes to mind, would you make an introduction? The best partnerships start with a trusted colleague saying 'You should talk to these people.'"
Relatable pain (Sunday payroll) Low-pressure referral ask
60 sec BD Call / Peer Conversation
"Dr. [Name], I reached out because you have a reputation as someone people trust. That matters more than you realize.

SGA Dental Partners -- practice owners keep their name, team, equity in the platform. Pikos Institute, 4Front Symposium, 1:1 mentorship. Thurston Group, 38 years, $4B+ returned, 8 platforms.

The dentists we partner with almost never find us through Google. They find us because a trusted colleague said 'Look into this.' You are that colleague for a lot of dentists.

I am not asking you to endorse anything. If you hear a colleague mention overwhelm, succession planning, or frustration about technology investment -- would you pass along my name?

And for your practice: if you refer patients for full-arch or complex specialty care, our MODIS partners include Pikos Institute-trained implantologists. That cross-referral benefits your patients directly.

Can I send you a one-page overview to share if the moment comes up?"
Lead with flattery Referral as peer service Cross-referral value prop Low-commitment CTA

Training Notes: What to Memorize

  1. The opening hook for each audience (first sentence)
  2. Three differentiators: Education (Pikos + 4Front + IGE), Thurston (38 years, $4B+, 8 platforms), Structure (name stays, team stays, equity)
  3. Cavendish proof story: 20 years, complacency, all-on-four, 16.5%, "it is fun again"
  1. Big idea: "Dentists come to SGA to get better, not just to get bought."
  2. Closing CTA: "Would it make sense to schedule 45 minutes..."

Practice rule: Record yourself. The 15-second version must actually be 15 seconds. Listen for corporate jargon and brochure-speak.
