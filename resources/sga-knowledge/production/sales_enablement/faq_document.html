01

Partnership Process

Underlying concern: "Is this going to be a drawn-out, opaque process where I lose control?"

SGA follows a transparent, three-step partnership path designed to feel like a conversation between peers, not a corporate acquisition process. No brokers, no high-pressure timelines, no hidden steps.

Step 1: Explore. A confidential, mutual discovery call. We learn about your practice, goals, and concerns. You learn about SGA's model, culture, and track record. Many of the best partnerships start with a 45-minute call where the dentist was "just curious."

Step 2: Partner. Structured evaluation with transparent valuation methodology. We structure a JV equity partnership tailored to your profile -- community, group, or specialty. You retain an ownership stake in the combined SGA platform.

Step 3: Thrive. SGA transitions back-office operations within 60-90 days. You are paired with a clinical mentor. You attend the 4Front Symposium. Your name stays. Your team stays. You get back to dentistry.

Closer: Typical timeline from first conversation to close is 3-6 months. We do not rush partners. You will have a direct point of contact throughout.

Underlying concern: "I do not want this hanging over my head for a year."

Typical timeline: 3-6 months. Smaller community practices can move faster; larger multi-location groups may take longer.

Explore (2-4 weeks), Partner (6-12 weeks of evaluation and deal structuring), Thrive (30-60 days of post-close transition).

Closer: We have closed in as few as 90 days. The pace is set by you, not us. Weekly updates during active evaluation.

Underlying concern: "I do not trust the buyer to give me a fair deal without my own representation."

No broker needed -- we encourage direct conversations. That said, we work seamlessly with transition advisors, dental CPAs, and healthcare attorneys. Many partners had advisors review the terms, and we welcome the scrutiny.

What we caution against: paying a broker 8-12% of your deal value simply to make introductions. The Thurston Group has learned that partnerships structured for mutual benefit outperform exploitative deals. Over $4 billion in partner returns proves it.

Closer: If you have an advisor, bring them. If you do not, we can recommend independent dental transition specialists.

Underlying concern: "Am I too small? Am I not profitable enough?"

Three tiers: Community (1-3 doctors, $1M-$3M), Group (4-10 doctors, $3M-$10M), and Specialty. Typically $400K+ EBITDA, but quality matters more: strong patient relationships, stable team, healthy reputation.

Closer: The Explore conversation costs nothing but 45 minutes. Even when the fit is not right today, conversations often lead to partnerships 12-18 months later.

02

Financial Structure

Underlying concern: "Am I going to get lowballed?"

Transparent, EBITDA-based valuation methodology. Current market: 6-9x EBITDA for mid-market practices, with premiums for larger groups, strategic geography, and high-growth profiles. We evaluate revenue trends, patient mix, payer mix, and strategic fit.

We do not play accounting games. Your valuation is based on auditable financials -- we encourage your own CPA to review every number.

Closer: We walk you through valuation methodology step by step. Nothing is hidden. Our deal terms have survived independent scrutiny from dental CPAs across the country.

Underlying concern: "The equity did not turn out to be what they thought" -- #1 financial fear.

You receive 60-70% of valuation in cash at close. The remaining 30-40% is "rolled" into equity in the combined SGA platform -- you own shares in the entire network, not just your practice.

Your equity appreciates as the platform grows. Every new practice, every operational efficiency, every revenue increase across the network increases your stake. This is the "second bite of the apple."

The Thurston Group has executed this model across 8 platforms and returned $4B+ to partners. The equity appreciation story is not theoretical -- it has happened repeatedly.

Closer: We can connect you with current SGA partners who will tell you exactly how the equity model has performed. Real people. Real numbers.

Underlying concern: "Will I take a pay cut to work in my own office?"

Market-rate base salary plus production bonus (generally 25-30% above baseline). Total compensation comparable to or better than solo earnings -- without the overhead risk, staffing burden, and capital expenditures.

As a solo owner, "compensation" was whatever was left after expenses. As an SGA partner, clinical income is predictable, equity grows independently, and the 10-15 hours/week of admin burden is handled by professionals.

Closer: Dr. Hallissey: "I am more engaged with my patients -- QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office."

Underlying concern: "PE will flip us in 3 years."

The Thurston Group is structurally different from typical PE. Founded in 1988, 38 years building healthcare partnerships. Not a fund with a 5-year exit horizon. Their philosophy is patient capital: build value, compound returns, long-term relationships.

When an exit occurs, it benefits partners. $4B+ returned across all platforms is the proof. SGA partners can speak directly with partners from prior Thurston platforms who have been through exits.

Closer: We will connect you with Patrick Haynes or Daniel Davis at Thurston. Principals on the phone answering your questions directly. No competitor offers that transparency.

Underlying concern: "They are going to take 70% of my collections."

The management fee covers full operations (billing, payroll, HR, compliance, procurement, marketing, recruiting, technology). Structured as a percentage of revenue, designed to be fully offset by operational savings and revenue growth.

Hiring these functions independently: $297,000-$575,000/year. SGA's fee is a fraction of that through economies of scale across 100+ practices. You are a co-owner, not an employee -- the fee is for services, not a cut of your income.

Closer: During the Partner phase, we provide a detailed financial model showing projected income, fees, savings, and net impact. Full transparency. No surprises at close.

No. Full stop. Clinical autonomy is a structural principle, protected by governance and enforced by a Chief Clinical Officer -- Dr. Mitch Ellingson -- a practicing dentist.

You choose your materials, your lab, your schedule. No "crown days." No production quotas. No non-dentist managers overriding clinical judgment. SGA provides peer collaboration, mentorship, CE, and technology that expands what you CAN do -- not what you MUST do.

Closer: Dr. Ellingson: "We are going to ask you to lead more. We are going to amplify your voice." Call any SGA partner and ask: has anyone told you how to practice?

Underlying concern: "My patients and staff will freak out."

Your name stays. Non-negotiable. Dental Partners of SW Georgia is still Dental Partners of SW Georgia. Periodontal Associates of Memphis is still Periodontal Associates of Memphis. 100% of partner practices retain their name.

SGA operates as an invisible back-office partner. Your team stays. Benefits improve (Cigna health, 401(k) through Voya). 85% staff retention rate across the network.

Closer: Look at our partner practices' websites. Their names. Their branding. If you did not know SGA existed, you would never know. By design.

That fear is legitimate because it happens at other organizations. SGA was built as the structural opposite. No production targets by procedure type. No incentivized overtreatment. Our clinical advisory board is made up of partner dentists, not corporate executives.

Dr. Ellingson: "I want to protect my profession from becoming like medicine. We want it to be more about the doctors and the patients."
