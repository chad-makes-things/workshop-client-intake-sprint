Part 3: Review Platform Strategy

Platform priority by audience type. The right proof at the wrong platform does not convert.

Practice Partners / Professionals

Patients — MODIS / Innovative Dental

Review Request Process

🧾 For Patients
  1. 1At checkout: "We're so glad your appointment went well. Would you mind sharing your experience? A quick Google review helps other patients find us." Hand printed card with QR code to review page.
  2. 224 hours later: Automated text or email with direct link and 3-question prompt.
  3. 37 days later: Second request once only — do not repeat after two requests.
👔 For Practice Partners
  1. 1At 90-day check-in: "Your experience has been really positive. Would you be open to sharing a review on Google or Glassdoor? It helps other dentists evaluating their options."
  2. 2Provide a direct link — make it as easy as possible. Do not ask them to search for it.
Negative Review Protocol
  • • Respond within 24 hours
  • • Acknowledge the concern directly — do not be defensive
  • • Offer to resolve offline with contact information
  • • Do not reveal patient or partner details in public response

Part 4: Social Proof Placement Map

The right proof at the wrong stage of the funnel does not convert. This map specifies which proof type deploys where.

Top of Funnel — Awareness

TOFU: Establish Category Credibility

Numbers Only

The prospect does not know SGA exists or has only heard the name. They need to quickly understand why this is worth learning more about. No stories yet — pattern interruption and credibility signaling.

Proof Types
  • • Aggregate numbers: "100+ practices. 38 years. $4B+ returned."
  • • Third-party validation: press mentions, Becker's Dental features
  • • Authority signals: Thurston Group, Dr. Pikos, Dr. Olson (138K YT)
  • • Logo bar: recognizable SGA partner practice names
Where to Deploy
  • • Paid social ads (Facebook/Instagram)
  • • YouTube pre-roll
  • • Dentaltown sponsored content
  • • Google display
  • • Podcast sponsorships
Example Copy

"38 years. 8 platforms. $4B+ returned to partners."

Full stop. No claims. No stories. Credibility through scale.

Middle of Funnel — Consideration

MOFU: Differentiate and Peer Match

Specificity Required

The prospect knows SGA exists and is evaluating. They are actively comparing. MOFU readers are sophisticated (Schwartz Level 4) — they will dismiss generic claims.

Proof Types
  • • Named testimonials with specific outcomes (Godat, Cavendish 16.5%)
  • • Case study summaries — one-page format or teaser with "read full story"
  • • Comparison content framed through partner voices
  • • Avatar-matched proof: owner to owners, specialist to specialists
Where to Deploy
  • • Email nurture sequences
  • • Blog / SEO content
  • • LinkedIn thought leadership
  • • Retargeting ads
  • • Landing pages, webinar content
Example Copy

"QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office."
— Dr. Mitchel S. Godat

Bottom of Funnel — Decision

BOFU: Confirm and Remove Barriers

Strongest Proof Only

The prospect is ready to act. They are asking "why SGA and not someone else?" The BOFU proof does not need to persuade — it needs to confirm. Give every piece of evidence and then step back.

Proof Types
  • • Full-length video testimonials (3–5 min)
  • • Long-form case studies (1,500–2,000 words)
  • • "Call them yourself" — partner phone/email offered
  • • ROI calculator / valuation assessment
  • • Live or recorded partner panel Q&A
Where to Deploy
  • • Partnership landing pages
  • • Sales meeting follow-up emails
  • • Post-webinar follow-up
  • • Proposal appendix
  • • One-on-one sales conversations
Most Powerful BOFU Asset

"Here is Dr. Godat's email — ask him anything you want."

The "call them yourself" offer is more persuasive than any case study. Maintain a standing list of partners who have agreed to take calls.

Interviewer Principles

Questions by Avatar Type

The Single Most Important Question — Q21

"If you were having coffee with a [practice owner / associate / patient / specialist] who is exactly where you were 12–18 months ago — same situation, same fears — what would you say to them?"

Ask this every time. Never skip it. This question reliably produces the quotable, deployable sound bite that every other question has been building toward. Film the answer to this question as the opening of the video testimonial.

! Critical Notes for the Partnership Team

Never fabricate or embellish.

Every testimonial must be in the partner's own words, approved before publication, and attributable by name and practice. A single fabricated quote, if discovered, destroys the credibility of every real quote in the library.

Specificity beats superlatives.

"16.5% revenue growth" is more credible than "significant revenue improvement." "QuickBooks no longer calls me to the back office" is more credible than "I feel less stressed." Push for the specific detail in every collection effort.

Permission documentation is non-negotiable.

Every piece of proof using a real name, a real image, a real financial figure, or a real clinical outcome requires documented written permission. Create a simple release form and use it consistently.

The "call them yourself" offer is the most powerful BOFU proof.

When a prospective practice owner says "I want to speak to someone who has actually done this," the ability to say "Here is Dr. Godat's email — ask him anything you want" is more persuasive than any case study. Maintain a standing list of partners willing to take calls.
