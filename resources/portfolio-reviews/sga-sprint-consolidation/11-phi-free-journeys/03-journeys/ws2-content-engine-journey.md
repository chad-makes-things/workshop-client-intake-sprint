## Why WS2 Is Least Affected by PHI Removal

The Content Engine processes **content assets** — photos, videos, graphics, social posts. In Phase A, the type of content shifts from clinical (patient before/after photos, procedure documentation) to brand (team photos, office tours, provider introductions, marketing collateral). But the pipeline infrastructure is content-type-agnostic: capture, process, review, approve, distribute. Every interface works identically regardless of what's in the image.

**Phase A impact summary:**
- 8 of 9 interfaces ship (only CE-9 Event Mode Console deferred)
- 3 interfaces modified (CE-1 Capture Bot, CE-2 Capture App, CE-7 Dashboard)
- 5 interfaces completely unchanged (CE-3 through CE-6, CE-8)
- One analytics metric deferred: content-to-booking attribution (requires patient funnel data)

**What Maria sees**: A unified champion dashboard combining culture scores (from Culture OS, native WS3 data) and content scores (from Content Engine, via Kafka `sgadental.ws2.content-engagement-updated`). Maria does not know these come from different systems. She sees "my champion performance."

**Content mission delivery (Phase A change):**
In the original design, content missions could be triggered by PMS appointment data ("2 implant cases today — capture before/after"). In Phase A, missions are generated by the Content Engine's mission scheduler using non-patient triggers:

| Original Mission Trigger (Full PHI) | Phase A Mission Trigger (PHI-Free) |
|---|---|
| "2 implant cases today — capture before/after photos" | "Team photo day — capture the team before huddle" |
| "New patient has a great smile — ask about a testimonial" | "Film a 30-second 'Why I love working here' clip" |
| "Post-procedure B/A opportunity with patient consent" | "Capture the new break room renovation" |
| PMS: `appointment-completed` event | Content Engine scheduler: weekly brand content calendar |

**How missions reach Maria (Phase A):**
- PWA push notification: "New content mission available in your Champion Hub"
- In-app assignment visible when Maria opens the Hub
- NOT via SMS capture bot in Phase A. The original CE-1 Capture Bot used the Communications Service to send SMS prompts. In Phase A, the Communications Service is not active (it handles patient messaging infrastructure). Content missions use PWA push notifications and in-app assignments instead.

**Phase B Addition:** When Communications Service activates, the SMS/WhatsApp capture bot (CE-1) resumes sending capture briefs via text message. This is an additional delivery channel, not a replacement — PWA push continues working.

```
│  [Retake]  [Upload →]       │
└──────────────────────────────┘
```

Maria taps **"Upload."**

**What happens behind the scenes:**
1. Photo uploaded to S3 via Content Engine upload API (`POST /api/content/upload`)
2. Metadata attached: `practice_id`, `content_type: team_photo`, `captured_by`, `timestamp`
3. Kafka event: `sgadental.ws2.capture-media-received`
4. Content Engine AI pipeline activates:
   - **Color correction**: Auto white balance, exposure normalization (Claude Vision API)
   - **Smart crop**: Generate 3 variants — Instagram square (1:1), Facebook landscape (16:9), Google Business (4:3)
   - **Brand overlay**: Apply Gen4 brand watermark (subtle corner logo)
   - **Caption generation**: Claude Haiku generates captions for each platform:
     - Instagram: "Meet the incredible team at Smile Center Buckhead! From left: Dr. Patel, Maria, Lisa, and the whole crew keeping smiles bright in Buckhead. #SGADental #TeamSmiles #BuckheadDentist"
     - Facebook: "Our team at Smile Center Buckhead is ready to make your visit exceptional. Meet the people behind the smiles!"
     - Google Business: "The Smile Center Buckhead team"
5. Processing time: ~45 seconds for all variants and captions
6. Kafka event: `sgadental.ws2.content-asset-created` with status `pending_review`

**Offline behavior:** If Maria is offline (common in clinical areas with poor signal), the photo is stored in IndexedDB. The Capture screen shows: "Photo saved. Will upload when online." A badge on the Capture tab shows "1 pending upload." On reconnection, uploads resume automatically with a progress indicator.

Maria checks off "Group team photo" on her checklist. She proceeds to capture the break room shot and the 30-second video clip using the same flow.

## What's Different from the Original Design — Summary

| Area | Original (Full PHI) | Phase A (PHI-Free) | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Content types captured** | Clinical before/after, patient testimonials, team photos, office content | Team photos, office tours, provider portraits, events, marketing | Pipeline works identically; content subject matter changes |
| **Capture mission triggers** | PMS appointment data ("2 implant cases today") | Content calendar, brand freshness, practice events | Mission delivery shifts from procedure-driven to brand-driven |
| **Capture mission delivery** | SMS/WhatsApp via Communications Service + PWA | PWA push notifications + in-app assignment only | One fewer channel; functionality preserved |
| **Before/after photo mode** | Active with patient consent step and alignment guides | Hidden via feature flag | Camera UI still works; one mode is suppressed |
| **Caption generation** | Procedure-aware ("Beautiful implant result...") | Brand-aware ("Meet the incredible team...") | Same AI model; different content context |
| **Attribution analytics** | Content published correlated with patient bookings | Empty state: "Available in Phase B" | One dashboard panel shows empty state |
| **Event Mode Console (CE-9)** | Active for clinical conferences and courses | Deferred entirely | One interface deferred |
| **All other interfaces** | Fully functional | Fully functional | No difference |

## The Phase A Content Engine Value Proposition

The Content Engine is complete and valuable without PHI. It delivers:

1. **Brand content pipeline**: Capture team/office content, AI-process it, editorially review it, and distribute it across social channels and practice storefronts
2. **Champion engagement**: Champions have missions, scorecard tracking, streaks, and leaderboards based on content capture frequency and quality
3. **Brand health monitoring**: Website freshness, GMB completeness, directory sync, social engagement metrics — all measured without patient data
4. **Studio request workflow**: Marketing collateral design, review, and delivery
5. **Asset management**: Organized registry of all approved brand assets across the network

The only thing missing is the ability to measure whether published content drives patient bookings (attribution). Everything else — the creative pipeline, the distribution engine, the engagement analytics — works at full capacity.
