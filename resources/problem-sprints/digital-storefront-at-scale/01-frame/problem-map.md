# Problem Map

## Actors

| Actor | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Practice Owner / Office Manager** | The person at the practice level responsible for providing brand assets, approving changes, and occasionally interacting with their digital presence. Has 5-minute windows. Views HQ marketing as a nuisance. |
| **HQ Marketing Team** | ~10 people (market managers, creatives, web dev) responsible for digital presence across 260+ practices. Overwhelmed. Currently doing manual, per-practice work. |
| **Executive Leadership** | SGA leadership team (CEO, VP Marketing, platform leads) who need visibility into network digital health and want to ensure consistent brand quality across all practices. |
| **New Acquisition Practice** | A practice (or batch of 20-50) that just closed an acquisition deal and needs to be onboarded into SGA's digital ecosystem from scratch. Often has legacy assets, lost passwords, image-only logos. |
| **Patient / Prospective Patient** | The end consumer searching for a dentist, landing on a practice website, reading reviews, checking social media. Their experience is directly impacted by storefront quality. |

## Journey Steps (Detail)

### Step 1: Close Deal & Handoff
- **Description**: Acquisition closes. Practice transitions from independent to SGA network. Business development hands off to marketing/operations for digital onboarding.
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: Handoff is informal. No standard digital onboarding checklist. Marketing learns about the acquisition via email or meetings, sometimes weeks after close.
- **Risk**: If handoff is delayed or incomplete, digital setup starts late and practice operates with stale/independent digital presence.

### Step 2: Asset Access & Recovery
- **Description**: HQ team attempts to gain access to the practice's existing digital assets — website hosting, domain registrar, Google My Business, social media accounts, CRM, phone system.
- **Critical**: YES — THIS IS THE #1 BOTTLENECK
- **Current State**: Passwords are lost. The person who set up accounts left years ago. Meta won't cooperate. GoDaddy won't help. This alone takes days to weeks.
- **Risk**: Without access, nothing downstream can happen. Some practices have never had formal digital assets at all.

### Step 3: Brand Identity Capture
- **Description**: HQ captures what makes this practice unique — their specialty mix, team photos, office aesthetic, local market positioning, differentiators. Currently done via a workbook or (recently) a GPT-based conversational interview.
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: Practices have logos as image files (not vector), no brand guidelines, no photography standards. Differentiation is weak — "all GPs say the same thing."
- **Risk**: Without brand capture, the site is generic. With too much brand capture, the process takes weeks of back-and-forth.

### Step 4: Site Build & Deploy
- **Description**: HQ builds or migrates the practice website. Currently custom per practice with Barret (assumed CMS/agency). Need 2-3 optimized wireframe templates by specialty (GP, Perio, Advanced/Full Arch).
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: Every site is a little different. Each requires custom design conversations. Takes weeks to months. 90% of practices don't care what it looks like; 10% care deeply.
- **Risk**: Customization kills speed. Without templates, every site is a project. With templates, 10% of practices push back.

### Step 5: GMB + Social Setup
- **Description**: Google My Business profile optimized (weekly updates), social handles created/claimed (Instagram, Facebook, potentially YouTube/TikTok), initial content published.
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: Not standardized. Some practices have GMB, some don't. Social handles may be claimed by former staff. No consistent posting cadence across the network.
- **Risk**: GMB is the #1 local discovery channel. Without it optimized, patient acquisition suffers immediately.

### Step 6: Lead Gen & CRM Integration
- **Description**: Forms connected, lead capture active, automation sequences firing (thank you emails, appointment reminders, follow-up sequences). Phone system integrated for call tracking.
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: "Just the flow of lead management right now that isn't just very specific to like a paid search campaign" doesn't exist. No standard CRM across practices. No automation for basic form → follow-up.
- **Risk**: Beautiful storefront with no lead capture is a billboard with no phone number.

### Step 7: Day-to-Day Operations
- **Description**: Practice operates normally. Digital storefront exists but needs ongoing attention — social content, review management, seasonal promotions, local events.
- **Critical**: NO (individually), YES (in aggregate)
- **Current State**: "We couldn't say today as standard across 260 locations" whether practices are posting weekly. Most aren't. Nobody's managing this consistently.
- **Risk**: Storefronts decay. What looked great at setup looks abandoned in 6 months.

### Step 8: Change Events
- **Description**: Something changes at the practice — a doctor leaves, a new service is added, hours change, a renovation happens. This needs to cascade across all digital touchpoints.
- **Critical**: YES
- **Current State**: Changes are communicated via random emails or not at all. When a doctor leaves, their name remains on the website, social profiles, signage, and marketing materials indefinitely.
- **Risk**: Stale information erodes trust. A patient searching for "Dr. Johnson" who left 6 months ago has a bad experience.

### Step 9: Curate & Update (365-Day Cycle)
- **Description**: Ongoing, proactive curation of each practice's digital presence — content publishing, review responses, GMB updates, seasonal campaigns, performance optimization.
- **Critical**: YES — THIS IS THE SCALE CHALLENGE
- **Current State**: Not happening systematically. Hub-and-spoke model exists in strategy docs but isn't operationalized. Central team creates assets; local customization doesn't happen.
- **Risk**: Without ongoing curation, the initial investment in setup depreciates rapidly.

### Step 10: Executive Visibility Dashboard
- **Description**: Leadership can see, at a glance, the digital health of every practice — which are at baseline, which are optimized, which are falling behind, what the network-wide KPIs look like.
- **Critical**: MEDIUM
- **Current State**: No dashboard exists. No automated reporting. Leadership gets anecdotal updates.
- **Risk**: Without visibility, leadership can't prioritize resources, can't identify problems early, and can't measure ROI on the digital investment.

## Critical Moments

| Step | Risk | Opportunity |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Step 2: Asset Access** | Complete blocker — nothing moves without access. Can take weeks. | If automated or pre-staged during deal process, this bottleneck disappears entirely. |
| **Step 3: Brand Capture** | Back-and-forth kills momentum. Practices don't know what they want. | Opinionated templates + conversational AI capture = brand identity in a single session. |
| **Step 4: Site Build** | Custom design per practice = months of work × 260+ practices. | Template-first approach with specialty-specific wireframes = deploy in days, not months. |
| **Step 8: Change Events** | Undetected changes = stale content across 7+ touchpoints. | Event-triggered cascading updates = every change automatically propagates. |
| **Step 9: 365-Day Curation** | 10-person team cannot manually curate 260+ storefronts. | Automation-first with human QC on exceptions = coverage without headcount. |
