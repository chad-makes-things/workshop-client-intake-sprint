# Problem Map

## How to Read This Map

This map traces the left-to-right journey from **staff preparation** through **patient advocacy**, showing where the hospitality playbook is designed to activate — and where it currently fails. Actors are labeled at each step. Critical moments (where the sprint could intervene) are marked with ★.

---

## Actors

| Actor | Role in Journey |
|-------|----------------|
| **New Staff Member** | Front desk, hygienist, DA, or clinical support — primary target for training and certification |
| **Practice Owner / Office Manager** | Responsible for culture at the practice level; runs or delegates morning huddle |
| **Hygienist / DA / Doctor** | Clinical staff executing the trust transfer protocol during patient visit |
| **Patient** | Experiencing the hospitality behaviors (or not) at every touchpoint |
| **SGA Platform / HQ** | Responsible for network-wide training deployment, measurement, reporting, and reinforcement |
| **Grant / Executive Team** | Culture embodiment and executive championing — currently cannot scale to 260 practices |

## Journey Map

```
[STAFF ARRIVES]
      │
      ▼
STEP 1: ONBOARDING / ORIENTATION
Actor: New Staff Member + Practice Owner
Current state: Ad hoc, informal, practice-by-practice. No standard hospitality module.
Hospitality intent: Staff learns clinical duties but receives NO structured "Caring with Charisma"
  introduction. Playbook exists as a document; no one reads it.
Gap: Zero structured hospitality onboarding. No certification milestone. No baseline measurement.
      │
      ▼
STEP 2: TRAINING COMPLETION ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: New Staff Member + SGA Platform
Current state: 4 phone-skills videos tied to Cinnamon analytics exist as a precedent; but only
  for phone interactions, not full hospitality behaviors.
Hospitality intent: Staff should complete a certification curriculum before engaging patients
  unsupervised — covering scripted responses, environmental behaviors, trust transfer protocol,
  morning huddle participation.
Gap: No comprehensive curriculum. No LMS. No completion tracking. No credential awarded.
      │
      ▼
STEP 3: MORNING HUDDLE ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: Practice Owner / Office Manager + All Staff
Current state: Dental Intel provides a platform; Gen 4 offices use it. Grant runs Monday
  meetings. But 4 out of 5 days have no structured hospitality content. Check-ins are manual.
Hospitality intent: Every morning starts with a 5-minute hospitality ritual — a short video,
  a topic (e.g., "trust transfer this week"), a team commitment, and a visible metric (reviews
  from yesterday, huddle streak).
Gap: No content library. No topic rotation. No completion verification. No connection to outcomes.
      │
      ▼
STEP 4: PATIENT ARRIVAL / PHONE INTERACTION
Actor: Front Desk Staff + Patient
Current state: Cinnamon captures phone calls. Top 4 phone issues identified. Training videos
  created for phone skills — this works.
Hospitality intent: Every incoming call follows a scripted hospitality protocol (warm greeting,
  "it's my pleasure" equivalents, zero friction scheduling). In-person arrival triggers
  environmental hospitality standards (cleanliness, refreshments, ambient music/fragrance).
Gap: Phone behavior tracked; in-person arrival not tracked. Environmental compliance manual.
      │
      ▼
STEP 5: WAITING ROOM / PRE-CHAIR EXPERIENCE
Actor: Front Desk Staff + Patient
Current state: Environmental standards defined in playbook. No verification mechanism. No
  reporting to SGA HQ.
Hospitality intent: Waiting room meets defined aesthetics (cleanliness, refreshments, signage,
  ambient experience). Staff proactively engages — walks patients, uses names, anticipates needs.
Gap: No environmental compliance check. No secret shopper equivalent. No score.
      │
      ▼
STEP 6: HYGIENIST HANDOFF — TRUST TRANSFER STEP 1 ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: Hygienist + Patient + Front Desk
Current state: Trust transfer protocol defined but not tracked. Hygienist is supposed to
  reinforce what the front desk established (warmth, why they're here, clinical context).
Hospitality intent: Hygienist greets patient using their name, references the relationship
  ("I see you're in for your 6-month cleaning — let's make sure today is great for you"), and
  sets up the DA handoff.
Gap: No verification. No voice data captured in operatory. No linkage to patient satisfaction.
      │
      ▼
STEP 7: DA HANDOFF — TRUST TRANSFER STEP 2
Actor: DA + Patient + Hygienist
Current state: DA may or may not reinforce the hygienist's setup. No protocol consistency.
Hospitality intent: DA takes the baton — reinforces trust, introduces doctor context ("Dr. X
  has been doing this for 20 years and really cares about painless experiences"), maintains
  warmth continuity.
Gap: No tracking. No training module specific to DA hospitality behaviors.
      │
      ▼
STEP 8: DOCTOR ENCOUNTER — TRUST TRANSFER STEP 3
Actor: Doctor + Patient + DA
Current state: Doctor interaction is highly variable. Some doctors are "Grant-like"; many are
  purely clinical. No hospitality expectation set for doctors.
Hospitality intent: Doctor validates the trust built by the entire team — uses patient name,
  acknowledges anxiety, confirms treatment plan in human language, closes with warmth.
Gap: Doctor training is the hardest — highest autonomy, lowest compliance culture. No protocol.
      │
      ▼
STEP 9: CHECKOUT / EXIT EXPERIENCE ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: Front Desk Staff + Patient
Current state: Checkout is a transactional moment. No structured hospitality exit protocol.
Hospitality intent: Front desk completes the circle — thanks patient by name, reminds them of
  next visit, provides a warm send-off, and within the next 24 hours triggers a review request
  via Swell.
Gap: Exit protocol not codified. Review trigger not tied to hospitality score or behavior completion.
      │
      ▼
STEP 10: POST-VISIT FOLLOW-UP / REVIEW SIGNAL ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: SGA Platform + Patient + Swell
Current state: Swell sends review requests. 5-star reviews are tracked. No linkage between
  review content and which hospitality behaviors were actually executed during that visit.
Hospitality intent: Review signals are fed back into the hospitality score — which practices
  are performing, which behaviors correlate with 5-star outcomes, which staff certifications
  correlate with better reviews.
Gap: Data exists in silos. No attribution model. No feedback loop to staff or practice owners.
      │
      ▼
STEP 11: NETWORK REPORTING / ACCOUNTABILITY
Actor: SGA Platform + Regional Directors + Practice Owners
Current state: Manual. No hospitality score. No leaderboard. No benchmark comparison.
Hospitality intent: Regional directors see a real-time "Hospitality Score" per practice —
  certification rates, huddle completion rates, voice behavior compliance, review velocity.
  Leaderboard drives healthy competition. Bottom quartile gets intervention support.
Gap: No unified score. No reporting infrastructure. No connection between behavior and outcome data.
      │
      ▼
STEP 12: PATIENT ADVOCACY / REFERRAL ★ CRITICAL MOMENT
Actor: Patient + Community + Practice
Current state: Referrals happen organically. No systematic connection between hospitality
  investment and new patient acquisition.
Hospitality intent: Patients who experience "Caring with Charisma" become active advocates —
  writing reviews, referring friends, returning consistently. This is the business outcome that
  makes the hospitality investment measurable and justifiable.
Gap: No attribution. No referral program tied to hospitality behaviors. No closed loop.
```

## Critical Moments Summary

| Step | Moment | Why Critical |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Step 2 | Training Completion | Where culture gets built or bypassed at hire |
| Step 3 | Morning Huddle | Daily flywheel — sets the tone for the entire day |
| Step 6 | Hygienist Handoff (Trust Transfer 1) | Highest leverage in-chair moment |
| Step 9 | Checkout / Exit | Final impression + review trigger |
| Step 10 | Review Signal Attribution | Where behavior connects to business outcome |
| Step 12 | Patient Advocacy | The ultimate proof point for the program's ROI |

## Current State vs. Intent Summary

| Actor | Current Reality | What the Playbook Intends |
|-------|----------------|--------------------------|
| New Staff | No hospitality onboarding | Certification before patient contact |
| Practice Owner | Inconsistent huddle facilitation | Daily structured ritual with content |
| Clinical Staff | Trust transfer protocol not executed consistently | Traceable 3-step handoff every patient |
| Patient | Variable experience across 260 practices | Consistent 5-star experience regardless of location |
| SGA HQ | No visibility into behavior adoption | Real-time hospitality score per practice |
| Grant / Exec Team | Culture embodied by individuals, not system | Culture embedded in process and platform |
