# Goal and Sprint Questions

## Long-Term Goal (2–5 Years)

**In 5 years**, every SGA team member — from front desk coordinator to lead hygienist — will be a certified "Caring with Charisma" practitioner. New staff will complete a structured onboarding curriculum that functions like a professional credential (not an HR checkbox). Every morning huddle will begin with a structured 5-minute hospitality ritual reinforced by video content. Hospitality behaviors will be tracked automatically through voice analytics, review signals, and environmental compliance checks — and every practice will have a visible score. The "Caring with Charisma" credential will be recognized industry-wide, adopted by at least 5 dental schools as curriculum, and serve as SGA's most powerful recruiting and retention differentiator.

**In 2 years**, SGA will have operationalized the hospitality playbook across 80% of its practices, with staff completing certification at hire, morning huddles running structured content 5 days a week, and a real-time hospitality score visible to every practice owner and regional director. At least 30% of measurable 5-star review growth will be attributable to certified hospitality behavior adoption.

## Sprint Questions

Sprint questions define what we need to learn during this sprint. Each question is tagged with risk level and rationale.

---

### SQ1: Can we reliably train and certify staff on specific hospitality behaviors using existing tech infrastructure?

**Risk Level**: HIGH
**Rationale**: This is the core operationalization problem. Training 260+ practices' worth of rotating staff is expensive and inconsistently executed. If the LMS or certification system doesn't fit into daily workflow, it won't be completed. The training precedent (phone skills videos tied to Cinnamon) is encouraging but was one cohort, not ongoing. We need to understand if a scalable certification path — required for new hires, accessible on mobile, short-form — can realistically reach 100% of staff.

---

### SQ2: Can we measure hospitality behavior adoption using signals already available in the tech stack (Cinnamon voice, Swell reviews, Dental Intel huddle check-ins)?

**Risk Level**: HIGH
**Rationale**: Measurement is the only way to move from "we hope this is working" to accountability. The existing tech stack (Cinnamon call analytics, Swell review data, Dental Intel huddle completion) contains meaningful hospitality signals — but they aren't currently synthesized into a unified score or dashboard. If we can't build reliable measurement from existing signals, the entire accountability layer collapses, and the program becomes a mandate with no feedback loop.

---

### SQ3: Will staff genuinely engage with a hospitality certification if it has real career value (credential, jacket, resume-worthy) versus treating it as mandatory compliance?

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Rationale**: The difference between a training program that gets gamed and one that builds culture is whether staff perceive it as valuable to themselves. SGA's instinct — a jacket, a credential that can go on a resume, a bonus pool tied to outcomes — is directionally correct. This question tests whether the motivation architecture (intrinsic + extrinsic) is sufficient to drive voluntary, high-quality engagement, especially in high-turnover dental front office roles.

---

### SQ4: Can morning huddle content (video + structured exercises) be made compelling enough that it actually happens every day, consistently, without direct executive supervision?

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Rationale**: Grant currently runs Monday morning meetings and is charismatic enough to make them work. The challenge is replicating that energy across 260 practices on every day of the week. Pre-produced video content, structured facilitation guides, and topic rotation are all possible — but the question is whether practice owners and lead staff will run the content consistently when Grant isn't watching. Dental Intel already provides the platform; the question is content quality and incentive alignment.

---

### SQ5: Can we create a lightweight "trust transfer" tracking mechanism that verifies the hygienist-to-DA-to-doctor handoff protocol is being executed — without creating excessive documentation burden?

**Risk Level**: LOW-MEDIUM
**Rationale**: The trust transfer protocol is one of the highest-leverage hospitality behaviors because it affects every patient's experience with every provider. Cinnamon captures phone interactions but not in-room handoffs. This question tests whether voice data, post-appointment review signals, or structured huddle documentation can serve as a proxy for trust transfer compliance without requiring staff to fill out additional forms. Lightweight verification is achievable; full real-time tracking may be overbuilt for this sprint.

---

## Risk Summary

| Question | Risk | Why It Matters |
|----------|------|----------------|
| SQ1: Staff certification via existing tech | HIGH | Core operationalization bet |
| SQ2: Behavior measurement from existing signals | HIGH | Without this, no accountability |
| SQ3: Career-value motivation architecture | MEDIUM | Culture vs. compliance |
| SQ4: Morning huddle consistency without supervision | MEDIUM | Daily ritual is the flywheel |
| SQ5: Trust transfer tracking (lightweight) | LOW-MEDIUM | High leverage, tricky to verify |
