# Sprint Target

## What is a Sprint Target?

The sprint target identifies the single part of the journey map where focused design effort will create the most leverage. Rather than trying to solve the entire problem, the sprint focuses on the moment where the right solution changes everything downstream.

---

## Target Candidates

### Candidate A: Staff Onboarding → Training Completion (Step 1–2 on the Problem Map)

**Framing**: The moment a new staff member arrives and completes (or doesn't complete) the "Caring with Charisma" certification before touching patients.

**Why this is a strong target**:
- Every problem downstream (inconsistent huddles, weak trust transfers, low review scores) traces back to staff who were never properly trained on hospitality behaviors
- A certification required at hire creates a natural forcing function — unlike optional programs, it happens before the pressure of daily patient work sets in
- The training precedent (phone skills videos + Cinnamon verification) already proved this model works for SGA
- Solving this creates a permanent culture injection point that scales with every new hire across all 260 practices
- Builds toward the "dental schools adopt it" north star — a curriculum is the artifact

**Risks**:
- High-turnover dental front offices mean training investment gets depleted quickly without reinforcement
- Content creation is expensive and time-consuming; getting Grant on camera consistently requires executive time commitment
- Doesn't address existing staff who were never certified — creates a two-tier workforce without a retroactive track

**Feasibility**: HIGH — LMS platforms (Northpass, Lessonly) can be deployed quickly; video content is a production challenge, not a technology challenge; Dental Intel already provides a completion tracking mechanism

### Candidate B: Morning Huddle (Step 3 on the Problem Map) ★ RECOMMENDED

**Framing**: The moment every morning where the entire practice team is together for 5 minutes — the highest-leverage touchpoint for culture reinforcement, behavior activation, and accountability that currently has no structured hospitality content.

**Why this is the strongest target**:
- The morning huddle is the ONLY moment in the entire practice day when every staff member is together before patients arrive — a guaranteed, repeating, daily intervention point
- Dental Intel already provides the platform infrastructure used by Gen 4 offices; the problem is purely content and activation, not new technology
- Grant already runs Monday meetings and they work — the proof of concept exists; the problem is replication for 5 days/week and 260 practices
- Solving the morning huddle solves multiple problems simultaneously: it is the training reinforcement mechanism, the accountability moment, the culture ritual, AND the daily metric review
- The morning huddle can serve as the "delivery system" for the entire hospitality playbook — all behaviors (trust transfer, phone scripts, environmental standards) can be introduced, rotated, and reinforced here
- This target aligns with both new-hire reinforcement (they join the huddle from day 1) and existing staff (they get brought into the program without a separate retroactive track)
- Highest frequency intervention: 260 practices × 5 days/week = 1,300 culture touchpoints per week vs. a one-time onboarding event

**Risks**:
- Content production at scale (260 days of meaningful video content per year) requires significant investment
- Practice owner buy-in is required — if the owner doesn't run it or delegate it properly, it doesn't happen
- Content quality must compete with staff's attention in a busy pre-patient environment; bad content gets ignored

**Feasibility**: HIGH — Dental Intel platform exists; video production is the bottleneck; Grant's participation in initial content creation is the key unlock

### Candidate C: Post-Visit Review Signal Attribution (Step 9–10 on the Problem Map)

**Framing**: The moment a patient checks out and the system captures (or fails to capture) the connection between which hospitality behaviors were executed during that visit and the patient's resulting review score.

**Why this is a strong target**:
- This is where behavior-to-outcome attribution lives — without this link, the entire program is faith-based (we believe it works) rather than evidence-based (we know it works)
- Swell and Cinnamon already capture relevant signals; the problem is synthesis and attribution
- A closed-loop measurement system that shows "practices with higher huddle completion rates have 2.3x more 5-star reviews" is the most powerful executive argument for continuing to invest in the program
- Builds toward the north star: an academic paper showing "Caring with Charisma certification correlates with patient retention and new patient acquisition" is what gets dental schools interested

**Risks**:
- Attribution is genuinely difficult — many factors influence reviews beyond hospitality behavior (wait time, pain levels, billing disputes)
- Requires significant data engineering work to connect Cinnamon, Swell, and Dental Intel signals into a unified attribution model
- This target produces the measurement system but not the training or culture change — it validates what's working, but there's no guarantee the behaviors are present yet to measure

**Feasibility**: MEDIUM — data infrastructure requires new integration work; attribution model is analytically challenging; but the underlying data exists

## Target Recommendation

### ★ RECOMMENDED TARGET: Morning Huddle (Candidate B)

**Score**: 22/25

**Reasoning**: The morning huddle is the highest-leverage, highest-frequency intervention point in the entire problem map. It is the only moment in the day where the entire team is together — before patients arrive — and where culture can be actively shaped rather than passively hoped for. Dental Intel already provides the platform; the bottleneck is content and activation design.

Critically, the morning huddle serves as the **delivery system for the entire playbook**. Solving this target solves the others:
- Certification completion can be tracked and celebrated in the huddle
- Trust transfer protocols are introduced and rotated weekly in the huddle
- Hospitality scores (from Swell + Cinnamon) are reviewed in the huddle
- Environmental compliance is checked as a huddle opening ritual
- The leaderboard lives in the huddle

This is the flywheel. Everything else is a spoke.

**The sprint question for this target**: Can we design a morning huddle content system and activation framework so compelling and easy to run that 80% of SGA practices complete a structured hospitality huddle at least 4 days per week within 6 months of launch?
