# Expert Notes: HMW Notes + Expert Insights

## How Might We (HMW) Notes

HMW notes are reframes of problems as opportunities. Each note opens a design possibility without prescribing a solution. Grouped by theme below.

---

### Theme 1: Training & Certification Design

**HMW-01**: How might we make "Caring with Charisma" certification feel like a professional credential that staff are proud to put on a resume — not just a mandatory HR training?

**HMW-02**: How might we break the hospitality curriculum into micro-modules (under 5 minutes each) that can be completed between patients on a phone, without requiring a scheduled training block?

**HMW-03**: How might we use Grant's charisma and on-camera presence to make training videos genuinely entertaining — more like YouTube than compliance training?

**HMW-04**: How might we design a certification pathway that has visible levels (Bronze, Silver, Gold) so staff always have something to work toward, not just a single pass/fail gate?

**HMW-05**: How might we tie certification completion to something staff actually care about — a physical jacket, a bonus unlock, a visible badge in their staff profile — rather than just an email confirmation?

---

### Theme 2: Morning Huddle as Culture Flywheel

**HMW-06**: How might we pre-produce 260 days of 5-minute morning huddle content so that any practice owner can run a high-quality huddle without being Grant?

**HMW-07**: How might we make the morning huddle content feel spontaneous and local (referencing yesterday's reviews, this week's focus) even when it's pre-recorded?

**HMW-08**: How might we design topic rotation across 5 days (Monday: trust transfer, Tuesday: environmental standards, etc.) so the playbook is systematically reviewed every week without feeling repetitive?

**HMW-09**: How might we use the morning huddle as the primary accountability moment — where yesterday's hospitality scores are reviewed and today's intention is set — rather than a passive viewing experience?

**HMW-10**: How might we give practice owners a "Done for you Monday" experience — where they simply press play and a pre-built video with their practice's actual data walks the team through the week?

---

### Theme 3: Measurement & Accountability

**HMW-11**: How might we build a "Hospitality Score" for every practice that is automatically updated daily — using signals already in Cinnamon, Swell, and Dental Intel — without requiring manual input?

**HMW-12**: How might we use Cinnamon voice analytics to detect specific hospitality behaviors on phone calls (warm greetings, zero hold time, scripted responses) and score them automatically?

**HMW-13**: How might we create a network-wide leaderboard that feels like healthy competition rather than surveillance — where practices opt into being ranked and celebrate top performers publicly?

**HMW-14**: How might we connect the dots between staff certification rate and 5-star review rate — so that SGA can prove (with data) that "Caring with Charisma" certification drives business outcomes?

**HMW-15**: How might we detect when trust transfer is NOT happening based on review sentiment signals (patients mentioning feeling rushed, confusion about their treatment, no warmth from staff)?

---

### Theme 4: Staff Motivation & Engagement

**HMW-16**: How might we design a bonus pool model tied to 5-star review count that gives every staff member a visible stake in the outcome — not just the practice owner?

**HMW-17**: How might we create a "Hospitality Wall of Fame" at each practice where reviews mentioning specific staff by name are printed and celebrated?

**HMW-18**: How might we make new hire onboarding feel like joining an elite hospitality culture (like starting at the Ritz Carlton) rather than onboarding at a dental office?

**HMW-19**: How might we design the certification program so that high-turnover front desk staff carry the credential with them when they leave — making SGA-trained staff a recognized quality signal in the dental labor market?

**HMW-20**: How might we create a "Hospitality Captain" role at each practice — a certified culture ambassador who owns huddle facilitation, training completion tracking, and team accountability?

---

### Theme 5: Environmental & In-Practice Compliance

**HMW-21**: How might we build a simple environmental compliance checklist (waiting room standards, refreshments, music, cleanliness) into the morning huddle so it happens before every patient day?

**HMW-22**: How might we use patient photos, Google review language, or periodic digital walkthroughs to verify environmental standards without sending a mystery shopper to 260 locations?

**HMW-23**: How might we make the environmental standards part of the certification — so staff can only advance to the next level if their practice passes a documented environmental review?

---

### Theme 6: Network Scale & Consistency

**HMW-24**: How might we design the playbook so that a rural practice with 4 staff and an urban cosmetic practice with 30 staff both find it equally applicable — without requiring two separate programs?

**HMW-25**: How might we build a "Caring with Charisma" brand identity so strong that dental schools, hiring platforms, and industry events recognize the credential as the gold standard for patient experience?

## Expert Insights

### From the Hospitality Industry

**Ritz Carlton — The Gold Standards System**: The Ritz Carlton operationalizes its service philosophy through "Gold Standards" — a card every employee carries with 12 service values. Crucially, every Ritz Carlton shift begins with a "Daily Line-Up" (equivalent to a morning huddle) where one of the 12 values is read and discussed. This ritual-based reinforcement means the culture is reviewed in rotation every 12 days. Implication for SGA: Topic rotation is the mechanism, not just content volume. The daily ritual IS the culture delivery system.

**Chick-fil-A — Culture as Franchise Requirement**: Chick-fil-A's operator model creates intense culture alignment by making the franchisee personally accountable for team behavior. "It's my pleasure" is not optional — it's a brand standard verified through mystery shoppers. Critically, Chick-fil-A invests more in operator selection (values alignment) than in post-hire correction. Implication for SGA: The hospitality certification should be a hiring filter, not just a training artifact. Practices that want to be "certified" should select for hospitality orientation at hire.

**Disney University — Operational Mythology**: Disney's training program ("Traditions") runs before new hires ever touch a guest. Cast members learn Disney history, language (guests, not customers; costumes, not uniforms), and philosophy before performing any job function. The mythology precedes the mechanics. Implication for SGA: The "Caring with Charisma" certification should begin with the story — why this matters, what patient experience means, the vision of dentistry as wellness — before any scripted behavior is introduced.

**Four Seasons — Cross-Property Consistency**: The Four Seasons achieves consistency across 120+ properties in 50 countries through a shared culture framework, documented standards, and property general managers who are personally evaluated on employee engagement scores. Culture is on the GM's scorecard. Implication for SGA: Practice owners need to be personally accountable for their practice's hospitality score — and that score needs to be tied to their performance review or incentive structure.

### From Behavior Change Science

**B.J. Fogg — Tiny Habits**: Behavior change is most durable when new behaviors are (1) small enough to require minimal motivation, (2) anchored to existing behaviors (habit stacking), and (3) immediately celebrated. Implication for SGA: Morning huddle hospitality rituals should be under 5 minutes, anchored to the existing huddle habit, and end with a visible team celebration (e.g., reviewing yesterday's 5-star reviews). Complexity kills adoption.

**Fogg Behavior Model**: Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. All three must be present. For SGA: Motivation = bonus pool + credential + career value. Ability = short video, simple script, easy Dental Intel check-in. Prompt = morning huddle reminder, Cinnamon feedback, weekly score report. The system fails if any one of the three is absent.

**Dan Ariely — Intrinsic Motivation in the Workplace**: Ariely's research on "meaningful work" shows that workers disengage when they don't see the impact of their actions. Implication for SGA: Connecting staff behavior (e.g., using a patient's name 3x during a visit) to a specific outcome (that patient wrote a 5-star review mentioning their name) creates the feedback loop that makes the behavior self-reinforcing without ongoing external pressure.

**Grit and Deliberate Practice (Duckworth / Ericsson)**: Elite skill development requires deliberate, structured practice with immediate feedback — not passive learning. Implication for SGA: Training videos alone won't work. The curriculum needs to include practice simulations, role-plays, or structured scenarios where staff can practice responses and receive immediate feedback before executing with real patients.

### From Learning & Training Technology

**Lessonly (now Seismic Learning) — Modern LMS Design**: Modern LMSs prioritize short-form lessons (< 5 minutes), mobile-first delivery, scenario-based practice, and completion tracking with manager visibility. Top-performing platforms report 90%+ completion rates when modules are under 5 minutes and tied to on-the-job triggers. Implication for SGA: A custom "Caring with Charisma" LMS could be as simple as a WhatsApp-style microlearning channel with video + quiz + scenario — no dedicated platform needed if the content is right.

**Northpass (now Gainsight PX) — External Certification Programs**: Northpass was built specifically for companies that want to certify external users (partners, customers) — with branded portals, progress dashboards, certificate issuance, and API integrations. Implication for SGA: The credentialing infrastructure for "Caring with Charisma" is off-the-shelf — the investment is in content and culture architecture, not platform development.

**Gamification in Enterprise Training (Octalysis Framework — Yu-kai Chou)**: The most effective workplace gamification systems tap into "Epic Meaning & Calling" (being part of something bigger), "Accomplishment" (progress, levels, completion), and "Social Influence" (leaderboards, community). Pure point-scoring without social context creates short-lived engagement. Implication for SGA: The leaderboard and bonus pool are only half the system — the "epic meaning" layer (dental schools adopting this, being part of the first DSO to operationalize 5-star culture) is what makes it sustainable.

### From Healthcare Culture Change

**Mayo Clinic — Culture as Clinical Strategy**: Mayo's "patients first" culture is operationalized through a specific hierarchy of priorities (patient needs first, then needs of the patient group, then institutional needs). This hierarchy is taught at hire and referenced in every decision. Implication for SGA: "Caring with Charisma" needs a clear value hierarchy that staff can reference in ambiguous situations — "when in doubt, choose the more hospitable option."

**ChenMed — Culture at Scale in Healthcare**: ChenMed operates 100+ primary care clinics with a culture-first model where doctors are trained in "lovology" — caring for patients like family members. Every ChenMed physician receives cultural onboarding before clinical onboarding. Culture is measured through patient satisfaction scores that appear on physician scorecards. Implication for SGA: Doctors and hygienists need cultural onboarding, not just clinical onboarding — and their hospitality behaviors need to appear on their performance scorecards.

**Press Ganey — Patient Experience Measurement**: Press Ganey, the leading patient satisfaction measurement company, has found that emotional experience (feeling heard, feeling cared for) accounts for more than 50% of the variance in patient satisfaction scores — more than clinical quality or wait times. Implication for SGA: The trust transfer protocol and warm handoff behaviors are not "nice to have" — they are the primary drivers of the 5-star reviews SGA is trying to generate.

### From Franchise & Certification Systems

**McDonald's Hamburger University**: McDonald's trains 5,000 leaders annually at its global training centers, with 115+ courses in 28 languages. The key design principle: leadership training precedes operational training. Culture is Module 1. Implication for SGA: The "Caring with Charisma" credential should be required before any staff member takes on patient-facing responsibility — it is Module 1, not an optional add-on.

**Starbucks — Barista Certification System**: Starbucks uses a level-based certification (Coffee Master, Black Apron) that creates aspirational identity among baristas. Black Apron certification requires demonstrated expertise, a tasting panel, and visible symbols (the apron itself). This creates a self-selecting culture of excellence. Implication for SGA: The "jacket" idea is exactly right — physical symbols of achievement create identity investment that drives sustained behavior.

**The Joint Commission — Healthcare Accreditation**: Healthcare accreditation programs provide a powerful external validation model: facilities that achieve certification gain marketing value, recruiting advantages, and insurance credentialing benefits. Implication for SGA: The long-term moat for "Caring with Charisma" is external recognition — if the credential is recognized by dental schools, payers, or hiring platforms, it becomes self-perpetuating.
