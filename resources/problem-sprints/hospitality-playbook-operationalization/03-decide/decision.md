# Decision Record

## Sprint Target (Confirmed — Phase 3)

**Morning Huddle as Culture Flywheel** — Design a morning huddle content system and activation framework so compelling and easy to run that 80% of SGA practices complete a structured hospitality huddle at least 4 days per week within 6 months of launch.

---

Solutions are scored 1–5 against each sprint question. Each score is accompanied by a one-sentence rationale.

**Sprint Questions**:
- SQ1: Can we reliably train and certify staff on specific hospitality behaviors using existing tech infrastructure?

## Recommendation

### RECOMMENDED: Solution 1 — The Huddle OS (with Solution 2 as Phase 2)

**Reasoning**: The sprint target — making the morning huddle happen consistently at 80% of practices — is best served by removing friction from the execution, not by adding complexity. The Huddle OS solves the hardest problem directly: practice owners don't run consistent huddles because they don't have content that's worth running. When the content is excellent, turnkey, and personalized to their practice's actual data (yesterday's reviews, this week's H-Score trend), the resistance drops to near zero.

The Huddle OS also serves as the **delivery system for every other solution**:
- Certification modules (Solution 2) are introduced in the huddle
- H-Score trends (Solution 3) are reviewed in the huddle
- Trust transfer reminders (Solution 4) rotate weekly in the huddle
- Champion-led culture moments (Solution 5) can replace the scripted content as practices mature

Building the Huddle OS first creates the daily ritual container into which all other elements can be layered — without requiring a complete platform build on day 1. The content production investment is real but is also the most direct path to the cultural impact SGA is trying to achieve.

**Note on Solution 2 (CCA Academy)**: The score is identical, and Solution 2 solves a different (but equally important) problem — the motivation architecture. The recommended sequence is: **Huddle OS first** (solves the delivery problem), **CCA Academy second** (solves the motivation problem). Running them simultaneously risks spreading execution capacity too thin.
