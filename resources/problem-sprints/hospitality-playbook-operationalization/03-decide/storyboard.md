# Storyboard: The Hospitality Playbook Platform

## User Decision (Phase 5)

The user selected **all five solutions** as components of one unified architecture — The Hospitality Playbook Platform. This storyboard traces how three distinct users experience the platform across time: a **new staff member**, a **practice owner**, and a **network administrator at SGA HQ**.

---

## Characters

| Character | Role | Entry Point |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Maya** | New front desk coordinator, just hired at a mid-sized SGA practice in Phoenix | First day of onboarding |
| **Dr. Vasquez** | Practice owner, 8-year SGA partner, used to doing things manually | First platform notification |
| **Jordan** | SGA Network Operations Director, oversees 80 practices across the Southwest | Weekly network review |

---

## SCENE 1: Practice Onboarding — The Platform Arrives

**Who**: Dr. Vasquez (practice owner)
**Where**: Office, 7:45 AM, Monday
**Moment**: SGA has launched The Hospitality Playbook Platform. Dr. Vasquez receives a one-page setup guide and a tablet pre-configured with the Playbook dashboard.

**Action**: Dr. Vasquez opens the dashboard for the first time. The screen shows: "Welcome to The Hospitality Playbook Platform. Your practice is being activated. Here's where you stand today." Below it: an H-Score baseline reading of 54/100 (Developing), a huddle completion rate of 0% for this week, and a zero certification rate for staff.

**System Response**: The platform has automatically pulled Dr. Vasquez's existing Swell review history (last 90 days) and Cinnamon phone analytics to generate the baseline H-Score. It has auto-enrolled all current staff in the CCA Bronze track and pre-loaded 5 days of morning huddle content into Dental Intel. A personalized "Your First 30 Days" roadmap appears — 3 actions, in priority order: (1) Run your first huddle today, (2) Send staff the CCA Bronze welcome link, (3) Designate your Caring with Charisma Champion.

**User Reaction**: Dr. Vasquez is mildly surprised to see an H-Score already calculated. "54 — that's lower than I expected." He feels a mix of curiosity and mild competitiveness. He clicks into the H-Score breakdown and sees Swell reviews have been average (3.8 stars over 90 days) and phone answer rates have been inconsistent. He decides to run the huddle today.

**Critical Detail**: The baseline H-Score arriving pre-calculated — before the practice has done anything — creates immediate accountability and intrinsic motivation. The score feels real because it is derived from real data the practice already generated.

---

## SCENE 2: First Morning Huddle — The Ritual Begins

**Who**: Dr. Vasquez + 6-person practice team (Maya not yet hired)
**Where**: Break room, 7:55 AM, Monday
**Moment**: The first platform-powered morning huddle

**Action**: Dr. Vasquez clicks "Start Today's Huddle" on the Dental Intel screen. A 5-minute guided experience auto-loads. Today is Week 1, Theme 1: "Caring with Charisma — What We Believe." Grant appears on screen in a 60-second video: direct to camera, warm, speaking as if he's in the room. He tells the story of a patient who cried at checkout because she hadn't been to a dentist in 8 years and expected to be judged — and wasn't. "That moment is what we're building here. Not just clean teeth. Something people carry home."

**System Response**: After Grant's video, the screen transitions to "Yesterday's Wins" — even on day 1, the system surfaces the last Swell 5-star review received by the practice (a patient named Jim who mentioned the front desk by name). The team sees Jim's review on screen. Then the "Today's Practice" segment: a 90-second role-play prompt — the first hospitality phrase, the warm greeting protocol. Staff pair up for 30 seconds. The huddle closes with a shared intention card read aloud: "Today, every patient feels like the most important person in this building."

**User Reaction**: One staff member says "that's cheesy" but smiles when she reads it aloud. Another pulls up Jim's review on her phone to show the others. Dr. Vasquez notices the team is more energized than usual going into the patient day. He marks huddle complete in Dental Intel.

**Critical Detail**: The real Swell review on-screen — a real patient, a real name, a real reaction — creates an emotional anchor that no manufactured training example can replicate. The platform surfaces what's already there rather than manufacturing fictional scenarios.

## SCENE 3: Maya's First Day — Onboarding into the Playbook

**Who**: Maya (new front desk coordinator)
**Where**: Practice break room, 8:30 AM, two weeks after platform launch
**Moment**: Maya's first day. Her office manager hands her a tablet showing the "Caring with Charisma Academy" onboarding screen.

**Action**: Maya is told: "Before you take any patient calls or work the front desk, you need to complete the first three CCA Bronze modules. It takes about 20 minutes. You can do it here or on your phone." Maya opens Module 1: "The Story of Caring with Charisma." It's a 4-minute video — Grant again, this time telling the origin story: why a dental DSO decided to model itself after the Ritz Carlton, what it felt like to walk into a dental office and feel unwelcome, what could change if that experience were designed rather than accidental.

**System Response**: The platform tracks Maya's module progress in real-time. After Module 1, a scenario appears: "A patient calls and says 'I hate the dentist.' What do you say?" Three response options appear. Maya selects the correct response. The platform plays a short audio clip: Grant's voice saying "That's exactly right — and here's why it works." The system marks Module 1 complete and logs it in Maya's CCA profile.

**User Reaction**: Maya is surprised. "This doesn't feel like a training video." She finds herself watching Grant's story and leaning in. By the end of Module 3, she understands the trust transfer protocol conceptually and has practiced the warm greeting phrase. She texts her sister: "My new job is weird. In a good way. I had to watch videos about hospitality before I could answer phones."

**Critical Detail**: Grant's authentic on-camera presence — not a produced corporate training voice but a real person with a real philosophy — makes Module 1 feel like being let in on something, not being instructed on something. The emotional hook precedes the behavioral instruction.

## SCENE 4: The Huddle With Live Data — Culture Meets Accountability

**Who**: Practice team (now including Maya, 3 weeks in)
**Where**: Break room, 7:55 AM, Thursday
**Moment**: A Thursday huddle — no Grant video today, just the automated daily content segment

**Action**: Maya is now part of the huddle. This week's theme is "Trust Transfer — How the Whole Team Builds One Experience." The screen shows today's agenda: a 60-second recap of the trust transfer protocol steps, then "Yesterday's Score" — the H-Score moved from 54 to 67 in three weeks. The screen shows the trend line. Below it: a single Swell review from yesterday where a patient wrote "Everyone I talked to seemed to know my whole story. I felt like they'd briefed each other about me." The team recognizes this as a trust transfer working.

**System Response**: The platform flags this review with a "Trust Transfer Signal Detected" badge — its sentiment analysis identified language patterns consistent with successful handoff execution. The review is queued to appear in the Champion's weekly network submission ("wow story" candidates). The huddle's "Today's Focus" segment prompts: "Today, each person name-drops the next person in the handoff: 'Dr. Chen is going to come in and ask you about...' Practice that transition phrase now."

**User Reaction**: The hygienist high-fives Maya. "That's literally the thing we practiced on Monday — and that patient noticed." Dr. Vasquez watches the H-Score trend line and makes a mental note: 13 points in 3 weeks. He opens the regional leaderboard tab and sees his practice has moved from 78th to 61st in the Southwest region.

**Critical Detail**: The platform connecting yesterday's patient language ("I felt like they'd briefed each other about me") to the specific protocol practiced 3 days ago closes the feedback loop in a way that no manager could engineer manually. Behavior to outcome in under 72 hours.

## SCENE 5: Maya Earns Her Bronze — The Credential Moment

**Who**: Maya
**Where**: Practice break room + her phone, 6 weeks after start
**Moment**: Maya completes her final CCA Bronze module and scenario assessment

**Action**: Maya finishes Module 10 ("The Exit Experience — Closing the Circle") on her phone during a 15-minute break. The final scenario presents: "A patient is leaving and hasn't said much. They seem neutral. What's the closing move?" Maya selects the response: use their name, confirm the next appointment, give them one warm personal observation. The assessment marks her Bronze certified.

**System Response**: The platform generates Maya's CCA Bronze certificate — her name, the certification date, the SGA "Caring with Charisma Academy" seal. A notification goes to Dr. Vasquez: "Maya has earned her CCA Bronze certification. Celebrate this at your next huddle." The certificate is sent to Maya's email with instructions for adding it to LinkedIn. Her certification status appears in the practice's staff dashboard as a green badge. The practice's certification rate moves from 60% to 73%.

**User Reaction**: Maya screenshots the certificate and sends it to her family. "I got certified." She adds "CCA Bronze — Caring with Charisma Academy" to her LinkedIn. She tells the office manager she wants to start working toward Silver. She asks: "What do I need to do to get the jacket someday?"

**Critical Detail**: The LinkedIn integration and the physical aspiration of the jacket transform completion from an administrative act into a personal milestone. The practice owner's huddle notification ensures it is publicly celebrated — not silently logged.

## SCENE 6: The Champion Emerges — Peer Leadership Activates

**Who**: Dr. Vasquez + Maya + SGA Platform
**Where**: Dr. Vasquez's office, 8 weeks after launch
**Moment**: The platform prompts Dr. Vasquez to designate a Caring with Charisma Champion

**Action**: Dr. Vasquez receives a platform notification: "Your practice is ready to designate a C&C Champion. Based on huddle participation, certification progress, and peer signals, we recommend Maya. Champions lead the daily huddle, onboard new staff, and represent your practice in the regional Champion network." Dr. Vasquez clicks "Designate Maya as Champion" and sends her a message.

**System Response**: The platform activates Maya's Champion profile. She receives: access to the Champion content library (additional Grant content, facilitation guides, "wow story" submission form), an invitation to the next Regional Champion Call (15 practices in the Southwest cohort, facilitated by SGA), and a Champion-specific certification track that eventually leads to CCA Gold. Her Champion status appears on the practice's public-facing profile page (visible to job applicants and partner organizations).

**User Reaction**: Maya reads the Champion invitation and feels genuinely moved. "I've been here 8 weeks." She calls her mom. She starts leading the next morning's huddle herself — not reading from a script but building on what the platform provides. She submits the patient trust transfer story from Scene 4 as a "wow story" for the regional network.

**Critical Detail**: The platform's Champion recommendation — based on actual engagement data, not just the practice owner's personal preference — identifies the right person objectively and gives the practice owner a data-informed prompt rather than leaving the decision entirely to discretion.

## SCENE 7: Jordan's Network View — Accountability at Scale

**Who**: Jordan (SGA Network Operations Director)
**Where**: Home office, Friday 8:00 AM
**Moment**: Weekly network review

**Action**: Jordan opens the Network Intelligence Dashboard. She sees her 80-practice portfolio organized by H-Score. The color-coded heatmap shows: 18 practices are "Exceptional" (75+), 41 are "Established" (60-74), 16 are "Developing" (40-59), 5 are "Emerging" (<40). She clicks into one of the 5 "Emerging" practices — a rural practice in New Mexico with a 38 H-Score and 0% huddle completion this week.

**System Response**: The platform surfaces an automated intervention alert: "This practice has missed 4 consecutive huddles and has not designated a Champion. CCA Bronze certification rate is 25%. Recommended action: Regional Director outreach + Huddle Health Check. Would you like to schedule a call?" Jordan clicks "Schedule." The platform pre-drafts a message to the practice owner with this week's H-Score context and a proposed 20-minute check-in.

**User Reaction**: Jordan appreciates that the platform tells her who needs help rather than requiring her to read 80 separate reports. She schedules 3 intervention calls. She also sends a Slack note to SGA leadership: "Southwest region hit 62 average H-Score this week — up from 48 last month. Dr. Vasquez's practice is a standout — Maya is running the best huddles in the region."

**Critical Detail**: The intervention trigger is automatic and prescriptive — Jordan doesn't have to decide who needs help, the platform identifies them and proposes the action. Her job shifts from detective to coach.

## SCENE 8: The Network Leaderboard — Healthy Competition

**Who**: Dr. Vasquez + Practice team
**Where**: Break room, Monday morning huddle, 12 weeks after launch
**Moment**: The huddle includes the monthly network leaderboard segment

**Action**: This week's huddle is special — it's the first of the month, and the platform delivers the monthly "Network Highlights" segment. The screen shows: Top 5 practices in the Southwest region, ranked by H-Score. Dr. Vasquez's practice is #3. The platform reads aloud a "wow story" submitted by Maya two weeks ago — the trust transfer patient experience — which was selected as the Southwest "Story of the Month" and is being shared across all 80 practices in the region this week.

**System Response**: Maya's submitted story is part of this week's huddle content for every practice in the Southwest. Practices see the story, the practice name, and Maya's first name as the submitted Champion. The platform's Rewards Engine records that Dr. Vasquez's practice has been in "Exceptional" status for 4 consecutive weeks — triggering the bonus pool contribution notification. Each staff member receives a push notification: "Your practice has earned a $200 bonus pool contribution this month."

**User Reaction**: The team erupts when the bonus pool notification appears. Maya blushes when the team realizes her story is being shared network-wide. Dr. Vasquez takes a photo of the leaderboard rank and sends it to his family group chat. Two staff members ask about CCA Silver requirements.

**Critical Detail**: The bonus pool notification arriving at the moment of recognition — during the huddle, publicly, while the team is together — amplifies the emotional impact exponentially compared to a private payroll adjustment.

## SCENE 9: SGA Leadership — The North Star Data

**Who**: SGA Executive Team + Jordan
**Where**: Quarterly business review, board room
**Moment**: Six months after platform launch across the first 50 pilot practices

**Action**: Jordan presents the six-month Network Intelligence Report. The platform has generated: average H-Score across pilot practices (up from 51 to 69), huddle completion rate (up from ~12% to 78%), CCA Bronze certification rate (up from 0% to 84%), 5-star review velocity (up 34% network-wide compared to the same period last year), and a statistically significant correlation: practices with H-Scores above 70 generate 2.8x more 5-star reviews than practices below 50.

**System Response**: The platform has auto-generated a "Network Impact Report" PDF — formatted for executive presentation — that includes the correlation analysis, practice-level case studies (Dr. Vasquez's practice highlighted), and a projection of what H-Score improvement would mean for patient acquisition across the full 260-practice network. The report is exportable and shareable. A section is pre-built for academic publication: the data structure, methodology, and correlation findings formatted for a dental journal submission.

**User Reaction**: The CEO says: "This is the first time we've been able to show that culture investment produces measurable revenue outcomes." A board member asks: "Can we share this with the dental schools we've been in conversation with?" Jordan nods. The academic publication pathway — always the north star — is now supported by real data from 50 practices over 6 months.

**Critical Detail**: The platform's auto-generated executive report transforms what would have been a multi-week manual analysis into a real-time, board-ready artifact. The academic publication formatting — built into the report from the beginning — keeps the dental school adoption pathway alive without requiring additional work.
