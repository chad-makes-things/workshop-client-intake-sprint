# Goal & Sprint Questions

**Sprint:** Studio Automation at Scale
**Agent:** Problem Mapper
**Date:** 2026-03-10

---

## Long-Term Goal

In 18 months, every SGA-affiliated practice — across all 160+ locations spanning SGA legacy, Gen4, and MODIS — can request, receive, and deploy on-brand creative assets in under 48 hours through an AI-agent-powered studio system, without a single email to a human designer for templated work, while the studio team shifts from reactive order-takers to proactive brand stewards who set standards and drive digital adoption.

**Ambition test:** This goal is ambitious because it requires fundamentally reengineering how 160 franchise-style locations interact with a central creative function — replacing ad hoc communication with a structured workflow, replacing manual design with AI generation, and shifting the cultural default from print to digital.

**Specificity test:** The goal passes: 18-month timeline, 160+ locations, 48-hour SLA, templated work off human designer's plate, studio role shifts from reactive to proactive.

---

## Sprint Questions

Sprint questions are the mechanisms we must get right. If any of these break, the goal fails.

| # | Sprint Question | Risk if We Get It Wrong |
|---|-----------------|------------------------|
| SQ1 | Can a practice manager with zero design training successfully submit a complete, usable creative brief through a chatbot — and get back an on-brand asset without calling a human? | If no, the bottleneck stays with the studio team. Scale is impossible. |
| SQ2 | Can AI-generated designs in Canva (or equivalent) consistently meet SGA brand standards, with only a single human quality gate standing between generation and delivery? | If no, the human review load doesn't shrink — it might grow. The whole model breaks. |
| SQ3 | Can we reduce discretionary print volume by 30-40% by building a digital alternatives challenge directly into the intake flow — before a practice commits to a print order? | If no, we automate a costly, low-ROI behavior and miss the bigger opportunity to shift the medium entirely. |
| SQ4 | Can we create a viable centralized digital asset management (DAM) system — seeded with brand foundation packages for underserved practices — that prevents design requests from failing because assets don't exist? | If no, the AI generator has nothing to work with for 30-40% of practices, and asset creation becomes a pre-bottleneck to the bottleneck. |
| SQ5 | Can we consolidate print procurement to a single curated vendor channel that practices can order from directly — without routing through the studio — with the studio only setting up and auditing, not touching each order? | If no, print vendor pain (Staples, local printers, wrong formats, delays) continues. Every approved design still requires a human to touch the order. |

---

## Passing Criteria for the Goal

By month 18:
- [ ] 80%+ of templated creative requests (business cards, appointment reminders, flyers, social posts) fulfilled in ≤48 hours without designer involvement
- [ ] Studio team has shifted from reactive production to proactive brand governance + DAM curation
- [ ] At least 30% reduction in print order volume vs. baseline, replaced by digital equivalents (patient email, in-office TV screens, digital frames)
- [ ] 100% of active practices have a minimum brand asset package (logo in correct formats, brand colors, at least one photo set)
- [ ] Single approved print vendor channel active with practices ordering directly
