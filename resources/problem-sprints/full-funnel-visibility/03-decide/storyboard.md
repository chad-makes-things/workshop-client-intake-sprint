# Storyboard: The Funnel OS

## Solution Selected
**The Funnel OS** — Full-Stack Unified Platform with staged delivery. A purpose-built patient funnel operating system for SGA, with three native layers: Data Integration, Executive Dashboard, and Practice Nerve Center. Stage 1 uses an off-the-shelf BI tool for quick executive visibility while the full platform is built.

---

## Storyboard (10 Steps)

### Step 1: The Monday Morning — CEO Opens the Funnel Dashboard

**Scene**: It's 7:15 AM Monday. SGA's CEO opens the Funnel OS executive dashboard on their laptop before heading into the office. The dashboard loads the network-wide funnel view — a single screen showing the health of all 260+ practices.

**Action**: The CEO sees five horizontal bars representing the funnel stages (Leads Generated → Connected → Booked → Showed → Produced). Each bar shows network-wide aggregates with color coding: green (on track), yellow (watch), red (intervention needed). A sidebar shows "Top 5 Alerts" — AI-generated, ranked by revenue impact.

**System Response**: The dashboard pulls from the data integration layer, which has been ingesting data overnight from PMS systems (production, appointments), phone platforms (answer rates, call volume), and lead sources (form submissions, ad performance). The AI analysis layer has processed the overnight data and generated alerts for practices deviating from their baselines.

**User Reaction**: The CEO immediately sees that the Southeast region's phone answer rate has dropped 12% week-over-week. They click the alert to drill down.

**Critical Detail**: The dashboard must load in under 3 seconds, show data no more than 4 hours old, and the drill-down from network → region → practice must be 2 clicks, not 5. The CEO doesn't have time to navigate — the system must surface the important information, not bury it.

---

### Step 2: The Drill-Down — From Alert to Root Cause

**Scene**: The CEO clicks the phone answer rate alert. The view zooms from network-level to the Southeast region, showing a heatmap of all practices in that region with their phone answer rates.

**Action**: Three practices are highlighted in red. The CEO clicks one — "Towne Lake Dental, Woodstock GA." The practice-level funnel view opens, showing all 5 stages with week-over-week trends. Phone answer rate is at 62% (baseline: 84%). The AI has annotated the data: "Staffing change detected — front desk headcount dropped from 3 to 2 on March 3rd. Phone volume unchanged. Recommended: activate AI phone agent overflow for missed calls."

**System Response**: The integration layer has correlated phone system data (answer rates, call volume, time-of-day patterns) with HR data from iSolved (staffing change event). The AI layer identified the staffing change as the probable cause and generated a specific, actionable recommendation.

**User Reaction**: The CEO forwards the alert to the VP of Operations with a note: "Can we activate Sierra overflow at Towne Lake this week?" The issue was identified in 2 minutes instead of discovered 30 days later in a monthly report.

**Critical Detail**: The AI must explain WHY, not just WHAT. "Answer rate dropped" is useless. "Answer rate dropped because you lost a front desk person" is actionable. The system must correlate across data sources (phone + HR + scheduling) to generate causal explanations, not just flag metrics.

---

### Step 3: The HQ Ops Team Morning Standup

**Scene**: 9:00 AM. The HQ operations team opens the Funnel OS in their "Ops View" — a queue-based interface showing practices that need attention, ranked by urgency and revenue impact.

**Action**: The team lead scans the queue. 14 practices have flagged items. The queue shows: 3 phone-related (answer rates), 4 show-rate-related (appointments dropping off), 2 production-related (behind monthly targets), 5 lead-related (ad performance dipping). Each item has an AI-generated summary, recommended action, and estimated revenue impact if unaddressed.

**System Response**: The ops queue is generated by the AI analysis layer, which runs continuously. It ingests data from all 260+ practices, compares each practice to its own baseline (not a network average — each practice is unique), identifies deviations that cross configurable thresholds, and ranks them by estimated revenue impact. The queue refreshes every 15 minutes.

**User Reaction**: The team lead assigns items to team members. "Sarah, take the 3 phone issues — see if we need to activate AI agents. Mike, look at the show rate drops — check if confirmation sequences are running." Each assignment is logged in the system.

**Critical Detail**: The ops queue must be opinionated — ranked, not just listed. The team has 8 hours, not 80. If 14 items are flagged, the top 3 must be clearly the top 3. Revenue impact estimation is the ranking function. Also: the queue must show what's ALREADY been addressed (by practice staff or automation) so HQ doesn't duplicate effort.

---

### Step 4: The Practice Nerve Center — Office Manager's 20-Minute Agenda

**Scene**: 7:45 AM at Garner Dental Group in Mississippi. The office manager, Jennifer, opens the Practice Nerve Center on the front desk iPad before the first patient arrives. She has 20 minutes before the day starts.

**Action**: The Nerve Center shows a clean, simple screen: "Good morning, Jennifer. Here are your 3 priorities for today." Each priority is a card with a specific action, context, and estimated impact:
1. "📞 Call back 4 missed calls from yesterday (est. 2 new patients, $1,200 revenue)" — with the patient names and phone numbers ready to tap.
2. "✅ Confirm 3 at-risk appointments for tomorrow (show rate prediction: 55% without confirmation)" — with one-tap confirm/text buttons.
3. "📋 2 patients from last week have unscheduled treatment plans worth $8,400 — suggested follow-up scripts attached."

**System Response**: The Nerve Center pulled from the phone system (missed calls), PMS (unconfirmed appointments + unscheduled treatment), and the AI layer (show rate prediction based on historical patterns for similar appointments). It ranked the actions by estimated revenue impact and generated the specific follow-up scripts based on the treatment plan type.

**User Reaction**: Jennifer taps the first card, calls the 4 missed patients in 8 minutes (AI pre-dialed each one). Two book appointments. She texts the 3 at-risk appointments in 2 minutes using the one-tap button. She saves the treatment plan follow-ups for her lunch break. Total: 12 minutes, $3,600+ in pipeline created.

**Critical Detail**: The Nerve Center must be zero-learning-curve. No training manual. No "navigate to the calls section." It's 3 cards, each with one action, each tappable. The phone numbers are pre-loaded. The text templates are pre-written. Jennifer's job is to EXECUTE the agenda, not figure out what the agenda should be. If this takes more than 20 minutes, it failed.

---

### Step 5: AI Phone Agent Handoff — Sierra Catches a Missed Call

**Scene**: 11:30 AM at a practice in Arizona. The front desk is with patients. A new patient calls. The phone rings 4 times, then Sierra (AI phone agent) picks up.

**Action**: Sierra answers: "Thanks for calling [Practice Name], this is Sierra. How can I help you today?" The patient asks about teeth whitening. Sierra checks the PMS schedule, finds an opening next Tuesday at 2 PM, and books the appointment. Sierra sends a confirmation text to the patient and logs the interaction.

**System Response**: The Funnel OS records this as a complete funnel event: Lead Generated (inbound call) → Connected (Sierra answered, ring 4) → Booked (appointment Tuesday 2 PM). The event flows into the data integration layer. The executive dashboard updates: this practice's "AI-assisted bookings" count increments. The AI analysis layer notes that 35% of this practice's bookings this week were AI-assisted (baseline: 20%) — flagging that the practice may be understaffed on phones.

**User Reaction**: The office manager sees the Sierra booking in her Nerve Center at end of day as a completed item (no action needed — Sierra handled it). The HQ ops team sees the AI-assistance trend in the ops queue as an informational flag.

**Critical Detail**: Sierra's actions must flow into the SAME funnel view as human actions. The system doesn't care if a human or AI answered the phone — it tracks the funnel stage transition. But it DOES track who handled it (human vs. AI) so HQ can see when AI is compensating for capacity gaps.

---

### Step 6: Show Rate Intervention — Proactive Recovery

**Scene**: 2:00 PM Tuesday. The AI analysis layer detects that a practice in Michigan has 6 appointments for Wednesday morning, but only 2 have been confirmed. Historical pattern: unconfirmed appointments at this practice have a 40% show rate.

**Action**: The AI generates an intervention: "4 unconfirmed appointments tomorrow AM at Troy Family Dental. Expected: 1.6 of 4 unconfirmed will no-show. Recommended: automated confirmation texts now + phone call from front desk for the 2 highest-value appointments." The intervention appears in both the practice's Nerve Center and the HQ ops queue.

**System Response**: The automation layer sends confirmation texts to all 4 patients immediately. The 2 highest-value appointments (an implant consult at $4,500 and a full exam at $350) are flagged for personal phone calls by front desk staff. The Nerve Center shows: "2 calls to make before end of day — scripts ready."

**User Reaction**: The front desk makes both calls. The implant consult confirms. The full exam reschedules to Friday. The 2 text confirmations get responses — 1 confirms, 1 says they need to reschedule. Wednesday morning: 4 out of 6 appointments show up instead of the predicted 3.6. One additional slot is filled by a same-day booking from the waitlist.

**Critical Detail**: The AI must know service type matters. An unconfirmed routine cleaning is different from an unconfirmed implant consult — different value, different no-show risk, different intervention strategy. The system applies different confirmation cadences and escalation rules based on appointment type and value.

---

### Step 7: Weekly Executive Review — The Friday Report

**Scene**: Friday 4:00 PM. The CEO opens the executive dashboard for the weekly review. Instead of waiting for a 30-page PDF that arrives 30 days late, they see a live "Week in Review" generated by the AI.

**Action**: The AI summary shows: "This week across the network: 4,200 new leads generated (↑3% vs. last week), 3,150 connected (75% connection rate), 2,520 booked (80% booking rate), 2,142 showed (85% show rate), $3.2M production (↑1.5% vs. last week). 3 practices flagged for intervention. 2 interventions completed this week with positive outcomes. 1 emerging trend: Southeast region phone answer rates declining — recommend regional staffing review."

**System Response**: The weekly summary is auto-generated from the data lake. Every number is clickable — drill down to region, practice, day, individual funnel step. Trend arrows show week-over-week changes. The "interventions completed" section links to the specific actions taken and their measured outcomes (did the intervention actually improve the metric?).

**User Reaction**: The CEO shares the summary with the leadership team. For the first time, the entire team is looking at the same, current data. The regional VP for the Southeast follows up on the phone staffing issue. Discussion takes 15 minutes instead of 2 hours of arguing about whose spreadsheet is right.

**Critical Detail**: The weekly summary must be auto-generated, not manually assembled. Zero human effort to produce it. And it must show OUTCOMES of interventions, not just problems — this is how the organization builds confidence that the system works and interventions matter.

---

### Step 8: The Practice Nerve Center — End-of-Day Scorecard

**Scene**: 5:30 PM at Garner Dental Group. Jennifer (office manager) checks the Nerve Center before closing for the day. The screen shows her daily scorecard.

**Action**: The scorecard shows: "Today: 12 calls answered (92% answer rate ✅), 4 new patients booked (↑1 vs. daily avg ✅), 1 no-show (managed — rescheduled ✅), $18,400 production (102% of daily target ✅). Your 3 priorities: 2 of 3 completed. The unscheduled treatment plans are queued for tomorrow."

**System Response**: The scorecard is calculated from the same funnel data that feeds the executive dashboard. Jennifer sees her practice's health in the same language the CEO sees the network's health. The incomplete priority (treatment plan follow-ups) automatically rolls to tomorrow's agenda.

**User Reaction**: Jennifer feels good — she can see the impact of her 20-minute morning session. The 2 patients she called back this morning booked and are showing up next week. She closes the Nerve Center and goes home. Tomorrow, the agenda will be fresh and prioritized again.

**Critical Detail**: The end-of-day scorecard is the feedback loop that makes the 20-minute morning session feel worthwhile. If Jennifer does the work but never sees the result, she'll stop doing it. The scorecard must connect actions to outcomes: "You called back 4 patients this morning → 2 booked → estimated $1,200 in revenue." This is how you build habit.

---

### Step 9: AI Learning Loop — The System Gets Smarter

**Scene**: Sunday night. No humans are working. The AI analysis layer runs its weekly learning cycle.

**Action**: The system reviews all interventions from the past week across 260+ practices. It measures: which interventions were executed? Which were ignored? Of the executed ones, which actually improved the target metric? It updates its models: "Confirmation texts sent 24 hours before appointment improve show rates by 8%. Confirmation texts sent 4 hours before improve show rates by 12%. Phone calls from front desk for appointments >$500 improve show rates by 18%."

**System Response**: The AI updates its recommendation engine. Next week's interventions will prioritize phone calls for high-value appointments (because the data shows 18% improvement) over texts for low-value ones (8% improvement). Practices where interventions were ignored get flagged for HQ follow-up — not punitively, but diagnostically: "Is the Nerve Center not surfacing actions well enough? Is the practice overwhelmed?"

**User Reaction**: Nobody sees this happen. But next week, the recommendations are slightly better, the priorities slightly more accurate, the estimated revenue impacts slightly more trustworthy. Over months, the AI becomes the institutional knowledge that no single person at SGA possesses.

**Critical Detail**: The learning loop must be transparent. HQ should be able to ask: "Why is the AI recommending phone calls instead of texts for this practice?" and get an answer grounded in data: "Because phone calls at this practice have historically improved show rates by 22%, while texts only improve by 6%." Opaque AI recommendations erode trust. Explainable recommendations build it.

---

### Step 10: Month-End — The Old World vs. The New World

**Scene**: March 31st. End of month. In the old world, this is when the scramble begins — pulling reports from 10 systems, reconciling numbers, building spreadsheets, hoping the data is right. The executive team wouldn't see a complete picture until April 25-30.

**Action**: In the new world, the CEO opens the Funnel OS. The monthly view is already there — it's been building in real-time all month. Total network production: $42.8M. Funnel conversion rates by stage. Practice-by-practice rankings. Trends. Anomalies. Interventions taken and their outcomes. All live. All accurate. All generated without a single human assembling a spreadsheet.

**System Response**: The monthly view is just the weekly view aggregated over 4 weeks. No special "month-end process." No reconciliation. The data has been flowing continuously. The AI has been monitoring continuously. The interventions have been happening continuously. Month-end is just a calendar artifact — the business has been managed in real-time all month.

**User Reaction**: The CEO shares the monthly dashboard with the Thurston Group investor call. For the first time, the investor update is based on real-time data, not 30-day-old approximations. The conversation shifts from "here's what happened last month" to "here's what we're doing about what's happening right now."

**Critical Detail**: The real test of the system is month-end. If the Funnel OS makes month-end reconciliation obsolete — if the real-time data matches the financial close within 2% — the system has proven its value. The goal is not just "faster reporting" but "continuous management." The difference between a rearview mirror and a windshield.
