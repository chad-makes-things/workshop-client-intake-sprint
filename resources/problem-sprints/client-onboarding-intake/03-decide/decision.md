# Decision Record -- Client Onboarding Intake Sprint

## Sprint Target

**Guided Intake and Collection (Steps 2-6):** Replace scattered multi-channel collection with a guided, flexible intake experience that works for all client types.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Each criterion is derived from one of the five sprint questions. Weights reflect risk level: HIGH-risk questions receive a weight of 3, MEDIUM-risk questions receive a weight of 2. This ensures the scoring surface reflects where failure would be most damaging.

| # | Criterion | Source | Risk | Weight |
|---|-----------|--------|------|:------:|
| C1 | **Client Adoption** -- Will clients actually use this instead of texting/emailing ad hoc? | Q1 | HIGH | 3 |
| C2 | **Narrative Quality** -- Will the system produce framework inputs that yield equal or better narratives? | Q2 | HIGH | 3 |
| C3 | **Sophistication Spectrum** -- Does it work for both polished-deck clients and "we just have stories" clients? | Q3 | HIGH | 3 |
| C4 | **Personal Touch** -- Does the experience feel warm and relational, not transactional? | Q4 | MEDIUM | 2 |
| C5 | **Facilitator Trust** -- Will the facilitator adopt and rely on this system? | Q5 | MEDIUM | 2 |

**Maximum possible weighted score:** 65 (5 points x 13 total weight)

## Analysis of Results

### The Kickoff Call scores a perfect 65/65 -- but has a fatal constraint.

Solution 4 is the best answer to every sprint question *in isolation*. Live conversation produces the highest-quality input, adapts to any client, feels the most personal, and the facilitator already trusts the approach because they already do it.

The problem is that The Kickoff Call does not solve the sprint's underlying objective: **scaling intake beyond the facilitator's personal capacity.** A 45-minute call per client means 7.5 hours of live intake for 10 concurrent clients. This solution trades one bottleneck (manual collection) for another (mandatory synchronous time). It is the right answer at 3 clients per month. It is the wrong architecture at 10+.

### The Interview scores 53/65 and has no fatal constraint.

Solution 1 is the strongest scalable solution. It directly addresses client adoption through structure and guidance. It handles the sophistication spectrum through branching paths. It produces framework-ready structured output. And it can serve 50 clients as easily as 5 -- no facilitator time required during intake after the initial video recording.

Its weakness is personal touch (scored 3/5). It is, at its core, a well-designed web form. The facilitator video mitigates this, but the client is ultimately interacting with an interface. This is a real tradeoff, not a minor quibble.

### The Head Start is the strongest complement, not the strongest lead.

Solution 3's "we did our homework" moment is the most impressive single feature in the entire set. Pre-populating 40-60% of the framework through web scraping is powerful. But it has two vulnerabilities: it works poorly for thin-web-presence clients (the sophistication spectrum problem), and review-and-edit engagement depends on client initiative (the adoption problem). As a standalone solution, these gaps are meaningful. As a feature embedded within another solution, it is transformative.

---

## Recommendation

**The Interview (Solution 1) is the recommended direction**, with a strong recommendation to incorporate The Head Start's web-scraping pre-population as a core feature within the interview flow.

The Interview is the only solution that simultaneously (a) changes client behavior through structure rather than hoping they self-direct, (b) scales without adding facilitator labor, and (c) produces framework-mapped outputs by design rather than through post-hoc AI categorization. It scored highest among the scalable solutions across all weighted criteria.

The Kickoff Call outscored it on pure quality, but its inability to scale is disqualifying for a system meant to remove the facilitator bottleneck. If The Kickoff Call could be conducted by AI or a trained team member, it would be the clear winner -- but that is a future capability, not a present one.

The Head Start's web-scraping approach should be integrated into The Interview: before the client begins the guided flow, the system scrapes their web presence and pre-fills answers where possible. The client then reviews pre-populated fields and fills in the rest. This hybrid captures The Head Start's "we did our homework" delight while retaining The Interview's structural guidance and branching logic.
