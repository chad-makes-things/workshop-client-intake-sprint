# Sprint Target — Practice-Level + SGA Brand Media Strategy

## Selected Target

**The content capture gap — at both the practice level AND the SGA brand level.**

We are targeting two parallel problems with one shared infrastructure:

1. **Practice-level**: The moment between "a high-value case is scheduled" and "HQ receives a usable before/after with patient consent" — this is where the patient acquisition content pipeline dies.

2. **SGA brand-level**: The moment between "a partner story, leadership insight, or network milestone happens" and "it becomes a LinkedIn post, a recruitment video, or a thought leadership piece" — this is where the B2B content pipeline dies.

The capture behavior is identical: someone holds a phone, records something, uploads it. The system infrastructure is shared. What changes is the **playbook** — who captures, what they capture, who it's for, and where it goes.

---

## Why This Target

### It's the highest-leverage failure point

The SGA content repurposing matrix can produce 156 monthly pieces from 11 pillar inputs. The social growth strategy is designed. The digital storefront system is built. The channels are defined. **The only thing missing is raw content from practices.** Before/afters are the simplest, highest-value content type — two photos that drive implant and cosmetic conversions better than any other asset.

### It's where the money is

The ~25+ implant/cosmetic practices and ~40 specialty practices represent the highest-value content opportunity. A single compelling before/after on Instagram or YouTube can drive $30K-$80K full-arch cases. Dr. Olson's channel proves this at 138K subscribers. But the other 65+ high-value practices produce almost nothing.

### It solves the human behavior problem

The conversation identified the core issue: "the biggest issue we have around capturing media is that they never provide us those before and afters." This isn't a technology problem — it's a behavior problem. The system needs to:
1. **Know** what cases are coming (PMS integration)
2. **Prompt** the right person at the right time (mobile alerts)
3. **Simplify** the capture (phone, <60 seconds)
4. **Close the loop** (show them the finished product)

### It's scalable from small to large

Start with the 10-15 practices already actively engaged. Prove the system works. Then roll to all 65+ high-value practices. Then adapt the playbook for 160+ GPs. The same infrastructure scales — you just swap the playbook.

### The SGA brand uses the same system with a different playbook

The SGA social growth strategy already defines the content: Lamonte Jensen's thought leadership, Dr. Ellingson's "protect the profession" pieces, partner testimonials, 4Front symposium content, Pikos Institute highlights, MODIS Community Conversations. What's missing is the same thing that's missing at practice level — **a capture system that prompts the right person, simplifies the recording, and automates everything downstream.**

The audience is different (practice owners evaluating partnership, associate dentists evaluating careers, specialist partners) but the behavior is identical: hold phone → say words → the system does the rest.

Brand-level content types:
- **Partner story captures**: New partner signs → prompt for 90-second testimonial at signing event
- **Leadership thought pieces**: Jensen/Ellingson/Wong get weekly topic prompts → record 4 min on phone → system cuts to LinkedIn clips
- **Conference/event content**: 4Front symposium, Pikos courses → event content capture playbook → repurposing pipeline
- **Network milestone content**: 100th practice, new state entry, team achievements → prompt capture → brand posts
- **Clinical innovation showcases**: New technology deployed → capture walkthrough → B2B content for recruitment/acquisition

## Target User: The Content Champion

**Who**: Dental assistant or front desk staff member designated as the practice's "social media champion"

**Context**: Already works at the practice. Has a phone. Is enthusiastic about social media (or at least not resistant). Needs a system that tells them what to do, when to do it, and makes it stupidly simple.

**Primary interaction**: A mobile app on their personal phone that:
- Shows them today's content opportunities (pulled from PMS)
- Guides them through capture (open camera, take photo/video, confirm)
- Uploads automatically
- Shows them when their content goes live

**Secondary user**: The doctor, who needs to be involved minimally — hold phone for 4 minutes, record a topic, hand it back.

**Tertiary user**: HQ marketing team (Dakota's team), who needs a dashboard showing content flowing in from across the network, a review/approve queue, and production pipeline visibility.

## How We'll Know This Sprint Succeeded

1. The system design covers the full pipeline: case identification → alert → capture → upload → production → distribution
2. The mobile capture experience is demonstrably simpler than anything that exists today
3. The architecture integrates with PMS data for proactive alerts
4. There are clear playbooks for at least 2 practice types (implant/cosmetic + specialty)
5. The HTML prototype demonstrates the core user experience for the Content Champion
6. The storyboard traces a real scenario: "Thursday implant case → Tuesday published before/after on Instagram and digital storefront"
